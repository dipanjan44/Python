<p><b>7 Billion Actions</b> is a worldwide campaign established by the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Population_Fund" title="United Nations Population Fund">United Nations Population Fund</a> (UNFPA) in 2011 to commemorate the <a href="/wiki/World_population" title="World population">world population</a> milestone of 7 billion people. UNFPA is a <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> organization that works on population and development issues, with an emphasis on <a href="/wiki/Reproductive_health" title="Reproductive health">reproductive health</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gender_equality" title="Gender equality">gender equality</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> 7 Billion Actions works with multiple corporations, organizations and individuals to address the 7 Key Issues such as <a href="/wiki/Poverty" title="Poverty">poverty</a>, gender equality, <a href="/wiki/Youth" title="Youth">youth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ageing" title="Ageing">ageing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Urbanization" title="Urbanization">urbanization</a>, <a href="/wiki/Natural_environment" title="Natural environment">environment</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Reproductive_health" title="Reproductive health">reproductive health</a> and rights. The UN has declared the World Population surpassing 7 billion on 31 October 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-unfpa_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-unfpa-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Prior to this date, the UN celebrated <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Day_of_Six_Billion" title="The Day of Six Billion">the Day of Six Billion</a> in October 1999.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Day_of_Seven_Billion">Day of Seven Billion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=7_Billion_Actions&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Day of Seven Billion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The <a href="/wiki/Day_of_Seven_Billion" title="Day of Seven Billion">Day of Seven Billion</a>, October 31, 2011, is the day that has been officially designated by the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Population_Fund" title="United Nations Population Fund">United Nations Population Fund</a> (UNFPA) as the approximate day on which the total <a href="/wiki/World_population" title="World population">world population</a> reached a population of seven billion people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-un_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-un-4">[4]</a></sup> United Nations Secretary General <a href="/wiki/Ban_Ki-moon" title="Ban Ki-moon">Ban Ki-moon</a> spoke at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_Nations_Headquarters" title="United Nations Headquarters">United Nations building</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> on this milestone in the size of world population about the issues that it will raise, along with promoting 7 Billion Actions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-actions_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-actions-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Campaign_goals">Campaign goals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=7_Billion_Actions&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Campaign goals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The 7 Billion Actions Campaign aims to raise awareness of key population issues such as reproductive health, poverty, women empowerment, youth, environment sustainability, ageing, and urbanization, by encouraging people to take actions and recommit to humanity.
</p><p>The campaign will count and showcase 7 billion online and offline actions that tell the story of the people behind the number and the connections they share with each other and the issues they each face.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="7_key_issues">7 key issues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=7_Billion_Actions&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 7 key issues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The 7 Billion Actions Campaign centers on highlighting individuals and organizations making significant impact and creating innovative solutions in the 7 key issues identified as most imperative for the growing global population.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p>
<ol><li><b>Poverty and Inequality: Breaking the Cycle</b>
<dl><dd>"Reducing poverty and inequality also slows population growth."</dd></dl></li>
<li><b>Women and Girls: Empowerment and Progress</b>
<dl><dd>"Investing in the power of women and girls will accelerate progress on all fronts."</dd></dl></li>
<li><b>Young People:  Forging the Future</b>
<dl><dd>"Energetic and open to new technologies, history’s largest and most interconnected population of young people is transforming global politics and culture."</dd></dl></li>
<li><b>Reproductive Health and Rights: The Facts of Life</b>
<dl><dd>"Ensuring that every child is wanted and every childbirth safe leads to smaller and stronger families."</dd></dl></li>
<li><b>Environment: Healthy Planet, Healthy People</b>
<dl><dd>"Demands for water, trees, food and fossil fuels will only increase as world population grows to 7 billion and beyond."</dd></dl></li>
<li><b>Ageing: An Unprecedented Challenge</b>
<dl><dd>"Lower fertility and longer life expectancy add up to a new challenge worldwide: ageing populations."</dd></dl></li>
<li><b>Urbanization: Planning for Growth</b>
<dl><dd>"The next two billion people will live in cities, so we need to plan for them now."</dd></dl></li></ol>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Main_campaign_components">Main campaign components</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=7_Billion_Actions&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Main campaign components">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>7BillionActions.org: Showcases stories, interviews, and news. In-depth platform to help individuals connect and take action with efforts taking place near them or across the globe. Interactive data visualizations highlighting challenges and solutions.
</p><p>I Count: An online and SMS/mobile effort allowing anyone on Earth to step forward to be counted as one of the 7 billion and share what issues matters most to them.
These are but a few of the important resources being created to facilitate connection and collaboration.
</p><p>World Story Book: A community-created collection of photos, text, audio, and video that tell the stories of struggle and success of the people behind the number.
</p><p>7Billion Hashtag (#7Billion): An ongoing social media conversation on Twitter, Facebook, and other major social media platforms (representing each major region) designed to help fuel awareness, engagement, and participation.
</p>
<h2><span id="Corporate.2C_NGO.2C_and_media_partnerships"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Corporate,_NGO,_and_media_partnerships">Corporate, NGO, and media partnerships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=7_Billion_Actions&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Corporate, NGO, and media partnerships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/IBM" title="IBM">IBM</a>: As a way to celebrate their 100th anniversary, IBM is launching its <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Global_Celebration_of_Service&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Global Celebration of Service (page does not exist)">Global Celebration of Service</a>, a worldwide volunteer service initiative which calls for staff to donate their time and energy for specific social causes. 7 Billion Actions will be presented as one of the official programs which staff can support.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> As part of the new partnership, IBM volunteers will contribute their engineering, marketing, communications, social media and analytics expertise to help build the 7 Billion Actions campaign platform.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p><p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CrowdSPRING" title="CrowdSPRING">crowdSPRING</a>: crowdSPRING launched a series of design competitions to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crowd_source" title="Crowd source">crowd source</a> the official logo for the 7 Billion Actions Campaign. The first round of the competition drew over one thousand submissions and the winning design will become the 7 Billion Action Campaign's official global logo. The next phase will open voting to the public in order to select a group of regionally themed logos.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p><p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Geographic_(magazine)" title="National Geographic (magazine)">National Geographic</a>: This partnership includes special feature in each 2011 issue, traveling photography exhibit, videos, the Happy 7 Billionth Baby project, and other related initiatives.
</p><p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blog_Talk_Radio" title="Blog Talk Radio">Blog Talk Radio</a>: Using their telephone-to-internet platform, the 7 Billion Actions Campaign will launch the Conversations for a World at 7 Billion channel to showcase a variety of audio programs discussing the topics and themes facing the 7 billion. People in different countries and regions will have the opportunity listen to and call into these programs and share their stories and actions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Playing_for_Change" title="Playing for Change">Playing for Change</a>: The non-profit group the Playing for Change Foundation will partner with 7 Billion Actions to produce an official campaign song.
</p>
