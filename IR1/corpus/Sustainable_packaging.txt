<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Sustainable packaging</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Molding_packaging_from_straw,_k9837-1.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="418" data-file-width="640" height="163" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/27/Molding_packaging_from_straw%2C_k9837-1.jpg/250px-Molding_packaging_from_straw%2C_k9837-1.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/27/Molding_packaging_from_straw%2C_k9837-1.jpg/375px-Molding_packaging_from_straw%2C_k9837-1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/27/Molding_packaging_from_straw%2C_k9837-1.jpg/500px-Molding_packaging_from_straw%2C_k9837-1.jpg 2x" width="250"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Molding_packaging_from_straw,_k9837-1.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Molded_pulp" title="Molded pulp">Molded pulp</a> uses recycled <a href="/wiki/Newsprint" title="Newsprint">newsprint</a> to form package components.  Here, researchers are molding packaging from <a href="/wiki/Straw" title="Straw">straw</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></div></div></div>
<p><b>Sustainable packaging</b> is the development and use of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Packaging" title="Packaging">packaging</a> which results in improved <a href="/wiki/Sustainability" title="Sustainability">sustainability</a>. This involves increased use of life cycle inventory (LCI) and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Life_cycle_assessment" title="Life cycle assessment">life cycle assessment</a> (LCA)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> to help guide the use of packaging which reduces the <a href="/wiki/Environmental_degradation" title="Environmental degradation">environmental impact</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ecological_footprint" title="Ecological footprint">ecological footprint</a>.  It includes a look at the whole of the supply chain: from basic function, to marketing, and then through to end of life (LCA) and rebirth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jedlicka_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Jedlicka-4">[4]</a></sup> Additionally, an eco-cost to value ratio can be useful<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
The goals are to improve the long term viability and quality of life for <a href="/wiki/Human" title="Human">humans</a> and the longevity of natural ecosystems. Sustainable packaging must meet the functional and economic needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Sustainability is not necessarily an end state but is a continuing process of improvement.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p>Sustainable packaging is a relatively new addition to the environmental considerations for packaging (see <a href="/wiki/Packaging_and_labeling" title="Packaging and labeling">Packaging and labeling</a>).  It requires more analysis and documentation to look at the package design, choice of materials, processing, and life-cycle.  This is not just the vague "green movement" that many businesses and companies have been trying to include over the past years.   Companies implementing these eco-friendly actions are reducing their <a href="/wiki/Carbon_footprint" title="Carbon footprint">carbon footprint</a>, using more recycled materials and  reusing more package components.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>  They often encourage suppliers, contract packagers, and distributors to do likewise.
</p><p>For example, researchers at the <a href="/wiki/Agricultural_Research_Service" title="Agricultural Research Service">Agricultural Research Service</a> are looking into using dairy-based films as an alternative to petroleum-based packaging. <a class="external autonumber" href="http://www.ars.usda.gov/is/pr/2010/100120.htm" rel="nofollow">[1]</a>  Instead of being made of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Synthetic_polymers" title="Synthetic polymers">synthetic polymers</a>, these dairy-based films would be composed of proteins such as <a href="/wiki/Casein" title="Casein">casein</a> and <a href="/wiki/Whey" title="Whey">whey</a>, which are found in milk.  The films would be <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Biodegradable" title="Biodegradable">biodegradable</a> and offer better oxygen barriers than synthetic, chemical-based films.  More research must be done to improve the water barrier quality of the dairy-based film, but advances in sustainable packaging are actively being pursued.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p><p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Environmental_marketing" title="Environmental marketing">Environmental marketing</a> claims on packages need to be made (and read) with caution. Ambiguous <a href="/wiki/Greenwashing" title="Greenwashing">greenwashing</a> titles such as <i>green packaging</i> and  <i>environmentally friendly</i> can be confusing without specific definition. Some regulators, such as the US Federal Trade Commission, are providing guidance to packagers<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p><p>Companies have long been reusing and recycling packaging when economically viable. Using minimal packaging has also been a common goal to help reduce costs. Recent years have accelerated these efforts based on social movements, consumer pressure, and regulation. All phases of packaging, distribution, and logistics are included.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p><p>Sustainable packaging is no longer focused on just recycling. Just as packaging is not the only eco target, although it is still top of mind for many. Right or wrong, packaging is frequently scrutinized and used as the measure of a company's overall sustainability, even though it may contribute only a small percentage to the total eco impact compared to other things, such as transportation, and water and energy use.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Criteria">Criteria</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainable_packaging&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Criteria">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The criteria for ranking and comparing packaging based on their sustainability is an active area of development.  General guidance, metrics, checklists, and scorecards are being published by several groups.
</p><p>Government,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Standards_organization" title="Standards organization">standards organizations</a>, consumers, retailers,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> and packagers are considering several types of criteria.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup>
</p><p>Each organization words the goals and targets a little differently. In general, the broad goals of sustainable packaging are:
</p>
<ol><li>Functional<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> – product protection, safety, regulatory compliance, etc.</li>
<li>Cost effective – if it is too expensive, it is unlikely to be used</li>
<li>Support long-term human and ecological health</li></ol>
<p>Specific factors for <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_design" title="Sustainable design">sustainable design</a> of packaging may include:
</p>
<ul><li>Use of minimal materials –  reduced packaging, reduced layers of packaging, lower mass (product to packaging ratio), lower volume, etc.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Logistics" title="Logistics">Logistics</a> efficiency (through complete life cycle) – cube utilization, tare weight, enablement of efficient transportation, etc.,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Energy" title="Energy">Energy</a> efficiency, total energy content and usage, use of <a href="/wiki/Renewable_energy" title="Renewable energy">renewable energy</a>, etc.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Recycled" title="Recycled">Recycled</a> content – as available and functional.  For <a href="/wiki/Food_contact_materials" title="Food contact materials">food contact materials</a>, there are special safety considerations, particularly for use of recycled plastics and paper.  Regulations are published by each country or region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></li>
<li>Recyclability – recovery value, use of materials which are frequently and easily <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Recycle" title="Recycle">recycled</a>, reduction of materials which hinder recyclability of major components, etc.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Reusable_packaging" title="Reusable packaging">Reusable packaging</a> – repeated <a href="/wiki/Reuse" title="Reuse">reuse</a> of package, reuse for other purposes, etc.</li>
<li>Use of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Renewable_resources" title="Renewable resources">renewable resources</a> in packaging</li>
<li>Use of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Biodegradable" title="Biodegradable">biodegradable</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Compostable" title="Compostable">compostable</a> materials – when appropriate and do not cause contamination of the recycling stream<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></li>
<li>Avoid the use of materials toxic to humans or the environment</li>
<li>Effects on atmosphere/climate – <a href="/wiki/Ozone_layer" title="Ozone layer">ozone layer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gas" title="Greenhouse gas">greenhouse gases</a> (<a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide" title="Carbon dioxide">carbon dioxide</a> and <a href="/wiki/Methane" title="Methane">methane</a>), <a href="/wiki/Volatile_organic_compound" title="Volatile organic compound">volatile organic compounds</a>, etc.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Water" title="Water">Water</a> use, reuse, treatment, waste, etc.</li>
<li>Worker impact: occupational health, safety, clean technology, etc.</li>
<li>Made from materials healthy throughout the life cycle</li>
<li>Physically designed to optimize materials and energy</li>
<li>Use integral design to reduce the use of materials</li>
<li>Follow the requirements of the <a href="/wiki/2015_United_Nations_Climate_Change_Conference" title="2015 United Nations Climate Change Conference">2015 United Nations Climate Change Conference</a></li></ul>
<p>The chosen criteria are often used best as a basis of comparison for two or more similar packaging designs; not as an absolute success or failure.  Such a multi-variable comparison is often presented as a <a href="/wiki/Radar_chart" title="Radar chart">radar chart</a> (spider chart,  star chart, etc.).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Benefits">Benefits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainable_packaging&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Benefits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Some aspects of environmentally sound packaging are required by regulators while others are decisions made by each packager.  Investors, employees, management, and customers can influence corporate decisions and help set policies.  When investors seek to purchase stock, companies known for their positive environmental policy can be attractive.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup>  Potential stockholders and investors see this as a solid decision: lower environmental risks lead to more capital at cheaper rates. Companies that highlight their environmental status to consumers, can boost sales as well as product reputation. Going green  is often a sound investment that can pay off.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Costs">Costs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainable_packaging&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Costs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The process of engineering more environmentally acceptable packages can include consideration of the costs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup>  Some companies claim that their environmental packaging program is cost effective.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> Some alternative materials that are recycled/recyclable and/or less damaging to the environment can lead to companies incurring increased costs. Though this is common when any product begins to carry the true cost of its production (producer pays, producer responsibility laws, take-back laws). There may be an expensive and lengthy process before the new forms of packaging are deemed safe to the public, and approval may take up to two years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> It is important to note here, that for most of the developed world, tightening legislation, and changes in major retailer demand (Walmart's Sustainable Packaging Scorecard for example) the question is no longer "if" products and packaging should become more sustainable, but how-to and how-soon to do it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jedlicka_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Jedlicka-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Criticism">Criticism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainable_packaging&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Criticism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Efforts toward “greener” packaging are supported in the sustainability community, however, these are often viewed only as incremental steps and not as an end.  Some picture a true sustainable <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Steady_state_economy" title="Steady state economy">steady state economy</a> that may be very different from today's:  greatly reduced energy usage, minimal <a href="/wiki/Ecological_footprint" title="Ecological footprint">ecological footprint</a>,  fewer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Consumer_packaged_goods" title="Consumer packaged goods">consumer packaged goods</a>,  <a href="/wiki/Local_purchasing" title="Local purchasing">local purchasing</a>   with <a href="/wiki/Short_food_supply_chains" title="Short food supply chains">short food supply chains</a>, little <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Processed_foods" title="Processed foods">processed foods</a>, etc.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup>
Less packaging would be needed in a sustainable  <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carbon_neutral" title="Carbon neutral">carbon neutral</a> economy;  fewer packaging options would exist; simpler packaging forms may be necessary.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup>
</p>
