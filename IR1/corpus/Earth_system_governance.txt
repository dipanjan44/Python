<p><b>Earth system governance</b> is a recently developed <a href="/wiki/Paradigm_shift" title="Paradigm shift">paradigm</a> that builds on earlier notions of <a href="/wiki/Environmental_policy" title="Environmental policy">environmental policy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Environmental_protection" title="Environmental protection">nature conservation</a>, but puts these into the broader context of human-induced transformations of the entire <a href="/wiki/Earth_system_science" title="Earth system science">earth system</a>. It conceptualizes the system of formal and informal rules, rule-making mechanisms and actor-networks at all levels of human society (from local to global) that are set up to steer societies towards preventing, mitigating, and adapting to global and local environmental change and earth system transformation, within the normative context of <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_development" title="Sustainable development">sustainable development</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Introduction">Introduction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Earth_system_governance&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Introduction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The notion of governance refers to forms of steering that are less hierarchical than traditional governmental policy-making (even though most modern governance arrangements will also include some degree of hierarchy), rather decentralized, open to self-organization, and inclusive of non-state actors that range from industry and non-governmental organizations to scientists, indigenous communities, city governments and international organizations.
</p><p>The integrative new paradigm of earth system governance has evolved into an active research area that brings together a variety of social science disciplines including political science, sociology, economics, ecology, policy studies, geography, <a href="/wiki/Sustainability_science" title="Sustainability science">sustainability science</a>, and law.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Conferences">Conferences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Earth_system_governance&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Conferences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Major international conferences on ‘Earth System Governance’ have been held in Amsterdam (2007, 2009), Berlin (2008, 2010), Colorado (2011), Lund (2012, 2017), Tokyo (2013), Norwich (2014), Canberra (2015) and Nairobi (2016). In 2017, the 8th Annual Earth System Governance Conference took place in Lund, Sweden. This conference was co-hosted by Lund University during its 350 year celebration.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> In 2018 it is due to be held in Utrecht, The Netherlands<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Policy_engagement">Policy engagement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Earth_system_governance&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Policy engagement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 16–19 May 2011, more than twenty Nobel Laureates, several leading policy-makers and some of the world’s most renowned thinkers and experts on global sustainability met for the Third Nobel Laureate Symposium on Global Sustainability<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> at the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Swedish_Academy_of_Sciences" title="Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences">Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences</a> in Stockholm. The Nobel Laureate Symposium concluded with the <a href="/wiki/Stockholm_Memorandum" title="Stockholm Memorandum">Stockholm Memorandum</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> calling for "strengthening of Earth System Governance" as a priority for coherent global action.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> This memorandum has been submitted to the High-level Panel on Global Sustainability appointed by the UN Secretary General and fed into the preparations for the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Conference_on_Sustainable_Development" title="United Nations Conference on Sustainable Development">2012 UN Conference on Sustainable Development</a> (Rio+20).
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Earth_system_governance&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The new paradigm of earth system governance was originally developed in the Netherlands by Professor <a class="external text" href="http://www.earthsystemgovernance.org/people/person/frank-biermann" rel="nofollow">Frank Biermann</a> in his inaugural lecture at the VU University Amsterdam, which was published later in 2007<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Based on this pioneering contribution, Biermann was invited by the <a href="/wiki/International_Human_Dimensions_Programme" title="International Human Dimensions Programme">International Human Dimensions Programme on Global Environmental Change</a> to develop a long-term comprehensive international programme in this field, which became in 2009 the global <a href="/wiki/Earth_System_Governance_Project" title="Earth System Governance Project">Earth System Governance Project</a>.
</p><p>Key researchers who have applied the earth system governance framework in their work include <a class="external text" href="http://www.colostate.edu/academics/michele-betsill.aspx" rel="nofollow">Michele Betsill</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Dryzek" title="John Dryzek">John Dryzek</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peter_M._Haas" title="Peter M. Haas">Peter M. Haas</a>, <a class="external text" href="http://unu.edu/faculty/norichika-kanie" rel="nofollow">Norichika Kanie</a>, <a class="external text" href="http://www.lucsus.lu.se/html/lennart_olsson.aspx" rel="nofollow">Lennart Olsson</a>, and <a class="external text" href="http://www.bren.ucsb.edu/people/Faculty/oran_young.htm" rel="nofollow">Oran Young</a>. In 2011, Lund University appointed Biermann as guest professor of Earth System Governance, making him the worldwide first chair holder in this rapidly developing field of research.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Earth_System_Governance_Project">The Earth System Governance Project</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Earth_system_governance&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: The Earth System Governance Project">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Earth_System_Governance_Project" title="Earth System Governance Project">Earth System Governance Project</a></div>
<p>In 2009, the UN-sponsored global change research networks have set up a long-term research programme in earth system governance, the <a href="/wiki/Earth_System_Governance_Project" title="Earth System Governance Project">Earth System Governance Project</a>.
The Earth System Governance Project currently consists of a network of ca. 300 active and about 2,300 indirectly involved scholars from all continents, and is the largest social science research network in the area of governance and global environmental change. The Earth System Governance Project is essentially a scientific effort, but also aims to assist policy responses to the pressing problems of global environmental change.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The <a class="external text" href="http://earthsystemgovernance.org/ipo" rel="nofollow">International Project Office</a> of the Earth System Governance Project is based at Lund University, Sweden.
</p><p>Research centres on ‘Earth System Governance’ have been set up or designated at <a href="/wiki/VU_University_Amsterdam" title="VU University Amsterdam">VU University Amsterdam</a>; the <a href="/wiki/Australian_National_University" title="Australian National University">Australian National University</a>; <a href="/wiki/Chiang_Mai_University" title="Chiang Mai University">Chiang Mai University</a>; <a href="/wiki/Colorado_State_University" title="Colorado State University">Colorado State University</a>; <a href="/wiki/Lund_University" title="Lund University">Lund University</a>; <a href="/wiki/University_of_East_Anglia" title="University of East Anglia">University of East Anglia</a>; <a href="/wiki/University_of_Oldenburg" title="University of Oldenburg">University of Oldenburg</a>; the <a href="/wiki/Stockholm_Resilience_Centre" title="Stockholm Resilience Centre">Stockholm Resilience Centre</a>; the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Toronto" title="University of Toronto">University of Toronto</a>; the <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Institute_of_Technology" title="Tokyo Institute of Technology">Tokyo Institute of Technology</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yale_University" title="Yale University">Yale University</a>. In addition, strong networks on earth system governance research exist in China, Latin America, Central and Eastern Europe, and Russia.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Publications">Publications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Earth_system_governance&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Publications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>MIT Press launched in 2009 a new <a class="external text" href="http://earthsystemgovernance.org/esg%20and%20mit%20press%20book%20series" rel="nofollow">book series</a> on Earth System Governance. Recent papers drawing on the paradigm of integrated earth system governance research have analysed issues as diverse as river basin management in Hungary,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> deforestation policies in the Amazon,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> and climate change adaptation in Australia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Conceptual_framework">Conceptual framework</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Earth_system_governance&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Conceptual framework">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Earth System Governance Project builds on a conceptual framework that is organized into five <a class="external text" href="http://earthsystemgovernance.org/about/analytical-problems" rel="nofollow">analytical problems</a>. The five analytical problems identified in the Science and Implementation Plan of the <a class="external text" href="http://earthsystemgovernance.org/" rel="nofollow">Earth System Governance Project</a> are:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>The overall <i><a class="external text" href="http://earthsystemgovernance.org/about/analytical-problems/architecture" rel="nofollow">architecture</a></i> of earth system governance</li>
<li><i><a class="external text" href="http://earthsystemgovernance.org/about/analytical-problems/agency" rel="nofollow">Agency</a></i> beyond the state and of the state</li>
<li>The <i><a class="external text" href="http://earthsystemgovernance.org/about/analytical-problems/adaptiveness" rel="nofollow">adaptiveness</a></i> of governance mechanisms</li>
<li>The <i><a class="external text" href="http://earthsystemgovernance.org/about/analytical-problems/accountability" rel="nofollow">accountability and legitimacy</a></i> of governance mechanisms</li>
<li>The modes of <i><a class="external text" href="http://earthsystemgovernance.org/about/analytical-problems/allocation%20and%20access" rel="nofollow">allocation and access</a></i> in earth system governance</li></ul>
