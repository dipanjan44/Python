<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The <b>poise</b> (symbol <b>P</b>; <span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="'p' in 'pie'">p</span><span title="/ɔɪ/: 'oi' in 'choice'">ɔɪ</span><span title="'z' in 'zoom'">z</span></span>, <wbr/><span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="'p' in 'pie'">p</span><span title="'w' in 'wind'">w</span><span title="/ɑː/: 'a' in 'father'">ɑː</span><span title="'z' in 'zoom'">z</span></span>/</a></span></span>) is the unit of <a href="/wiki/Viscosity#Dynamic_viscosity" title="Viscosity">dynamic viscosity</a> (absolute viscosity) in the <a href="/wiki/Centimetre%E2%80%93gram%E2%80%93second_system_of_units" title="Centimetre–gram–second system of units">centimetre–gram–second system of units</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It is named after <a href="/wiki/Jean_L%C3%A9onard_Marie_Poiseuille" title="Jean Léonard Marie Poiseuille">Jean Léonard Marie Poiseuille</a> (see <a href="/wiki/Hagen%E2%80%93Poiseuille_equation" title="Hagen–Poiseuille equation">Hagen–Poiseuille equation</a>).

<p>The poise is often used with the <a href="/wiki/Metric_prefix" title="Metric prefix">metric prefix</a> <i>centi-</i> because the viscosity of water at 20 °C (<a href="/wiki/Standard_conditions_for_temperature_and_pressure" title="Standard conditions for temperature and pressure">NTP</a>) is almost exactly 1 centipoise.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> A centipoise is one hundredth of a poise, or one millipascal-second (mPa⋅s) in SI units (1 cP = 10<sup>−3</sup> Pa⋅s = 1 mPa⋅s).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>The CGS symbol for the centipoise is cP.  The abbreviations cps, cp, and cPs are sometimes seen.
</p><p>Liquid water has a viscosity of 0.00890 P at 25 °C and a pressure of 1 <a href="/wiki/Atmosphere_(unit)" title="Atmosphere (unit)">atmosphere</a> (0.00890 P = 0.890 cP = 0.890 mPa⋅s).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
