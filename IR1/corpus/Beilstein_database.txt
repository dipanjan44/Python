<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Beilstein database</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p>The <b>Beilstein database</b> is the largest <a href="/wiki/Chemical_database" title="Chemical database">database</a> in the field of <a href="/wiki/Organic_chemistry" title="Organic chemistry">organic chemistry</a>, in which compounds are uniquely identified by their <a href="/wiki/Beilstein_Registry_Number" title="Beilstein Registry Number">Beilstein Registry Number</a>. The database covers the scientific literature from 1771 to the present and contains experimentally validated information on millions of <a href="/wiki/Chemical_reaction" title="Chemical reaction">chemical reactions</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chemical_substance" title="Chemical substance">substances</a> from original <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scientific_publication" title="Scientific publication">scientific publications</a>. The electronic database was created from <i>Beilstein's Handbook of Organic Chemistry</i>, founded by <a href="/wiki/Friedrich_Konrad_Beilstein" title="Friedrich Konrad Beilstein">Friedrich Konrad Beilstein</a> in 1881, but has appeared online under a number of different names, including Crossfire Beilstein.  Since 2009, the content has been maintained and distributed by <a href="/wiki/Elsevier" title="Elsevier">Elsevier</a> Information Systems in Frankfurt under the product name "<a href="/wiki/Reaxys" title="Reaxys">Reaxys</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>The database contains information on reactions, substances, structures and properties. Up to 350 fields containing chemical and physical data (such as <a href="/wiki/Melting_point" title="Melting point">melting point</a>, refractive index etc.) are available for each substance. References to the literature in which the reaction or substance data appear are also given. 
</p><p>The Beilstein content made available through <a href="/wiki/Reaxys" title="Reaxys">Reaxys</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> is complemented by information drawn from Gmelin (which gives access to the <a href="/wiki/Gmelin_database" title="Gmelin database">Gmelin Database</a>), a very large repository of <a href="/wiki/Organometallic_chemistry" title="Organometallic chemistry">organometallic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Inorganic_chemistry" title="Inorganic chemistry">inorganic</a> information, as well as by information drawn from the <a href="/wiki/Patent" title="Patent">Patent</a> Chemistry Database. The Reaxys registered trademark and the database itself are owned and protected by Elsevier Properties SA and used under license.
</p>
