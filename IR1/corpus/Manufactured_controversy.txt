<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Manufactured controversy</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>A <b>manufactured controversy</b> (often shortened to <a href="/wiki/Portmanteau" title="Portmanteau">portmanteau</a> <b>manufactroversy</b>) is a contrived disagreement, typically motivated by <a href="/wiki/Profit_(economics)" title="Profit (economics)">profit</a> or <a href="/wiki/Ideology" title="Ideology">ideology</a>, designed to create public confusion concerning an issue about which there is no substantial academic dispute.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wordspy_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Wordspy-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ceccarelli_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ceccarelli-2">[2]</a></sup> This concept has also been referred to as <b>manufactured uncertainty</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Michaels_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Michaels-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mechanisms_of_manufacturing_controversy_and_uncertainty">Mechanisms of manufacturing controversy and uncertainty</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manufactured_controversy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Mechanisms of manufacturing controversy and uncertainty">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2><p>
Manufacturing controversy has been a tactic used by ideological and corporate groups in order to "neutralize the influence of academic scientists" in public policy debates.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Attie_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Attie-4">[4]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cherry_picking_(fallacy)" title="Cherry picking (fallacy)">Cherry picking</a> of favorable data and sympathetic experts, aggrandizement of uncertainties within <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Theoretical_model" title="Theoretical model">theoretical models</a>, and <a href="/wiki/False_balance" title="False balance">false balance in media reporting</a> contribute to the generation of manufactured controversies.</p><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r856303468">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>The formula is to amplify uncertainties, cherry-pick experts, attack individual scientists, marginalize the traditional role of distinguished scientific bodies and get the media to report "both sides" of a manufactured controversy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Attie_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Attie-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p></blockquote><p> Those manufacturing uncertainty may label academic research as "<a href="/wiki/Junk_science" title="Junk science">junk science</a>" and use a variety of tactics designed to stall and increase the expense of the distribution of sound scientific information.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Michaels_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Michaels-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Michaels_&amp;_Monforton_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Michaels_&amp;_Monforton-5">[5]</a></sup> Delay tactics are also used to slow the implementation of regulations and public warnings in response to previously undiscovered health risks (e.g., the increased risk of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reye%27s_syndrome" title="Reye's syndrome">Reye's syndrome</a> in children who take <a href="/wiki/Aspirin" title="Aspirin">aspirin</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Michaels_&amp;_Monforton_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Michaels_&amp;_Monforton-5">[5]</a></sup> Chief among these stalling tactics is generating scientific uncertainty, "no matter how powerful or conclusive the evidence",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Michaels_&amp;_Monforton_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-Michaels_&amp;_Monforton-5">[5]</a></sup> in order to prevent regulation.
</p><p>Another tactic used to manufacture controversy is to cast the <a href="/wiki/Scientific_community" title="Scientific community">scientific community</a> as intolerant of dissent and conspiratorially aligned with industries or sociopolitical movements that quash challenges to <a href="/wiki/Conventional_wisdom" title="Conventional wisdom">conventional wisdom</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Engber-3_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Engber-3-6">[6]</a></sup> This form of manufactured controversy has been utilized by <a href="/wiki/Environmental_movement" title="Environmental movement">environmentalist advocacy</a> groups, religious challengers of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Theory_of_evolution" title="Theory of evolution">theory of evolution</a>, and opponents of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Global_warming_legislation" title="Global warming legislation">global warming legislation</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Engber-2_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Engber-2-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legal_effects">Legal effects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manufactured_controversy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Legal effects">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the United States, the generation of manufactured uncertainty about scientific data has affected political and legal proceedings in many different areas. The <a href="/wiki/Data_Quality_Act" title="Data Quality Act">Data Quality Act</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_of_the_United_States" title="Supreme Court of the United States">Supreme Court</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Daubert_standard" title="Daubert standard"><i>Daubert</i> standard</a> have been cited as tools used by those manufacturing controversy to obfuscate <a href="/wiki/Scientific_consensus" title="Scientific consensus">scientific consensus</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Michaels_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-Michaels-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Attie_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-Attie-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>Concerns have been raised regarding the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Conflicts_of_interest" title="Conflicts of interest">conflicts of interest</a> inherent in many types of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Industry_regulation" title="Industry regulation">industry regulation</a>. For example, many industries, such as the <a href="/wiki/Pharmaceutical_industry" title="Pharmaceutical industry">pharmaceutical industry</a>, are a major source of funding for the research necessary to achieve government regulatory approval for their product.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Camargo_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Camargo-8">[8]</a></sup> In developing regulations, agencies such as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Food_and_Drug_Administration_(United_States)" title="Food and Drug Administration (United States)">Food and Drug Administration</a> and the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Environmental_Protection_Agency" title="United States Environmental Protection Agency">Environmental Protection Agency</a> rely heavily on unpublished studies from industry sources that have not been <a href="/wiki/Peer_review" title="Peer review">peer reviewed</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Michaels,_Monforton,_Lurie_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Michaels,_Monforton,_Lurie-9">[9]</a></sup> This can allow a given industry control over the extent of available research, and the pace at which it is reviewable, when challenging scientific research that may threaten their business interests.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Prominent_examples">Prominent examples</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manufactured_controversy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Prominent examples">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Examples of controversies that have been labeled manufactured controversies:
</p>
<ul><li>The <a href="/wiki/Health_effects_of_tobacco" title="Health effects of tobacco">health risks of smoking tobacco</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Attie_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-Attie-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ozone_depletion" title="Ozone depletion">Depletion of the ozone layer</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Attie_4-4"><a href="#cite_note-Attie-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Climate_change_denial" title="Climate change denial">Climate change denial</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ceccarelli_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Ceccarelli-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
<li>Development of <a href="/wiki/Skin_cancer" title="Skin cancer">skin cancer</a> from exposure to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ultraviolet_radiation" title="Ultraviolet radiation">ultraviolet radiation</a> via <a href="/wiki/Sunlight" title="Sunlight">sunlight</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tanning_lamp" title="Tanning lamp">tanning lamps</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Michaels_&amp;_Monforton_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-Michaels_&amp;_Monforton-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Armenian_Genocide_denial" title="Armenian Genocide denial">Denial of Armenian Genocide</a> by the government of <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vaccine_controversy" title="Vaccine controversy">Vaccination controversies</a>, particularly those alleging a causative relationship between the <a href="/wiki/MMR_vaccine_controversy" title="MMR vaccine controversy">MMR vaccine</a> or <a href="/wiki/Thiomersal_controversy" title="Thiomersal controversy">thiomersal</a> in the development of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Autism_spectrum_disorder" title="Autism spectrum disorder">autism spectrum disorders</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hall_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hall-11">[11]</a></sup></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AIDS_denialism" title="AIDS denialism">AIDS denialism</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ceccarelli_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Ceccarelli-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
<li>The "<a href="/wiki/Teach_the_Controversy" title="Teach the Controversy">Teach the Controversy</a>" efforts of <a href="/wiki/Intelligent_design" title="Intelligent design">intelligent design</a> supporters<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ceccarelli_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-Ceccarelli-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Carcinogen" title="Carcinogen">carcinogenicity</a> of <a href="/wiki/Hexavalent_chromium" title="Hexavalent chromium">hexavalent chromium</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Michaels,_Monforton,_Lurie_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-Michaels,_Monforton,_Lurie-9">[9]</a></sup></li></ul>
