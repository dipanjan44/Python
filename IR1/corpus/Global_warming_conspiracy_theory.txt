<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Global warming conspiracy theory</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>A <b>global warming conspiracy theory</b> invokes claims that the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scientific_consensus_on_global_warming" title="Scientific consensus on global warming">scientific consensus on global warming</a> is based on conspiracies to produce manipulated data or suppress dissent. It is one of a number of tactics used in <a href="/wiki/Climate_change_denial" title="Climate change denial">climate change denial</a> to legitimize political and public controversy disputing this consensus.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Deithelm_McKie_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Deithelm_McKie-1">[1]</a></sup>
<a href="/wiki/Global_warming" title="Global warming">Global warming</a> <a href="/wiki/Conspiracy_theory" title="Conspiracy theory">conspiracy theorists</a> typically allege that, through worldwide acts of professional and criminal misconduct, the science behind global warming has been invented or distorted for ideological or financial reasons, or both.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Achenbach_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Achenbach-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Global_warming_conspiracy_theory&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2><p>
As stated by the <a href="/wiki/Intergovernmental_Panel_on_Climate_Change" title="Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change">Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change</a> (IPCC), the largest contributor to global warming is the increase in atmospheric <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide" title="Carbon dioxide">carbon dioxide (CO<sub>2</sub>)</a> since 1750, particularly from <a href="/wiki/Flue-gas_emissions_from_fossil-fuel_combustion" title="Flue-gas emissions from fossil-fuel combustion">fossil fuel combustion</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cement#CO2_emissions" title="Cement">cement production</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Land_use" title="Land use">land use</a> changes such as <a href="/wiki/Deforestation#Atmospheric" title="Deforestation">deforestation</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>  The IPCC's <a href="/wiki/IPCC_Fifth_Assessment_Report" title="IPCC Fifth Assessment Report">Fifth Assessment Report</a> (AR5) states: </p><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r856303468">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"> <p><i>Human influence has been detected in warming of the atmosphere and the ocean, in changes in the <a href="/wiki/Water_cycle" title="Water cycle">global water cycle</a>, in reductions in <a href="/wiki/Cryosphere" title="Cryosphere">snow and ice</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Sea_level_rise" title="Sea level rise">global mean sea level rise</a>, and in changes in some <a href="/wiki/Extreme_weather" title="Extreme weather">climate extremes</a>. This evidence for human influence has grown since <a href="/wiki/IPCC_Fourth_Assessment_Report" title="IPCC Fourth Assessment Report">AR4</a>. It is extremely likely (95–100%) that human influence has been the dominant cause of the observed warming since the mid-20th century</i>. — IPCC AR5 WG1 Summary for Policymakers<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AmericasClimateChoices-2010-SciPanel_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-AmericasClimateChoices-2010-SciPanel-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p></blockquote><p> The evidence for global warming due to human influence has been recognized by the national science academies of all the major industrialized countries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SciAcademy_Statement_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-SciAcademy_Statement-7">[7]</a></sup> No scientific body of national or international standing maintains a formal opinion dissenting from the summary conclusions of the IPCC.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p><p>Despite this <a href="/wiki/Scientific_opinion_on_climate_change" title="Scientific opinion on climate change">scientific consensus on climate change</a>, allegations have been made that scientists and institutions involved in global warming research are part of a global scientific conspiracy or engaged in a manipulative hoax.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> There have been allegations of malpractice, most notably in the <a href="/wiki/Climatic_Research_Unit_email_controversy" title="Climatic Research Unit email controversy">Climatic Research Unit email controversy</a>. Eight committees investigated these allegations and published reports, each finding no evidence of fraud or scientific misconduct.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6committees_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-6committees-10">[10]</a></sup> The Muir Russell report stated that the scientists' "rigor and honesty as scientists are not in doubt," that the investigators "did not find any evidence of behavior that might undermine the conclusions of the IPCC assessments," but that there had been "a consistent pattern of failing to display the proper degree of openness."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The scientific consensus that global warming is occurring as a result of <a href="/wiki/Human_impact_on_the_environment" title="Human impact on the environment">human activity</a> remained unchanged at the end of the investigations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-scientificamerican_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-scientificamerican-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Claims">Claims</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Global_warming_conspiracy_theory&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Claims">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In a speech given to the US Senate Committee on the Environment and Public Works on July 28, 2003, entitled "The Science of Climate Change",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Inhofe_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Inhofe-14">[14]</a></sup> Senator <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/James_Inhofe" title="James Inhofe">James Inhofe</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republican_Party_(USA)" title="Republican Party (USA)">Republican</a>, for <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma" title="Oklahoma">Oklahoma</a>) concluded by asking the following question: "With all of the hysteria, all of the fear, all of the phony science, could it be that man-made global warming is the greatest hoax ever perpetrated on the American people?" He further stated, "some parts of the <a href="/wiki/Intergovernmental_Panel_on_Climate_Change" title="Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change">IPCC</a> process resembled a Soviet-style trial, in which the facts are predetermined, and ideological purity trumps technical and scientific rigor."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Inhofe-FactsAndScience_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Inhofe-FactsAndScience-15">[15]</a></sup> Inhofe has suggested that supporters of the <a href="/wiki/Kyoto_Protocol" title="Kyoto Protocol">Kyoto Protocol</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Chirac" title="Jacques Chirac">Jacques Chirac</a> are aiming at <a href="/wiki/Global_governance" title="Global governance">global governance</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup>
</p><p>Commenting on criticism of the <a href="/wiki/Lavoisier_Group" title="Lavoisier Group">Lavoisier Group</a> by <a href="/wiki/Clive_Hamilton" title="Clive Hamilton">Clive Hamilton</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Cooler_Heads_Coalition" title="Cooler Heads Coalition">Cooler Heads Coalition</a> notes that "Hamilton accuses the Lavoisier Group of painting the UN's global warming negotiations as "an elaborate conspiracy in which hundreds of climate scientists have twisted their results to support the climate change theory in order to protect their research funding" and adds, "Sounds plausible to us."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/William_M._Gray" title="William M. Gray">William M. Gray</a> said in 2006 that global warming became a political cause because of the lack of any other enemy following the end of the <a href="/wiki/Cold_War" title="Cold War">Cold War</a>. He went on to say that its purpose was to exercise political influence, to try to introduce world government, and to control people, adding, "I have a demonic view on this."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Achenbach_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Achenbach-3">[3]</a></sup> The TV documentary <i><a href="/wiki/The_Great_Global_Warming_Swindle" title="The Great Global Warming Swindle">The Great Global Warming Swindle</a></i> was made by <a href="/wiki/Martin_Durkin_(television_director)" title="Martin Durkin (television director)">Martin Durkin</a>, who called global warming "a multi-billion-dollar worldwide industry, created by fanatically anti-industrial environmentalists." In the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Washington_Times" title="Washington Times">Washington Times</a></i> in 2007 he said that his film would change history, and predicted that "in five years the idea that the greenhouse effect is the main reason behind global warming will be seen as total bunk."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Swindle_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-Swindle-18">[18]</a></sup>
</p><p>Climate change has also been called the "greatest scam in history" by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/John_Coleman_(news_weathercaster)" title="John Coleman (news weathercaster)">John Coleman</a>, who co-founded the Weather Channel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-express_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-express-19">[19]</a></sup> When questioned by the IPCC regarding his claims, he responded "The polar ice is increasing, not melting away. Polar Bears are increasing in number."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-express_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-express-19">[19]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2012, <a href="/wiki/Donald_Trump" title="Donald Trump">Donald Trump</a> claimed that "The concept of global warming was created by and for the Chinese in order to make U.S. manufacturing non-competitive." <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2015, <a href="/wiki/The_Spectator" title="The Spectator">The Spectator</a> published an article describing the "climate change agenda" as a "conspiracy against the poor."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Criticism">Criticism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Global_warming_conspiracy_theory&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Criticism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Steve Connor links the terms "hoax" and "conspiracy," saying, "Reading through the technical summary of this draft (IPCC) report, it is clear that no one could go away with the impression that climate change is some conspiratorial hoax by the science establishment, as some would have us believe."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup>
</p><p>The documentary <i>The Great Global Warming Swindle</i> received criticism from several experts. <a href="/wiki/George_Monbiot" title="George Monbiot">George Monbiot</a> described it as  "the same old conspiracy theory that we’ve been hearing from the denial industry for the past ten years".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Similarly, in response to <a href="/wiki/James_Delingpole" title="James Delingpole">James Delingpole</a>, Monbiot stated that his Spectator article was "the usual conspiracy theories [...]  working to suppress the truth, which presumably now includes virtually  the entire scientific community and everyone from <a href="/wiki/Royal_Dutch_Shell" title="Royal Dutch Shell">Shell</a> to <a href="/wiki/Greenpeace" title="Greenpeace">Greenpeace</a>  and <a href="/wiki/The_Sun_(United_Kingdom)" title="The Sun (United Kingdom)">The Sun</a> to <a href="/wiki/Science_(journal)" title="Science (journal)">Science</a>."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup>  Some Australian meteorologists also weighed in, saying that the film made no attempt to offer a "critical deconstruction of climate science orthodoxies", but instead used various other means to suggest that climate scientists are guilty of lying or are seriously misguided.  Although the film's publicist's asserted that "global warming is 'the biggest scam of modern times'", these meteorologists concluded that the film was "not scientifically sound and presents a flawed and very misleading interpretation of the science".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup>
</p><p>Former UK <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State_for_Environment,_Food_and_Rural_Affairs" title="Secretary of State for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs">Secretary of State for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs</a> <a href="/wiki/David_Miliband" title="David Miliband">David Miliband</a> presented a rebuttal of the main points of the film and stated "There will always be people with conspiracy theories trying to do down the scientific consensus, and that is part of scientific and democratic debate, but the science of climate change looks like fact to me."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup>
</p><p><i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Geographic_(magazine)" title="National Geographic (magazine)">National Geographic</a></i> fact-checked 6 persistent scientific conspiracy theories. Regarding the persistent belief in a global warming hoax they note that the Earth is continuing to warm and the rate of warming is increasing as documented in numerous scientific studies. The rise in global temperature and its rate of increase coincides with the rise of greenhouse gases in the atmosphere due to human activity. Moreover, global warming is causing Arctic sea ice to thaw at historic rates, many species of plants are blooming earlier than expected, and the migration routes of many birds, fish, mammals, and insects are changing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Funding">Funding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Global_warming_conspiracy_theory&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Funding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Climate_change_denial#Lobbying" title="Climate change denial">Climate change denial § Lobbying</a>, and <a href="/wiki/ExxonMobil#Funding_of_global_warming_disinformation_and_denial" title="ExxonMobil">ExxonMobil § Funding of global warming disinformation and denial</a></div>
<p>There is evidence that some of those alleging such conspiracies are part of well-funded misinformation campaigns designed to  manufacture controversy, undermine the scientific consensus on climate change and downplay the projected <a href="/wiki/Effects_of_global_warming" title="Effects of global warming">effects of global warming</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Griffiths-2009_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-Griffiths-2009-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CBC-2007_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-CBC-2007-29">[29]</a></sup> Individuals and organisations kept the global warming debate alive long after most scientists had reached their conclusions. These doubts have influenced policymakers in both Canada and the US, and have helped to form government policies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CBC-2007_29-1"><a href="#cite_note-CBC-2007-29">[29]</a></sup>
</p>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r856303468" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>Since the late 1980s, this well-coordinated, well-funded campaign by contrarian scientists, free-market think tanks and industry has created a paralyzing fog of doubt around climate change. Through advertisements, op-eds, lobbying and media attention, greenhouse doubters (they hate being called deniers) argued first that the world is not warming; measurements indicating otherwise are flawed, they said. Then they claimed that any warming is natural, not caused by human activities. Now they contend that the looming warming will be minuscule and harmless. "They patterned what they did after the tobacco industry," says former senator Tim Wirth, who spearheaded environmental issues as an under secretary of State in the Clinton administration. "Both figured, sow enough doubt, call the science uncertain and in dispute. That's had a huge impact on both the public and Congress."</p><div class="templatequotecite">— <cite><a class="external text" href="http://academic.evergreen.edu/curricular/energy/0708/articles/TruthDenialNewsweek07Aug.pdf" rel="nofollow">The truth about denial</a>, S. Begley, Newsweek<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></cite></div>
</blockquote>
<p><a href="/wiki/Greenpeace" title="Greenpeace">Greenpeace</a> presented evidence of the energy industry funding <a href="/wiki/Climate_change_denial" title="Climate change denial">climate change denial</a> in their 'Exxon Secrets' project.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup>  An analysis conducted by <i>The Carbon Brief</i> in 2011 found that 9 out of 10 of the most prolific authors who cast doubt on climate change or speak against it had ties to <a href="/wiki/ExxonMobil" title="ExxonMobil">ExxonMobil</a>. Greenpeace have said that <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Koch_industries" title="Koch industries">Koch industries</a> invested more than US$50 million in the past 50 years on spreading doubts about climate change.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ExxonMobil_and_Koch_Industries_denial_funding_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-ExxonMobil_and_Koch_Industries_denial_funding-33">[33]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CarbonBrief_Study:_9_out_of_top_10_authors_linked_to_ExxonMobil_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-CarbonBrief_Study:_9_out_of_top_10_authors_linked_to_ExxonMobil-34">[34]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Greenpeace_Study:_Exposing_the_dirty_money_behind_fake_climate_science_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-Greenpeace_Study:_Exposing_the_dirty_money_behind_fake_climate_science-35">[35]</a></sup> ExxonMobil announced in 2008 that it would cut its funding to many of the groups that "divert attention" from the need to find new sources of clean energy, although in 2008 still funded over "two dozen other organisations who question the science of global warming or attack policies to solve the crisis."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guardian.co.uk_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-guardian.co.uk-36">[36]</a></sup> A survey carried out by the UK <a href="/wiki/Royal_Society" title="Royal Society">Royal Society</a> found that in 2005 ExxonMobil distributed US$2.9 million to 39 groups that "misrepresented the science of climate change by outright denial of the evidence".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guardian.co.uk_36-1"><a href="#cite_note-guardian.co.uk-36">[36]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fictional_representations">Fictional representations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Global_warming_conspiracy_theory&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Fictional representations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The novel <i><a href="/wiki/State_of_Fear" title="State of Fear">State of Fear</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Crichton" title="Michael Crichton">Michael Crichton</a>, published in December 2004, describes a conspiracy by scientists and others to create public panic about global warming. The novel includes 20 pages of footnotes, described by Crichton as providing a factual basis for the non-plotline elements of the story.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Footnotes_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-Footnotes-37">[37]</a></sup> In a Senate speech on 4 January 2005, <a href="/wiki/Jim_Inhofe" title="Jim Inhofe">Inhofe</a>  mistakenly described Crichton as a "scientist", and said the book's fictional depiction of environmental organizations primarily "focused on raising money, principally by scaring potential contributors with bogus scientific claims and predictions of a global apocalypse" was an example of "art imitating life."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup>
</p><p>In a piece headed <i>Crichton's conspiracy theory</i>, Harold Evans described Crichton's theory as being "in the paranoid political style identified by the renowned historian <a href="/wiki/Richard_Hofstadter" title="Richard Hofstadter">Richard Hofstadter</a>," and went on to suggest that "if you happen to be in the market for a conspiracy theory today, there's a rather more credible one documented by the pressure group Greenpeace," namely the funding by <a href="/wiki/ExxonMobil" title="ExxonMobil">ExxonMobil</a> of groups opposed to the theory of global warming.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup>
</p>
