<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Greenhouse_gas_emissions">Greenhouse gas emissions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Climate_change_in_Scotland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Greenhouse gas emissions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:GHG_Emissions.png"><img alt="GHG Emissions.png" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="320" data-file-width="360" height="196" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/98/GHG_Emissions.png/220px-GHG_Emissions.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/98/GHG_Emissions.png/330px-GHG_Emissions.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/9/98/GHG_Emissions.png 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:GHG_Emissions.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div></div></div></div>
<p>Scotland's <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gas" title="Greenhouse gas">greenhouse gas</a> emissions only account for 10% of the UK's emissions,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> in 2003 when figures were published. 37% of Scottish emissions are in energy supply and 17% in transport. Between 1990 and 2007, Scottish net emissions have reduced by 18.7%.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The industrial processes sector had the largest decrease, of 72% with a reduction of 48% in the public sector trailing closely behind.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">Impact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Climate_change_in_Scotland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Impact">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Agriculture">Agriculture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Climate_change_in_Scotland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Agriculture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In some cases, Scottish agriculture may experience a positive change as summers will be warmer and drier. A higher yields and the possibility of new crops being able to grow in Scotland.  However, soil quality would lower with heat and soil moisture stress.
The availability of fresh water could cause problems for livestock. Heat stress With warmer and wetter conditions, livestock could face new diseases such as <a href="/wiki/West_Nile_virus" title="West Nile virus">West Nile virus</a> and outbreaks of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bluetongue" title="Bluetongue">bluetongue</a> or <a href="/wiki/Parasitism" title="Parasitism">parasites</a> could increase.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Biodiversity_and_Ecosystems">Biodiversity and Ecosystems</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Climate_change_in_Scotland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Biodiversity and Ecosystems">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Past observations have indicated some of the likely effects of climate change on biodiversity. <a href="/wiki/Global_warming" title="Global warming">Global warming</a> has already changed timings of spring events such as leaf unfolding, bird migration and egg-laying. The population of <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a> could change due to the speed at which they adapt. 
</p><p>Changes in the ranges of plant and animal species have been observed. New species may move to Scotland with the changing climate. Shifts may occur on hillsides and species that are already confined to high mountains may become extinct in Scotland. 
</p><p>Severe effects are likely to occur on biodiversity. Species of plants and animals that can't adapt quickly enough may become extinct or be replaced by other creatures. Coastal habitats, including <a href="/wiki/Machair" title="Machair">machairs</a>, may disappear due to high sea levels eroding the land. Salmon <a href="/wiki/Spawning_bed" title="Spawning bed">spawning beds</a> may be wiped out by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Flash_floods" title="Flash floods">flash floods</a> causing population problems for the species. There will be new risks to animals, plants and their habitats, including non-native pests and diseases.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>When all these effects are combined with human response, such as land use change and the growth of new forests, Scotland's ecosystems could change drastically.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mitigation">Mitigation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Climate_change_in_Scotland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Mitigation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate_Change_Act">Climate Change Act</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Climate_change_in_Scotland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Climate Change Act">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Climate_Change_(Scotland)_Act_2009" title="Climate Change (Scotland) Act 2009">Climate Change (Scotland) Act 2009</a></div>
<p>The Climate Change (Scotland) Act 2009 is an Act passed by the Scottish Parliament.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CCS_Act_2009_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-CCS_Act_2009-5">[5]</a></sup> The Act includes an emissions target, set for the year 2050, for a reduction of at least 80% from the baseline year, 1990.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CCS_Act_2009_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-CCS_Act_2009-5">[5]</a></sup> Annual targets for greenhouse gas emissions must also be set, after consultation the relevant advisory bodies. Provisions are included in the Act for the creation of the Scottish Committee on Climate Change, as at present the only advisory body is the UK <a href="/wiki/Committee_on_Climate_Change" title="Committee on Climate Change">Committee on Climate Change</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CCS_Act_2009_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-CCS_Act_2009-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Ministers in parliament must now report on the progress of these targets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> As of January 2011, public sector bodies in Scotland must comply with new guidelines set out by the Scottish Government.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Protection_and_enforcement">Protection and enforcement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Climate_change_in_Scotland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Protection and enforcement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Environment_Protection_Agency" title="Scottish Environment Protection Agency">Scottish Environment Protection Agency</a></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Environment_Protection_Agency" title="Scottish Environment Protection Agency">Scottish Environment Protection Agency</a> (SEPA) is Scotland’s environmental regulator.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Environment_Act_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Environment_Act-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> SEPA's main role is in protecting and improving Scotland's natural environment.  SEPA does this by helping communities, businesses and industries understand their legal and moral responsibilities they have relating to the environment. 
</p><p>SEPA ensures customer compliance by applying environmental legislation, encouraging good practice and realising the many economic benefits for organisations when they employ good environmental practice.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup>  SEPA also protects communities by regulating activities that can cause harmful pollution and monitoring the quality of Scotland's air, land and water.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup>
</p><p>SEPA recognises that climate change is the single greatest threat to our future. The organisation has produced its own climate change plan which contains details about how it will reduce its carbon emissions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> This five year climate change plan introduces SEPA's specific role in climate change mitigation.
</p><p>SEPA, <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Natural_Heritage" title="Scottish Natural Heritage">Scottish Natural Heritage</a> (SNH), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Forestry_Commission_Scotland" title="Forestry Commission Scotland">Forestry Commission Scotland</a> (FCS) and <a href="/wiki/Historic_Scotland" title="Historic Scotland">Historic Scotland</a> are all government funded organisations with responsibilities for different aspects of Scotland’s environment and heritage. A joint statement on climate change has been created by all partners.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup>
</p>
<blockquote>
<p>"The Scientific evidence is now overwhelming: climate change presents very serious global risks and it demands an urgent global response".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup>
</p>
</blockquote>
<p>In Scotland, the effects of climate change can be seen in raised <a href="/wiki/Temperature" title="Temperature">temperature</a> changes, increased rainfall and less snow cover. These changes have a significant impact on the growing, breeding and migration seasons, as well as species abundance and diversity.
</p>
