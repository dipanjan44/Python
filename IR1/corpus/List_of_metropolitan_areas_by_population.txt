<p>One concept which measures the <a href="/wiki/List_of_largest_cities" title="List of largest cities">world's largest cities</a> is that of the <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_area" title="Metropolitan area">metropolitan area</a>, which is based on the concept of a <i><a href="/wiki/Labor_market_area" title="Labor market area">labor market area</a></i> and is typically defined as an employment core (an area with a high density of available jobs) and the surrounding areas that have strong <a href="/wiki/Commuting" title="Commuting">commuting</a> ties to the core. There is currently no generally accepted, globally consistent definition of exactly what constitutes a metropolitan area, thus making comparisons between cities in different countries especially difficult. However, for consistency, the sources on this article include official figures from governments only.
</p><p>As an alternative to the metropolitan area, <a href="/wiki/Eurostat" title="Eurostat">Eurostat</a> introduced the concept of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Larger_Urban_Zone" title="Larger Urban Zone">Larger Urban Zone</a> in 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Similarly, <a href="/wiki/OECD" title="OECD">OECD</a> defines <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Larger_Urban_Zone" title="Larger Urban Zone">Functional Urban Areas</a> for cities in OECD countries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Both Larger Urban Zone and Functional Urban Area define a city as an urban core surrounded by a commuting zone, and so are similar to the general concept of the "metropolitan area". Eurostat only computes Larger Urban Zone populations for <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a> member states, candidate members, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/European_Free_Trade_Area" title="European Free Trade Area">European Free Trade Area</a> members. OECD computes Functional Urban Area populations for OECD member states. These two statistics are therefore not available for most developing countries.
</p>
