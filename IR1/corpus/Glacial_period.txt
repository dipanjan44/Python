<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Glacial period</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">"Glacial" redirects here. For the science fiction short story, see <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Glacial_(short_story)" title="Glacial (short story)">Glacial (short story)</a>.</div>
<p>A <b>glacial period</b> (alternatively <b>glacial</b> or <b>glaciation</b>) is an interval of time (thousands of years) within an <a href="/wiki/Ice_age" title="Ice age">ice age</a> that is marked by colder temperatures and <a href="/wiki/Glacier" title="Glacier">glacier</a> advances. <a href="/wiki/Interglacial" title="Interglacial">Interglacials</a>, on the other hand, are periods of warmer climate between glacial periods. The <a href="/wiki/Last_glacial_period" title="Last glacial period">last glacial period</a> ended about 15,000 years ago.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Severinghaus1999_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Severinghaus1999-1">[1]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Holocene" title="Holocene">Holocene</a> epoch is the current interglacial. A time when there are no glaciers on Earth is considered a <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse_and_icehouse_Earth" title="Greenhouse and icehouse Earth">greenhouse</a> <a href="/wiki/Climate_state" title="Climate state">climate state</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Quaternary_ice_age">Quaternary ice age</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glacial_period&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Quaternary ice age">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Quaternary_glaciation" title="Quaternary glaciation">Quaternary  glaciation</a> and <a href="/wiki/Timeline_of_glaciation" title="Timeline of glaciation">timeline of glaciation</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:322px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Co2_glacial_cycles_800k.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="580" data-file-width="818" height="227" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c9/Co2_glacial_cycles_800k.png/320px-Co2_glacial_cycles_800k.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c9/Co2_glacial_cycles_800k.png/480px-Co2_glacial_cycles_800k.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c9/Co2_glacial_cycles_800k.png/640px-Co2_glacial_cycles_800k.png 2x" width="320"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Co2_glacial_cycles_800k.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Glacial and <a href="/wiki/Interglacial" title="Interglacial">interglacial</a> cycles as represented by atmospheric <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide" title="Carbon dioxide">CO<sub>2</sub></a>, measured from ice core samples going back 800,000 years. The stage names are part of the North American and the European Alpine subdivisions. The correlation between both subdivisions is tentative.</div></div></div>
<p>Within the <a href="/wiki/Quaternary_glaciation" title="Quaternary glaciation">Quaternary glaciation</a> (2.6 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Annum" title="Annum">Ma</a> to present), there have been a number of glacials and interglacials.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Last_glacial_period">Last glacial period</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glacial_period&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Last glacial period">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Last_glacial_period" title="Last glacial period">Last glacial period</a></div>
<p>The last glacial period was the most recent glacial period within the <a href="/wiki/Quaternary_glaciation" title="Quaternary glaciation">Quaternary glaciation</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Current_Ice_Age" title="Current Ice Age">Current Ice Age</a>, occurring in the <a href="/wiki/Pleistocene" title="Pleistocene">Pleistocene</a> <a href="/wiki/Epoch_(geology)" title="Epoch (geology)">epoch</a>, which began about 110,000 years ago and ended about 15,000 years ago.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Severinghaus1999_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Severinghaus1999-1">[1]</a></sup> The glaciations that occurred during this glacial period covered many areas of the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Hemisphere" title="Northern Hemisphere">Northern Hemisphere</a> and have different names, depending on their geographic distributions: <i>Wisconsin</i> (in <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>), <i>Devensian</i> (in <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a>), <i>Midlandian</i> (in <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a>), <i>Würm</i> (in the <a href="/wiki/Alps" title="Alps">Alps</a>), <i>Weichsel</i> (in northern <a href="/wiki/Central_Europe" title="Central Europe">central Europe</a>), <i>Dali</i> (in <a href="/wiki/East_China" title="East China">East China</a>), <i>Beiye</i> (in <a href="/wiki/North_China" title="North China">North China</a>), <i>Taibai</i> (in <a href="/wiki/Shaanxi" title="Shaanxi">Shaanxi</a>) <i>Luojishan</i> (in Southwest <a href="/wiki/Sichuan" title="Sichuan">Sichuan</a>), <i>Zagunao</i> (in Northwest Sichuan), <i>Tianchi</i> (in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tianshan_Mountains" title="Tianshan Mountains">Tianshan Mountains</a>) <i>Qomolangma</i> (in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Himalaya" title="Himalaya">Himalaya</a>), and <i>Llanquihue</i> (in <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a>). The glacial advance reached its <a href="/wiki/Last_Glacial_Maximum" title="Last Glacial Maximum">maximum extent</a> about 18,000 <a href="/wiki/Before_Present" title="Before Present">BP</a>. In <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, the ice sheet reached <a href="/wiki/Northern_Germany" title="Northern Germany">Northern Germany</a>. In the last 650,000 years, there were, on average, seven cycles of glacial advance and retreat.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Next_glacial_period">Next glacial period</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glacial_period&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Next glacial period">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Milankovitch_cycles" title="Milankovitch cycles">Milankovitch cycles</a></div>
<p>Since orbital variations are predictable,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Varadi2003_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Varadi2003-5">[5]</a></sup> computer models that relate orbital variations to climate can predict future climate possibilities. Two caveats are necessary: that <a href="/wiki/Human_impact_on_the_environment" title="Human impact on the environment">anthropogenic</a> effects (human-assisted <a href="/wiki/Global_warming" title="Global warming">global warming</a>) are likely to exert a larger influence over the short term; and that the mechanism by which <a href="/wiki/Orbital_forcing" title="Orbital forcing">orbital forcing</a> influences climate is not well understood.
Work by Berger and Loutre suggests that the current warm climate may last another 50,000 years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Berger2002_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Berger2002-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
