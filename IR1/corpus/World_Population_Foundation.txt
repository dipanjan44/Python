<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">World Population Foundation</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The <b>World Population Foundation (WPF)</b> was founded in 1987 in the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a> by Diana and <a href="/wiki/Roy_W._Brown" title="Roy W. Brown">Roy W. Brown</a>. Their purpose was to create an organisation to draw attention to the effects of high <a href="/wiki/Birth_rate" title="Birth rate">birth rates</a> and rapid <a href="/wiki/Population_growth" title="Population growth">population growth</a> on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maternal_mortality" title="Maternal mortality">maternal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Infant_mortality" title="Infant mortality">infant mortality</a>, communities and the environment, and to raise funds for population projects and programmes, with the ultimate aim of reducing world poverty and improving the quality of life of the world’s poorest people.
</p><p>From 1987 until 1994, WPF was a small organisation mainly supported by donations from the founders and by consultancy assignments. In 1994, the WPF played an important role in the Dutch delegation of the <a href="/wiki/International_Conference_on_Population_and_Development" title="International Conference on Population and Development">International Conference on Population and Development</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cairo,_Egypt" title="Cairo, Egypt">Cairo, Egypt</a>. At this conference, the Cairo Programme of Action was adopted which still serves as a foundation for WPF’s work.
</p><p>WPF works to reduce maternal and infant mortality, and improve <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sexual_health" title="Sexual health">sexual health</a> and <a href="/wiki/Reproductive_rights" title="Reproductive rights">reproductive rights</a>. It acknowledges that attention for these themes and to the improvement of services will contribute to the struggle against <a href="/wiki/Poverty" title="Poverty">poverty</a> worldwide. Therefore, WPF advocates the implementation of these themes in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UN" title="UN">UN</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Millennium_Development_Goals" title="Millennium Development Goals">Millennium Development Goals</a>.
</p><p>2006-2009, the World Population Foundation's board members include <a href="/wiki/Nafis_Sadik" title="Nafis Sadik">Nafis Sadik</a>, former Executive Director of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UNFPA" title="UNFPA">UNFPA</a>. As of 1 January 2011, Rutgers Nisso Groep and World Population Foundation (WPF) have merged under the new name Rutgers WPF. Chair of Rutgers WPF is Bert Koenders, former minister for Development Cooperation of the Netherlands.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_developing_countries">In developing countries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Population_Foundation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: In developing countries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>WPF mainly supports local organisations that provide young and underprivileged people with access to information and services concerning sexuality and reproduction.
</p><p>WPF works to strengthen the capacity of local organisations, assists in developing and implementing activities, and provides management training. WPF’s approach is based on Dutch policies on sexual and reproductive health, especially with regard to young people. As of 2006, WPF supported NGOs in nine countries.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p><p>Currently WPF maintains field offices in <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Vietnam" title="Vietnam">Vietnam</a> and <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a> (<a href="/wiki/Rutgers_WPF_Indonesia" title="Rutgers WPF Indonesia">Rutgers WPF Indonesia</a>).
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Advocacy_activities">Advocacy activities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Population_Foundation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Advocacy activities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>WPF engages in advocacy with policy makers in the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, and, at the international level, with the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a>. Indirect advocacy includes public awareness creation through the Dutch media and with partner organizations in developing countries.
</p><p>WPF is an active member of <a class="external text" href="http://www.share-net.nl" rel="nofollow">Share-Net</a> and <a class="external text" href="http://www.eurongos.org" rel="nofollow">EuroNGOs</a>. In 2004 WPF was granted 'Special Consultative Status' by the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Economic_and_Social_Council" title="United Nations Economic and Social Council">United Nations Economic and Social Council</a>.
</p><p>WPF established a youth organization: <a class="external text" href="http://www.choiceforyouth.org" rel="nofollow">CHOICE for youth and sexuality</a>. CHOICE went on to initiate the <a class="external text" href="http://www.youthcoalition.org" rel="nofollow">Youth Coalition</a>) and YouAct, a European youth networkon sexual and reproductive health and rights.
</p><p>In the Netherlands WPF is campaigning under the MYBODY label, with the slogan My Life, My Choice, MYBODY.
</p>
