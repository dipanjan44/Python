<p><b>Xenon tetroxide</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Chemical_compound" title="Chemical compound">chemical compound</a> of <a href="/wiki/Xenon" title="Xenon">xenon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oxygen" title="Oxygen">oxygen</a> with molecular formula XeO<sub>4</sub>, remarkable for being a relatively stable <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Noble_gas_compounds" title="Noble gas compounds">compound of a noble gas</a>. It is a yellow <a href="/wiki/Crystal" title="Crystal">crystalline</a> <a href="/wiki/Solid" title="Solid">solid</a> that is stable below −35.9 °<a href="/wiki/Celsius" title="Celsius">C</a>; above that temperature it is very prone to exploding and decomposing into elemental xenon and oxygen (O<sub>2</sub>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-selig_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-selig-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>All eight <a href="/wiki/Valence_electron" title="Valence electron">valence electrons</a> of xenon are involved in the bonds with the oxygen, and the <a href="/wiki/Oxidation_state" title="Oxidation state">oxidation state</a> of the xenon atom is +8. Oxygen is the only <a href="/wiki/Chemical_element" title="Chemical element">element</a> that can bring xenon up to its highest oxidation state; even <a href="/wiki/Fluorine" title="Fluorine">fluorine</a> can only give <a href="/wiki/Xenon_hexafluoride" title="Xenon hexafluoride">XeF<sub>6</sub></a> (+6).
Two other short-lived <a href="/wiki/Noble_gas_compound" title="Noble gas compound">xenon compounds</a> with an oxidation state of +8, XeO<sub>3</sub>F<sub>2</sub> and XeO<sub>2</sub>F<sub>4</sub>, are accessible by the reaction of xenon tetroxide with <a href="/wiki/Xenon_hexafluoride" title="Xenon hexafluoride">xenon hexafluoride</a>. XeO<sub>3</sub>F<sub>2</sub> and XeO<sub>2</sub>F<sub>4</sub> can be detected with <a href="/wiki/Mass_spectrometry" title="Mass spectrometry">mass spectrometry</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Perxenate" title="Perxenate">perxenates</a> are also compounds where xenon has the +8 oxidation state.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reactions">Reactions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xenon_tetroxide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Reactions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At temperatures above −35.9 °C, xenon tetroxide is very prone to explosion, decomposing into xenon gas and oxygen with ΔH = −643 kJ/mol:
</p>
<dl><dd>XeO<sub>4</sub> → Xe + 2 O<sub>2</sub></dd></dl>
<p>Xenon tetroxide dissolves in water to form <a href="/wiki/Perxenate#Perxenic_acid" title="Perxenate">perxenic acid</a> and in alkalis to form <a href="/wiki/Perxenate" title="Perxenate">perxenate</a> salts:
</p>
<dl><dd>XeO<sub>4</sub> + 2 H<sub>2</sub>O → H<sub>4</sub>XeO<sub>6</sub></dd>
<dd>XeO<sub>4</sub> + 4 NaOH → Na<sub>4</sub>XeO<sub>6</sub> + 2 H<sub>2</sub>O</dd></dl>
<p>Xenon tetroxide can also react with <a href="/wiki/Xenon_hexafluoride" title="Xenon hexafluoride">xenon hexafluoride</a> to give xenon oxyfluorides:
</p>
<dl><dd>XeO<sub>4</sub> + XeF<sub>6</sub> → XeOF<sub>4</sub> + XeO<sub>3</sub>F<sub>2</sub></dd>
<dd>XeO<sub>4</sub> + 2XeF<sub>6</sub> → XeO<sub>2</sub>F<sub>4</sub> + XeOF<sub>4</sub></dd></dl>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Synthesis">Synthesis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Xenon_tetroxide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Synthesis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>All syntheses start from the <a href="/wiki/Perxenate" title="Perxenate">perxenates</a>, which are accessible from the <a href="/wiki/Xenic_acid" title="Xenic acid">xenates</a> through two methods. One is the <a href="/wiki/Disproportionation" title="Disproportionation">disproportionation</a> of xenates to perxenates and xenon:
</p>
<dl><dd>2 HXeO<sub>4</sub><sup>−</sup> + 2 OH<sup>−</sup> → XeO<sub>6</sub><sup>4−</sup> + Xe + O<sub>2</sub> + 2 H<sub>2</sub>O</dd></dl>
<p>The other is oxidation of the xenates with <a href="/wiki/Ozone" title="Ozone">ozone</a> in basic solution:
</p>
<dl><dd>HXeO<sub>4</sub><sup>−</sup> + O<sub>3</sub> + 3 OH<sup>−</sup> → XeO<sub>6</sub><sup>4−</sup> + O<sub>2</sub> + 2 H<sub>2</sub>O</dd></dl>
