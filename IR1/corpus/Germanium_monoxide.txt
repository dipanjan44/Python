<p><b>Germanium monoxide</b>, GeO, is a chemical compound of <a href="/wiki/Germanium" title="Germanium">germanium</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oxygen" title="Oxygen">oxygen</a>. It can be prepared as a yellow sublimate at 1000 °C by reacting <a href="/wiki/Germanium_dioxide" title="Germanium dioxide">GeO<sub>2</sub></a> with Ge metal. The yellow sublimate turns brown on heating at 650 °C.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Greenwood_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Greenwood-1">[1]</a></sup> GeO is not well characterised.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Greenwood_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Greenwood-1">[1]</a></sup> It is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amphoteric" title="Amphoteric">amphoteric</a> dissolving in acids to form germanium(II) salts and in alkali to form "trihydroxogermanates" or "germanites" containing the Ge(OH)<sub>3</sub><sup>−</sup> ion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wiberg&amp;Holleman_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Wiberg&amp;Holleman-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
