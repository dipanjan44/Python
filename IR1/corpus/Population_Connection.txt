<p><b>Population Connection</b> (formerly <b>Zero Population Growth</b> or <b>ZPG</b>) is a non-profit organization in the United States that raises awareness of population challenges and advocates for improved global access to <a href="/wiki/Family_planning" title="Family planning">family planning</a> and <a href="/wiki/Reproductive_health" title="Reproductive health">reproductive health</a> care. The organization was founded in 1968 by <a href="/wiki/Paul_R._Ehrlich" title="Paul R. Ehrlich">Paul R. Ehrlich</a>, Richard Bowers and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Charles_Remington" title="Charles Remington">Charles Remington</a> in the wake of Ehrlich's best-selling book, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Population_Bomb" title="The Population Bomb">The Population Bomb</a></i>. The organization adopted its current name in 2002.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mission">Mission</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Population_Connection&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Mission">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"<a href="/wiki/Human_overpopulation" title="Human overpopulation">Overpopulation</a> threatens the quality of life for people everywhere. Population Connection is the national grassroots population organization that educates young people and advocates progressive action to stabilize world population at a level that can be sustained by Earth's resources."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>Population Connection is the largest grassroots population organization in the United States, with more than 500,000 members, supporters, and participating educators. Population Connection works to ensure that every woman around the world who wants to delay or end childbearing has access to the health services and contraceptive supplies she needs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Initiatives">Initiatives</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Population_Connection&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Initiatives">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Population_Connection&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Population Connection’s education program, known as <a class="external text" href="http://www.populationeducation.org/" rel="nofollow">Population Education</a> or PopEd, develops K-12 curricula and conducts professional development workshops for teachers that focus on human population issues. Since 1975, the program has developed age-appropriate curricula to complement students’ science and social science instruction about human population trends and their impacts on natural resources, environmental quality, and human well-being. The program staff and their network of regional volunteers facilitate approximately 550 workshops each year for 10,000-12,000 teachers and future teachers throughout the U.S. and parts of Canada. The program staff is then able to educate over three million students on the impacts of overpopulation each year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Population Education also hosts an international student video contest each year through their <a class="external text" href="https://www.worldof7billion.org/" rel="nofollow">World of 7 Billion</a> website.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Activism">Activism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Population_Connection&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Activism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a class="external text" href="http://www.populationconnectionaction.org/" rel="nofollow">Population Connection Action Fund</a> is the political arm of Population Connection. Founded in 2013, their mission is to educate the American people and advocate progressive action to stabilize world population at a level that can be sustained by Earth’s resources. Since the beginning of 2014, Population Connection Action Fund has delivered nearly half a million petition signatures to the White House calling for President Obama to rein in the overly broad enforcement of the <a href="/wiki/Helms_Amendment" title="Helms Amendment">Helms Amendment</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>Population Connection sponsors an annual advocacy event in Washington, D.C. called Capitol Hill Days. During the event activists attend information sessions, learn advocacy techniques, and lobby on Capitol Hill for a greater U.S. investment in international family planning. In 2018, 350 activists from 31 states participated in the event.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-capitol_hill_days_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-capitol_hill_days-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Population_Connection_Magazine"><i>Population Connection</i> Magazine</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Population_Connection&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Population Connection Magazine">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Population Connection</i> is Population Connection's quarterly publication. The magazine highlights the connections between <a href="/wiki/Human_overpopulation" title="Human overpopulation">overpopulation</a>, the environment, poverty, and women’s empowerment. It provides important population news, research, program developments, and legislative updates. Annual membership ($25) includes a one-year subscription to <i>Population Connection</i> magazine. All contributions, bequests, and gifts are fully tax-deductible in accordance with current laws.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p>
