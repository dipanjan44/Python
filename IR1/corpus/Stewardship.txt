<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Stewardship</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Stewardship</b> is an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ethic" title="Ethic">ethic</a> that embodies the responsible planning and management of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Resources" title="Resources">resources</a>. The concepts of stewardship can be applied to the environment and nature,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> economics,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> health,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> property,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> information,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> theology,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> etc.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_of_the_term">History of the term</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stewardship&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History of the term">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Stewardship was originally made up of the tasks of a domestic <a href="/wiki/Steward_(office)" title="Steward (office)">steward</a>, from <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/stig#Old_English" title="wikt:stig">stiġ</a> (<i>house</i>, <i>hall</i>) and <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/weard#Old_English" title="wikt:weard">weard</a>, (<i>ward</i>, <i>guard</i>, <i>guardian</i>, <i>keeper</i>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Stewardship in the beginning referred to the household servant’s duties for bringing food and drink to the castle’s dining hall. Stewardship responsibilities were eventually expanded to include everything the domestic, service and management needs of the entire household.  
</p><p>Commercial stewardship tends to the domestic and service requirements of passengers on ships, trains, airplanes or guests in restaurants.  This concept of stewardship continues to be referenced within these specific categories.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p><p>Stewardship is now generally recognized as the acceptance or assignment of responsibility to shepherd and safeguard the valuables of others.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p>

<ul><li><a class="external text" href="http://oceanservice.noaa.gov/education/planet-stewards//link_1" rel="nofollow">NOAA Planet Stewards Educational Project</a></li></ul>
<p>The NOAA Planet Stewards Education Project (PSEP) is an example of an environmental stewardship program in the United States to advance scientific literacy especially in areas that conserve, restore, and protect human communities and natural resources in the areas of climate, ocean, and atmosphere. It includes professional teachers of students of all ages and abilities, and informal educators who work with the public in nature and science centers, aquaria, and zoos. The project began in 2008 as the NOAA Climate Stewards Project. Its name was changed to NOAA Planet Stewards Educational Project in 2016. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
