<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">European Community number</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Not to be confused with <a href="/wiki/Enzyme_Commission_number" title="Enzyme Commission number">Enzyme Commission number</a>.</div>
<p>The <b>European Community number</b> (<b>EC number</b>) is a unique seven-digit identifier that was assigned to substances for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Regulatory" title="Regulatory">regulatory</a> purposes within the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a> by the European Commission. The <i>EC Inventory</i> comprises three individual inventories, EINECS, ELINCS and the NLP list.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Structure">Structure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=European_Community_number&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Structure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The EC Number may be written in a general form as: NNN-NNN-R,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> where R is a check digit and N represents integers. The check digit is calculated using the <a href="/wiki/International_Standard_Book_Number#Check_digits" title="International Standard Book Number">ISBN</a> method. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="EC_Inventory">EC Inventory</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=European_Community_number&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: EC Inventory">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The EC Inventory includes the substances in the following inventories. The content of these inventories is fixed and official.
</p>

<tr>
<td>European Inventory of Existing Commercial Chemical Substances (EINECS)
</td>
<td>Substances, excluding polymers, that were recorded as being commercially available in the EU from 1 January 1971 to 18 September 1981.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-geiss_inventory_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-geiss_inventory-3">[3]</a></sup> These were considered registered under Article 8(1) of the <a href="/wiki/Dangerous_Substances_Directive_(67/548/EEC)" title="Dangerous Substances Directive (67/548/EEC)">Dangerous Substances Directive (67/548/EEC)</a>. They are considered phase-in substances under the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/REACH_Regulation" title="REACH Regulation">REACH Regulation</a>.
</td>
<td><span class="nowrap">2xx-xxx-x</span><br/><span class="nowrap">3xx-xxx-x</span>
</td>
<td>100,102<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-geiss_inventory_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-geiss_inventory-3">[3]</a></sup>
</td></tr>
<tr>
<td>European List of Notified Chemical Substances (ELINCS)
</td>
<td>Substances notified under the Dangerous Substances Directive Notification of New Substances (NONS) that became commercially available after 18 September 1981.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</td>
<td><span class="nowrap">4xx-xxx-x</span>
</td>
<td>4,381
</td></tr>
<tr>
<td>No Longer Polymers (NLP)
</td>
<td>The definition of polymers was changed in April, 1992<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> with the result that substances previously considered to be polymers were no longer regarded as polymers. Consequently, a list, called the NLP-list (No Longer Polymer), was made of such substances that were not covered by the polymer definition but were placed on the market between after 18 September 1981 and 31 October 1993.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</td>
<td><span class="nowrap">5xx-xxx-x</span>
</td>
<td>703
</td></tr></tbody></table>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="List_numbers">List numbers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=European_Community_number&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: List numbers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/European_Chemicals_Agency" title="European Chemicals Agency">European Chemicals Agency</a> (ECHA) also applies the EC number format to what it calls "List number".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The number are assigned under the REACH Regulation without being legally recognised. Hence, they are not official because they have not been published in the <a href="/wiki/Official_Journal_of_the_European_Union" title="Official Journal of the European Union">Official Journal of the European Union</a>. List numbers are administrative tools only and shall not be used for any official purposes.
</p>
