<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Energy conservation</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Energy conservation</b> is the effort made to reduce the <a href="/wiki/Energy_consumption" title="Energy consumption">consumption of energy</a> by using less of an energy service. This can be achieved either by <a href="/wiki/Efficient_energy_use" title="Efficient energy use">using energy more efficiently</a> (using less energy for a constant service) or by reducing the amount of service used (for example, by driving less). Energy conservation is a part of the concept of <a href="/wiki/Eco-sufficiency" title="Eco-sufficiency">eco-sufficiency</a>. Energy conservation reduces the need for energy services and can result in increased <a href="/wiki/Natural_environment" title="Natural environment">environmental</a> quality, <a href="/wiki/National_security" title="National security">national security</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Personal_security" title="Personal security">personal financial security</a> and higher savings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-zehner-2011_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-zehner-2011-1">[1]</a></sup>
It is at the top of the sustainable <a href="/wiki/Energy_hierarchy" title="Energy hierarchy">energy hierarchy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
It also lowers energy costs by preventing future <a href="/wiki/Resource_depletion" title="Resource depletion">resource depletion</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>Energy can be conserved by reducing wastage and losses, improving efficiency through technological upgrades and improved operation and maintenance. On a global level energy use can also be reduced by the stabilisation of <a href="/wiki/Population_growth" title="Population growth">population growth</a>.
</p><p>Energy can only be transformed from one form to other, such as heat energy to motive power in cars, or kinetic energy of water flow to electricity in hydroelectric power plants. However machines are required to transform energy from one form to other. The wear and friction of the components of these machine while running cause loss of quadrillions of BTU and $500 billions in industries only in USA. It is possible to minimize these losses by adopting green engineering practices to improve life cycle of the components.(Ref.R.Chattopadhyay:Green Tribology, Green Surface Engineering and Global Warming', ASM International,USA, 2014. Also  3 books by same author published earlier on wear, surface engineering and global warming.)        

</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Energy_tax">Energy tax</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Energy_conservation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Energy tax">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Some countries employ <a href="/wiki/Energy_tax" title="Energy tax">energy</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carbon_taxes" title="Carbon taxes">carbon taxes</a> to motivate energy users to reduce their consumption. Carbon taxes can force consumption to shift to nuclear power and other energy sources that carry different sets of environmental side effects and limitations. On the other hand, taxes on all energy consumption can reduce energy use across the board while reducing a broader array of environmental consequences arising from energy production. The state of California employs a tiered energy tax whereby every consumer receives a baseline energy allowance that carries a low tax. As usage increases above that baseline, the tax increases drastically. Such programs aim to protect poorer households while creating a larger tax burden for high energy consumers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Building_design">Building design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Energy_conservation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Building design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:102px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Light_switch_with_passive_infrared_sensor.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3001" data-file-width="2251" height="133" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/Light_switch_with_passive_infrared_sensor.jpg/100px-Light_switch_with_passive_infrared_sensor.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/Light_switch_with_passive_infrared_sensor.jpg/150px-Light_switch_with_passive_infrared_sensor.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/Light_switch_with_passive_infrared_sensor.jpg/200px-Light_switch_with_passive_infrared_sensor.jpg 2x" width="100"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Light_switch_with_passive_infrared_sensor.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Occupancy_sensor" title="Occupancy sensor">Occupancy sensors</a> can conserve energy by turning off appliances in unoccupied rooms.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-calif_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-calif-5">[5]</a></sup></div></div></div>
<p>One of the primary ways to improve energy conservation in buildings is to perform an <a href="/wiki/Energy_audit" title="Energy audit">energy audit</a>. An energy audit is an <a href="/wiki/Inspection" title="Inspection">inspection</a> and analysis of energy use and flows for energy conservation in a building, process or system with an eye toward reducing energy input without negatively affecting output. This is normally accomplished by trained professionals and can be part of some of the national programs discussed above. Recent development of <a href="/wiki/Smartphone" title="Smartphone">smartphone</a> apps enables homeowners to complete relatively sophisticated energy audits themselves.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>Building technologies and smart meters can allow energy users, both commercial and residential, to visualize the impact their energy use can have in their workplace or homes. Advanced real-time energy metering can help people save energy by their actions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Illust_passive_solar_d1.gif"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="216" data-file-width="319" height="149" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/Illust_passive_solar_d1.gif/220px-Illust_passive_solar_d1.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/4/49/Illust_passive_solar_d1.gif 1.5x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Illust_passive_solar_d1.gif" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Elements of passive solar design, shown in a direct gain application</div></div></div>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Passive_solar_building_design" title="Passive solar building design">passive solar building design</a>, windows, walls, and floors are made to collect, store, and distribute <a href="/wiki/Solar_energy" title="Solar energy">solar energy</a> in the form of heat in the winter and reject solar heat in the summer. This is called passive solar design or climatic design because, unlike active <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Solar_heating" title="Solar heating">solar heating</a> systems, it does not involve the use of mechanical and electrical devices.
</p><p>The key to designing a passive solar building is to best take advantage of the local <a href="/wiki/Climate" title="Climate">climate</a>. Elements to be considered include window placement and glazing type, <a href="/wiki/Thermal_insulation" title="Thermal insulation">thermal insulation</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thermal_mass" title="Thermal mass">thermal mass</a>, and shading. Passive solar design techniques can be applied most easily to new buildings, but existing buildings can be retrofitted.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Energy_conservation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Carfree_city" title="Carfree city">Carfree_city</a></div>
<p>In the United States, suburban infrastructure evolved during an age of relatively easy access to fossil fuels, which has led to transportation-dependent systems of living. Zoning reforms that allow greater <a href="/wiki/Urban_density" title="Urban density">urban density</a> as well as designs for walking and bicycling can greatly reduce energy consumed for transportation. The use of telecommuting by major corporations is a significant opportunity to conserve energy, as many Americans now work in service jobs that enable them to work from home instead of commuting to work each day.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Consumer_products">Consumer products</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Energy_conservation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Consumer products">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Assorted_LED_Lamps.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1425" data-file-width="2043" height="153" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c6/Assorted_LED_Lamps.jpg/220px-Assorted_LED_Lamps.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c6/Assorted_LED_Lamps.jpg/330px-Assorted_LED_Lamps.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c6/Assorted_LED_Lamps.jpg/440px-Assorted_LED_Lamps.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Assorted_LED_Lamps.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>An assortment of energy-efficient semiconductor (LED) lamps for commercial and residential lighting use. LED lamps use at least 75% less energy, and last 25 times longer, than traditional incandescent light bulbs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></div></div></div>
<p>Consumers are often poorly informed of the savings of energy efficient products.  A prominent example of this is the energy savings that can be made by replacing an <a href="/wiki/Incandescent_light_bulb" title="Incandescent light bulb">incandescent light bulb</a> with a more modern alternative.  When purchasing light bulbs, many consumers opt for cheap incandescent bulbs, failing to take into account their higher energy costs and lower lifespans when compared to modern <a href="/wiki/Compact_fluorescent_lamp" title="Compact fluorescent lamp">compact fluorescent</a> and <a href="/wiki/LED_lamp" title="LED lamp">LED bulbs</a>.  Although these energy-efficient alternatives have a higher upfront cost, their long lifespan and low energy use can save consumers a considerable amount of money.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The price of LED bulbs has also been steadily decreasing in the past five years due to improvements in semiconductor technology.  Many LED bulbs on the market qualify for utility rebates that further reduce the price of purchase to the consumer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Estimates by the U.S. Department of Energy state that widespread adoption of LED lighting over the next 20 years could result in about $265 billion worth of savings in United States energy costs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p><p>The research one must put into conserving energy is often too time consuming and costly for the average consumer when there are cheaper products and technology available using today's fossil fuels.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Some governments and NGOs are attempting to reduce this complexity with <a href="/wiki/Ecolabel" title="Ecolabel">ecolabels</a> that make differences in energy efficiency easy to research while shopping.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p><p>To provide the kind of information and support people need to invest money, time and effort in energy conservation, it is important to understand and link to people's topical concerns.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> For instance, some retailers argue that bright <a href="/wiki/Lighting" title="Lighting">lighting</a> stimulates purchasing. However, health studies have demonstrated that headache, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stress_(medicine)" title="Stress (medicine)">stress</a>, <a href="/wiki/Blood_pressure" title="Blood pressure">blood pressure</a>, fatigue and worker error all generally increase with the common <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Over-illumination" title="Over-illumination">over-illumination</a> present in many workplace and retail settings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Davis_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Davis-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bain_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bain-16">[16]</a></sup> It has been shown that natural <a href="/wiki/Daylighting" title="Daylighting">daylighting</a> increases productivity levels of workers, while reducing energy consumption.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup>
</p><p>In warm climates where air conditioning is used, any household device that gives off heat will result in a larger load on the cooling system. Items such as stoves, dish washers, clothes dryers, hot water and incandescent lighting all add heat to the home. Low-power or insulated versions of these devices give off less heat for the air conditioning to remove. The air conditioning system can also improve in efficiency by using a heat sink that is cooler than the standard air heat exchanger, such as geothermal or water.
</p><p>In cold climates, heating air and water is a major demand on household energy use. Significant energy reductions are possible by using different technologies. <a href="/wiki/Heat_pump" title="Heat pump">Heat pumps</a> are a more efficient alternative to electrical resistance heaters for warming air or water. A variety of efficient <a href="/wiki/Clothes_dryer" title="Clothes dryer">clothes dryers</a> are available, and the <a href="/wiki/Clothes_line" title="Clothes line">clothes lines</a> requires no energy- only time. Natural-gas <a href="/wiki/Condensing_boiler" title="Condensing boiler">condensing boilers</a> and hot-air furnaces increase efficiency over standard hot-flue models. New construction implementing <a href="/wiki/Heat_exchanger" title="Heat exchanger">heat exchangers</a> can capture heat from waste water or exhaust air in bathrooms, laundry and kitchens.
</p><p>In both warm and cold climate extremes, airtight thermal <a href="/wiki/Building_insulation" title="Building insulation">insulated construction</a> is the largest factor determining the efficiency of a home. Insulation is added to minimize the flow of heat to or from the home, but can be labor-intensive to retrofit to an existing home.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Energy_conservation_by_country">Energy conservation by country</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Energy_conservation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Energy conservation by country">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Asia">Asia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Energy_conservation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Asia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Despite the vital role energy efficiency is expected to play in cost-effectively cutting energy demand, only a small part of its economic potential is exploited in the Asia. Governments have implemented a range of subsidies such as cash grants, cheap credit, tax exemptions, and co-financing with public-sector funds to encourage a range of energy-efficiency initiatives across several sectors. Governments in the Asia-Pacific region have implemented a range of information provision and labeling programs for buildings, appliances, and the transportation and industrial
sectors. Information programs can simply provide data, such as fuel-economy labels, or actively seek to encourage behavioral changes, such as Japan's <a href="/wiki/Cool_Biz_campaign" title="Cool Biz campaign">Cool Biz campaign</a> that encourages setting air conditioners at 28-degrees Celsius and allowing employees to dress casually in the summer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="European_Union">European Union</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Energy_conservation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: European Union">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the end of 2006, the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a> (EU) pledged to cut its annual consumption of primary energy by 20% by 2020.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> The 'European Union Energy Efficiency Action Plan' is long-awaited. Directive 2012/27/EU is on energy efficiency.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-directive-2012-27-eu_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-directive-2012-27-eu-21">[21]</a></sup>
</p><p>As part of the EU's SAVE Programme,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> aimed at promoting energy efficiency and encouraging energy-saving behavior, the <a href="/wiki/Boiler" title="Boiler">Boiler</a> Efficiency Directive<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> specifies minimum levels of efficiency for boilers utilizing liquid or gaseous fuels.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="India">India</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Energy_conservation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: India">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Petroleum_Conservation_Research_Association" title="Petroleum Conservation Research Association">Petroleum Conservation Research Association</a> (PCRA) is an Indian governmental body created in 1978 that engages in promoting energy efficiency and conservation in every walk of life. In the recent past PCRA has done mass-media campaigns in television, radio, and print media. An impact-assessment survey by a third party revealed that due to these larger campaigns by PCRA, the public's overall awareness level has gone up leading to saving of fossil fuels worth crores of rupees, besides reducing pollution.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Bureau_of_Energy_Efficiency" title="Bureau of Energy Efficiency">Bureau of Energy Efficiency</a> is an Indian government organization created in 2001 that is responsible for promoting energy efficiency and conservation.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Japan">Japan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Energy_conservation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Japan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:03-05-JPN153.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1280" data-file-width="960" height="227" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/03-05-JPN153.jpg/170px-03-05-JPN153.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/03-05-JPN153.jpg/255px-03-05-JPN153.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/03-05-JPN153.jpg/340px-03-05-JPN153.jpg 2x" width="170"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:03-05-JPN153.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div><b>Advertising with high energy in Shinjuku, Japan.</b></div></div></div>
<p>Since the <a href="/wiki/1973_oil_crisis" title="1973 oil crisis">1973 oil crisis</a>, energy conservation has been an issue in Japan. All oil-based fuel is imported, so domestic <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_energy" title="Sustainable energy">sustainable energy</a> is being developed.
</p><p>The Energy Conservation Center<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> promotes energy efficiency in every aspect of Japan. Public entities are implementing the efficient use of energy for industries and research. It includes projects such as the Top Runner Program.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> In this project, new appliances are regularly tested on efficiency, and the most efficient ones are made the standard.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lebanon">Lebanon</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Energy_conservation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Lebanon">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In Lebanon and since 2002 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Lebanese_Center_for_Energy_Conservation" title="The Lebanese Center for Energy Conservation">The Lebanese Center for Energy Conservation (LCEC)</a> has been promoting the development of efficient and rational uses of energy and the use of renewable energy at the consumer level. It was created as a project financed by the International Environment Facility (GEF) and the Ministry of Energy Water (MEW) under the management of the United Nations Development Programme (UNDP) and gradually established itself as an independent technical national center although it continues to be supported by the United Nations Development Programme (UNDP) as indicated in the Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) signed between MEW and UNDP on 18 June 2007.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Nepal">Nepal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Energy_conservation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Nepal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Until recently, <a href="/wiki/Nepal" title="Nepal">Nepal</a> has been focusing on the exploitation of its huge water resources to produce hydro power. Demand side management and energy conservation was not in the focus of government action. In 2009, bilateral Development Cooperation between Nepal and the Federal Republic of Germany, has agreed upon the joint implementation of "Nepal Energy Efficiency Programme". The lead executing agencies for the implementation are the Water and Energy Commission Secretariat (WECS). The aim of the programme is the promotion of energy efficiency in policy making, in rural and urban households as well as in the industry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup>
Due to the lack of a government organization that promotes energy efficiency in the country, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Federation_of_Nepalese_Chambers_of_Commerce_and_Industry_(FNCCI)" title="Federation of Nepalese Chambers of Commerce and Industry (FNCCI)">Federation of Nepalese Chambers of Commerce and Industry (FNCCI)</a> has established the Energy Efficiency Centre under his roof to promote energy conservation in the private sector. The Energy Efficiency Centre is a non-profit initiative that is offering energy auditing services to the industries. The Centre is also supported by Nepal Energy Efficiency Programme of <a href="/wiki/Deutsche_Gesellschaft_f%C3%BCr_Internationale_Zusammenarbeit" title="Deutsche Gesellschaft für Internationale Zusammenarbeit">Deutsche Gesellschaft für Internationale Zusammenarbeit</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup>
A study conducted in 2012 found out that Nepalese industries could save 160,000 Megawatt hours of electricity and 8,000 Terajoule of thermal energy (like diesel, furnace oil and coal) every year. These savings are equivalent to annual energy cost cut of up to 6.4 Billion Nepalese Rupees.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup>
As a result of Nepal Economic Forum 2014,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> an economic reform agenda in the priority sectors was declared focusing on energy conservation among others. In the energy reform agenda the government of Nepal gave the commitment to introduce incentive packages in the budget of the fiscal year 2015/16 for industries that practices energy efficiency or use efficient technologies (incl. cogeneration).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="New_Zealand">New Zealand</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Energy_conservation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: New Zealand">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a> the <a href="/wiki/Energy_Efficiency_and_Conservation_Authority" title="Energy Efficiency and Conservation Authority">Energy Efficiency and Conservation Authority</a> is the Government Agency responsible for promoting energy efficiency and conservation.  The <i><b>Energy Management Association of New Zealand</b></i> is a membership based organization representing the New Zealand energy services sector, providing training and accreditation services with the aim of ensuring energy management services are credible and dependable.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Nigeria">Nigeria</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Energy_conservation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Nigeria">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In Nigeria, the Lagos State Government is encouraging Lagosians to imbibe an energy conservation culture. The Lagos State Electricity Board (LSEB)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> is spearheading an initiative tagged "Conserve Energy, Save Money" under the Ministry of Energy and Mineral Resources. The initiative is designed to sensitize Lagosians around the theme of energy conservation by connecting with and influencing their behavior through do-it-yourself tips<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> and exciting interaction with prominent personalities. In September 2013, <a href="/wiki/Babatunde_Fashola" title="Babatunde Fashola">Governor Babatunde Raji Fashola</a> of Lagos State and rapper Jude 'MI'  Abaga (campaign ambassador)(<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup>) participated in the Governor's first ever Google+ Hangout<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> on the topic of energy conservation.
</p><p>In addition to the hangout, during the month of October (the official energy conservation month in the state), LSEB hosted experience centers in malls around Lagos State where members of the public were encouraged to calculate their current household energy consumption and discover ways to save money using the 1st-ever consumer-focused energy app in sub-saharan Africa.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> To get Lagosians started on energy conservation, Solar Lamps and Phillips Energy-saving bulbs were also given out at each experience center.

</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sri_Lanka">Sri Lanka</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Energy_conservation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Sri Lanka">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sri Lanka currently consumes <a href="/wiki/Fossil_fuel" title="Fossil fuel">fossil fuels</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hydroelectricity" title="Hydroelectricity">hydro power</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wind_power" title="Wind power">wind power</a>, <a href="/wiki/Solar_power" title="Solar power">solar power</a> and <a href="/wiki/Energy_crop" title="Energy crop">dendro power</a> for their day to day power generation. The <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka_Sustainable_Energy_Authority" title="Sri Lanka Sustainable Energy Authority">Sri Lanka Sustainable Energy Authority</a> is playing a major role regarding energy management and energy conservation. Today, most of the industries are requested to reduce their energy consumption by using renewable energy sources and optimizing their energy usage.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Turkey">Turkey</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Energy_conservation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Turkey">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a> aims to decrease by at least 20% the amount of energy consumed per GDP of Turkey by the year 2023 (energy intensity).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TR1_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-TR1-38">[38]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Energy_conservation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Energy_conservation_in_the_United_States" title="Energy conservation in the United States">Energy conservation in the United States</a></div>
<p><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:USEnFlow02-quads.gif"><img alt="USEnFlow02-quads.gif" data-file-height="560" data-file-width="700" height="560" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/4/4e/USEnFlow02-quads.gif" width="700"/></a>
The United States is currently the second largest single consumer of energy, following China. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Department_of_Energy" title="U.S. Department of Energy">U.S. Department of Energy</a> categorizes national energy use in four broad sectors: transportation, residential, commercial, and industrial.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup>
</p><p>Energy usage in transportation and residential sectors, about half of U.S. energy consumption, is largely controlled by individual consumers. Commercial and industrial energy expenditures are determined by businesses entities and other facility managers. National energy policy has a significant effect on energy usage across all four sectors.
</p><p>Another aspect of energy conversation is using Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design. (LEED) This program is not mandatory, it is voluntary. This program has many categories, Energy and Atmosphere Prerequisite, applies to energy conservation. This section focuses on energy performance, renewable energy, energy performance, and many more. This program is designed to promote energy efficiency and be a green building, which is part of conservation. As mention above “energy conservation are efforts made to reduce the consumption of energy.”
