<p><i><b>Population and Development Review</b></i> is a quarterly <a href="/wiki/Peer_review" title="Peer review">peer-reviewed</a> <a href="/wiki/Academic_journal" title="Academic journal">academic journal</a> published by <a href="/wiki/Wiley-Blackwell" title="Wiley-Blackwell">Wiley-Blackwell</a> on behalf of the <a href="/wiki/Population_Council" title="Population Council">Population Council</a>. It was established in 1975 and its current <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Editors-in-chief" title="Editors-in-chief">editors-in-chief</a> are <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Geoffrey_McNicoll&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Geoffrey McNicoll (page does not exist)">Geoffrey McNicoll</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Landis_MacKellar&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Landis MacKellar (page does not exist)">Landis MacKellar</a>. The journal focuses on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Population_studies" title="Population studies">population studies</a>, the relationships between population and economic, <a href="/wiki/Environmental_change" title="Environmental change">environmental</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Social_change" title="Social change">social change</a>, and related thinking on <a href="/wiki/Public_policy" title="Public policy">public policy</a>. 
</p><p>According to the <i><a href="/wiki/Journal_Citation_Reports" title="Journal Citation Reports">Journal Citation Reports</a></i>, the journal has a 2016 <a href="/wiki/Impact_factor" title="Impact factor">impact factor</a> of 2.792, ranking it 3rd out of 26 journals in the category "Demography"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WoS1_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-WoS1-1">[1]</a></sup> and 7th out of 143 journals in the category "Sociology".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WoS2_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-WoS2-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
