<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">North American collegiate sustainability programs</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p class="mw-empty-elt">
</p><p><b>North American collegiate sustainability programs</b> are institutions of higher education in the United States, Mexico, and Canada that have majors and/or minors dedicated to the subject of <a href="/wiki/Sustainability" title="Sustainability">sustainability</a>. Sustainability as a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor%27s_degree" title="Bachelor's degree">major</a> and <a href="/wiki/Minor_(academic)" title="Minor (academic)">minor</a> is spreading to more and more colleges as the need for humanity to adopt a more sustainable lifestyle becomes increasingly apparent with the onset of <a href="/wiki/Global_warming" title="Global warming">global warming</a>. The majors and minors listed here cover a wide array of sustainability aspects from business to construction to agriculture to simply the study of sustainability itself.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Canada">Canada</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_American_collegiate_sustainability_programs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Canada">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Acadia_University">Acadia University</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_American_collegiate_sustainability_programs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Acadia University">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Acadia_University" title="Acadia University">Acadia University</a> is located in <a href="/wiki/Wolfville" title="Wolfville">Wolfville, Nova Scotia</a>, Canada and offers an <a href="/wiki/Undergraduate_education" title="Undergraduate education">undergraduate</a> major in Environmental and Sustainability Studies. This major gives students the skills they need to make the changes necessary to create a more "sustainable and just society".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> There are four concentration areas (Sustainable Community Development, Innovation and Entrepreneurship for Sustainability, Environmental Thought and Practice, and Environmental Advocacy, Education, and Activism) and two different degree options (a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Arts" title="Bachelor of Arts">Bachelor of Arts</a> and a Bachelor of Recreation Management) offered within the major. Additionally, the University is home to the Arthur Irving Academy for the Environment which organizes sustainability programs on the campus.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dalhousie_University">Dalhousie University</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_American_collegiate_sustainability_programs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Dalhousie University">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Dalhousie_University" title="Dalhousie University">Dalhousie University</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Halifax_Regional_Municipality" title="Halifax Regional Municipality">Halifax, Nova Scotia</a>, Canada offers two different majors, Environment, Sustainability, and Society (ESS) <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and Sustainable Resources and The Environment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Both majors incorporate different outlooks on sustainability. Sustainable Resource and The Environment focuses on managing environments, whereas ESS focuses on changing for the future. Along with these two majors, Dalhousie also has an Office of Sustainability <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> which offers a wide variety of programs focusing on becoming more sustainable that range from transportation to eating sustainably.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Trent_University">Trent University</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_American_collegiate_sustainability_programs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Trent University">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Trent_University" title="Trent University">Trent University</a>, located in <a href="/wiki/Peterborough,_Ontario" title="Peterborough, Ontario">Peterborough, Ontario</a>, Canada, offers an undergraduate degree in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sustainable_Agriculture" title="Sustainable Agriculture">Sustainable Agriculture and Food Systems</a> as well as a <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Arts" title="Master of Arts">Master of Arts</a> in Sustainability. The Sustainable Agriculture program encompasses the aspects of growing “healthy, sustainable, and affordable food <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>”. The Masters program delves into the <a href="/wiki/Sustainability#Definition" title="Sustainability">three pillars of sustainability</a> to prepare students to become leaders in sustainable endeavors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In addition, Trent has its own Office of Sustainability that leads several programs including <a href="/wiki/Compact_fluorescent_lamp" title="Compact fluorescent lamp">lighting retrofits</a> and the installation of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Green_roofs" title="Green roofs">green roofs</a> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="University_of_British_Columbia">University of British Columbia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_American_collegiate_sustainability_programs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: University of British Columbia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/University_of_British_Columbia" title="University of British Columbia">University of British Columbia</a> offers an Environment and Sustainability major at its <a href="/wiki/Vancouver" title="Vancouver">Vancouver</a> campus. This major promotes an understanding of the variety of factors that affect life on the planet now and in the future.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Some courses that are part of the major include The Global Climate System as well as Environment and Resources. The University of British Columbia also has the Centre for Interactive Research on Sustainability that focuses on creating and implementing solutions to the “challenges of urban development <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>”
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mexico">Mexico</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_American_collegiate_sustainability_programs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Mexico">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="Instituto_Tecnol.C3.B3gico_y_de_Estudios_Superiores_de_Monterrey_.28ITESM.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Instituto_Tecnológico_y_de_Estudios_Superiores_de_Monterrey_(ITESM)">Instituto Tecnológico y de Estudios Superiores de Monterrey (ITESM)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_American_collegiate_sustainability_programs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Instituto Tecnológico y de Estudios Superiores de Monterrey (ITESM)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Also known as <a href="/wiki/Monterrey_Institute_of_Technology_and_Higher_Education" title="Monterrey Institute of Technology and Higher Education">Technologico De Monterrey</a> (In English: Monterrey Institute of Technology), ITESM is based in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Monterrey,_Mexico" title="Monterrey, Mexico">Monterrey, Mexico</a> but actually has thirty-one campuses throughout the country. The institute is offers a major in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sustainable_Development" title="Sustainable Development">Sustainable Development Engineering</a>, which focuses on the efficient use of energy and water as well as the management of emissions and the relationship between the environment and society.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Some of the classes offered include <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Industrial_Ecology" title="Industrial Ecology">Industrial Ecology</a> and Sustainable Use of Water. The Sustainable Development Engineering major is offered at their <a href="/wiki/Monterrey_Institute_of_Technology_and_Higher_Education,_Mexico_City" title="Monterrey Institute of Technology and Higher Education, Mexico City">Mexico City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Monterrey_Institute_of_Technology_and_Higher_Education,_State_of_Mexico" title="Monterrey Institute of Technology and Higher Education, State of Mexico">State of Mexico</a>, <a href="/wiki/Monterrey_Institute_of_Technology_and_Higher_Education,_Monterrey" title="Monterrey Institute of Technology and Higher Education, Monterrey">Monterrey</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Tecnol%C3%B3gico_de_Monterrey,_Campus_Santa_Fe" title="Tecnológico de Monterrey, Campus Santa Fe">Santa Fe</a> campuses.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_American_collegiate_sustainability_programs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Northeast">Northeast</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_American_collegiate_sustainability_programs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Northeast">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Columbia_University">Columbia University</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_American_collegiate_sustainability_programs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Columbia University">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><a href="/wiki/Columbia_University" title="Columbia University">Columbia University</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> offers a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sustainable_Development" title="Sustainable Development">Sustainable Development</a> major that focuses on educating students on how to accomplish a sustainable society. Students will take classes from multiple clusters including Sustainable Development Foundation, Basic Disciplinary Foundation, Skills/Action, and Analysis and Solutions to Complex Problems.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The University has also developed a <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Science" title="Master of Science">Master of Science</a> in Sustainability Management <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> geared towards the role of sustainability in organizations. Columbia is also home to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Earth_Institute" title="Earth Institute">Earth Institute</a> whose mission is to bring people together to address and overcome some of the most pressing problems facing the Earth and humanity today. The institute also oversees sustainability initiatives for Columbia’s campus <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Drew_University">Drew University</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_American_collegiate_sustainability_programs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Drew University">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Located in <a href="/wiki/Madison,_New_Jersey" title="Madison, New Jersey">Madison, New Jersey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Drew_University" title="Drew University">Drew University</a> offers a major in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Environmental_Studies" title="Environmental Studies">Environmental Studies and Sustainability</a>. This major studies the current environmental problems facing the world today and provides the skills needed to overcome these problems. Students who take part in this academic program will have an experiential requirement that involves an environmental-related, hands-on experience <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup>
Drew University has numerous sustainability initiatives underway as well <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Philadelphia_University">Philadelphia University</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_American_collegiate_sustainability_programs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Philadelphia University">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Located in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Philadelphia,_Pennsylvania" title="Philadelphia, Pennsylvania">Philadelphia, Pennsylvania</a>, <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_University" title="Philadelphia University">Philadelphia University</a> has both a major and a minor in Environmental Sustainability that prepare students to serve as strategic leaders of environmental initiatives for corporations, communities, and organizations. The courses they offer are diverse and range from <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_architecture" title="Sustainable architecture">Sustainable Technologies for Architecture</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Economics" title="Economics">Economics of Sustainability</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> They also offer a <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Science" title="Master of Science">Master of Science</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sustainable_Design" title="Sustainable Design">Sustainable Design</a> focusing on green design and the development of net zero carbon buildings <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Unity_College">Unity College</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_American_collegiate_sustainability_programs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Unity College">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><a href="/wiki/Unity_College_(Maine)" title="Unity College (Maine)">Unity College</a> in <a href="/wiki/Unity,_Maine" title="Unity, Maine">Unity, Maine</a> has not one, but two sustainability majors. Their Sustainable Energy Management major is both a technical and conceptual major designed to educate students in building sustainable structures that focus on <a href="/wiki/Renewable_energy" title="Renewable energy">renewable energy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> On the other hand, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sustainable_Agriculture" title="Sustainable Agriculture">Sustainable Agriculture</a> major they offer <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> concentrates on teaching sustainable agricultural practices that emphasize local farming and activism when it comes to governmental food policies. As an environmental college, there is a lot of focus on sustainability initiatives at Unity College. In fact, they’ve employed a full-time sustainability coordinator whose job is to make Unity College as environmentally friendly as possible.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Wilson_College">Wilson College</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_American_collegiate_sustainability_programs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Wilson College">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Located in <a href="/wiki/Chambersburg,_Pennsylvania" title="Chambersburg, Pennsylvania">Chambersburg, Pennsylvania</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wilson_College_(Pennsylvania)" title="Wilson College (Pennsylvania)">Wilson College</a> is a women’s college that offers two sustainability majors. <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_business" title="Sustainable business">Business Sustainability and Environmental Management</a> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> integrates sustainability practices into the business world. Environmental Sustainability <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> focuses more on solving environmental problems. Wilson has a Center for Sustainable Living that works with the Fulton Center for Sustainable Living (which serves as an environmental educational facility as well as a produce farm) <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Southeast">Southeast</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_American_collegiate_sustainability_programs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Southeast">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Eastern_Mennonite_University">Eastern Mennonite University</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_American_collegiate_sustainability_programs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Eastern Mennonite University">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><a href="/wiki/Eastern_Mennonite_University" title="Eastern Mennonite University">Eastern Mennonite University</a> in <a href="/wiki/Harrisonburg,_Virginia" title="Harrisonburg, Virginia">Harrisonburg, Virginia</a> offers a major in Environmental Sustainability.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> This program focuses on forward-looking sustainability practices and requires students to choose one of two areas of study: Environmental Science Concentration (focuses on traditional environmental sciences) or Environmental and Social Sustainability Concentration (combines concepts from the first concentration and incorporates sociological aspects). Besides the major, Eastern Mennonite University has integrated some sustainable initiatives around campus, including the largest solar insulation in the state of Virginia and a dormitory that is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LEED_certified" title="LEED certified">LEED certified</a> at the gold level.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Furman_University">Furman University</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_American_collegiate_sustainability_programs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Furman University">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><a href="/wiki/Furman_University" title="Furman University">Furman University</a> in <a href="/wiki/Greenville,_South_Carolina" title="Greenville, South Carolina">Greenville, South Carolina</a> offers a major in <a href="/wiki/Sustainability_science" title="Sustainability science">Sustainability science</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> The focus of this major is to prepare and educate students on how to enhance the long-term quality of life. This program offers nearly 30 different elective courses along with the required coursework. All of the elective courses are broken into three different categories: Environmental Security, Human Security, and Sustainable Production and Consumption. Furman University also has the Sustainability Planning Council (SPC) <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> and the David E. Shi Center of Sustainability,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> which both focus on promoting sustainability throughout the campus.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="University_of_Florida">University of Florida</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_American_collegiate_sustainability_programs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: University of Florida">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/University_of_Florida" title="University of Florida">University of Florida</a> in <a href="/wiki/Gainesville,_Florida" title="Gainesville, Florida">Gainesville, Florida</a>, offers three different types of majors and minors that incorporate sustainability: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sustainability_Studies" title="Sustainability Studies">Sustainability Studies</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> Sustainability and the Built Environment,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> and Sustainable Crop Production and Management.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> Each major or minor provides a diverse look into sustainability topics, while offering an array of courses from Agriculture and Environmental Quality to Organic &amp; Sustainable Crop Production and from Sustainable <a href="/wiki/Ecotourism" title="Ecotourism">Ecotourism</a> Development to Social and Cultural Sustainability. These majors/minors focus on gaining the knowledge and skill sets to effectively contribute to the growth of societies of current and future generations. In addition to the range of academic programs, University of Florida has an Office of Sustainability that has created nine different sustainability programs for the University of Florida including the One Less Car and Tailgator Game Day Recycling programs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup>
</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Aquinas_College">Aquinas College</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_American_collegiate_sustainability_programs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Aquinas College">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><a href="/wiki/Aquinas_College_(Michigan)" title="Aquinas College (Michigan)">Aquinas College</a>, located in <a href="/wiki/Grand_Rapids,_Michigan" title="Grand Rapids, Michigan">Grand Rapids, Michigan</a>, is one of the first to offer a major and minor in <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_business" title="Sustainable business">Sustainable Business</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> Recognizing that businesses have a huge impact on the environment, Aquinas’ academic program seeks to educate students in sustainable business practices that not only promote profitability, but environmental and societal well being too. Courses for the major and minor include Sustainable Business Management and Building <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Social_Capital" title="Social Capital">Social Capital</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Baldwin_Wallace_University">Baldwin Wallace University</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_American_collegiate_sustainability_programs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Baldwin Wallace University">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Located in <a href="/wiki/Berea,_Ohio" title="Berea, Ohio">Berea, Ohio</a>, <a href="/wiki/Baldwin_Wallace_University" title="Baldwin Wallace University">Baldwin Wallace University</a> offers a major and minor in Sustainability.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> This academic program focuses on preparing students to create solutions to the world’s current environmental and social problems through courses such as Sustainable Food and <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_business" title="Sustainable business">Green Business</a>. In addition to their undergraduate program, Baldwin Wallace University also has a <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Business_Administration" title="Master of Business Administration">Master of Business Administration</a> in Sustainability.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> Besides taking steps to reduce the campus’ environmental footprint, Baldwin Wallace also holds a yearly Sustainability <a href="/wiki/Academic_conference" title="Academic conference">Symposium</a> that is open to the public <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Maharishi_University_of_Management">Maharishi University of Management</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_American_collegiate_sustainability_programs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Maharishi University of Management">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Maharishi_University_of_Management" title="Maharishi University of Management">Maharishi University of Management</a> found in <a href="/wiki/Fairfield,_Iowa" title="Fairfield, Iowa">Fairfield, Iowa</a>, began its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sustainable_Living" title="Sustainable Living">Sustainable Living</a> degree program back in 2003, making it a leader in its field. Its <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Science" title="Bachelor of Science">Bachelor of Science</a> and minor in Sustainable Living both focus on <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_community" title="Sustainable community">sustainable community</a> development.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> Other than implementing hands-on projects and internship opportunities, this program operates on the <a href="/wiki/Maharishi_University_of_Management#Block_system_and_academic_day" title="Maharishi University of Management">block system</a> (students focus only on one course per month) and concentrates on the <a href="/wiki/Maharishi_University_of_Management#development_of_consciousness" title="Maharishi University of Management">development of consciousness</a>. The University is currently in the process of building a Sustainable Living Center that, once finished, will not only surpass the highest <a href="/wiki/Leadership_in_Energy_and_Environmental_Design" title="Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design">LEED</a> standard for a building (platinum) but comply with the <a href="/wiki/Living_Building_Challenge" title="Living Building Challenge">Living Building Challenge</a> as well.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Minneapolis_College_of_Art_and_Design">Minneapolis College of Art and Design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_American_collegiate_sustainability_programs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Minneapolis College of Art and Design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The <a class="external text" href="https://mcad.edu/" rel="nofollow">Minneapolis College of Art and Design</a> (MCAD), located in <a href="/wiki/Minneapolis" title="Minneapolis">Minneapolis, Minnesota</a>, offers a fully online <a class="external text" href="https://mcad.edu/academic-programs/sustainable-design" rel="nofollow">Master of Arts in Sustainable Design Program</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup>. MCAD was the first art and design school with a fully online program in sustainability. The College and Program are fully accredited by both the Higher Learning Commission (HLC) and the National Association of Schools of Art + Design (NASAD). The program covers cutting-edge theories to practical applications and leadership strategies, and emphasizes systems thinking, biomimicry, business, lifecycle analysis, and collaboration. Every student completes an independent thesis project and presentation. Courses are taught by practicing design and sustainability professionals.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Northland_College">Northland College</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_American_collegiate_sustainability_programs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: Northland College">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><a href="/wiki/Ashland,_Wisconsin" title="Ashland, Wisconsin">Ashland, Wisconsin</a> is the site of <a href="/wiki/Northland_College_(Wisconsin)" title="Northland College (Wisconsin)">Northland College</a> that offers both a major and a minor in Sustainable Community Development.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> Both programs are geared toward students who wish to be agents of change for communities in the way of sustainability. Courses include Methods of Sustainable Community Development and Capitalism, Justice, and Sustainability. Northland is one of five colleges that are part of the <a href="/wiki/Eco_League" title="Eco League">Eco League</a> (a group of colleges dedicated to the pursuit of sustainability education) and offers a campus sustainability work study program <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> to its students among other things.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="University_of_Michigan">University of Michigan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_American_collegiate_sustainability_programs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: University of Michigan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/University_of_Michigan" title="University of Michigan">University of Michigan</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ann_Arbor,_Michigan" title="Ann Arbor, Michigan">Ann Arbor, Michigan</a> offers both a major and minor in Sustainable Systems.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> These programs focus on how to enhance systems so that they’re more sustainable using technology and enterprise. Students can choose from over forty different courses in three different clusters: Systems Analysis for Sustainability, Sustainable Design and Technology, and Sustainable Enterprise that cover a broad range of themes (including the built environment, mobility, and food systems). Additionally, the University of Michigan has an Office of Campus Sustainability <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> and includes the Graham Environmental Sustainability Institute.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Southwest">Southwest</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_American_collegiate_sustainability_programs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=27" title="Edit section: Southwest">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Arizona_State_University">Arizona State University</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_American_collegiate_sustainability_programs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=28" title="Edit section: Arizona State University">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/ASU_School_of_Sustainability" title="ASU School of Sustainability">ASU School of Sustainability</a> at <a href="/wiki/Arizona_State_University" title="Arizona State University">Arizona State University</a> in <a href="/wiki/Tempe,_Arizona" title="Tempe, Arizona">Tempe, Arizona</a> offers the BA, BS, MA, MS, and PhD in Sustainability.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> Other degrees offered are the Master of Sustainable Solutions (applied non-thesis program) and the Executive Master in Sustainability Leadership (13-month executive degree). Through the university's online platform, students may also pursue a BA in Sustainability and a Master of Sustainability Leadership degree. It is the first School of Sustainability in the United States, founded in 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> Several sustainability programs are found in other colleges at ASU, including Business, Engineering, Public Service and Community Solutions.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Prescott_College">Prescott College</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_American_collegiate_sustainability_programs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=29" title="Edit section: Prescott College">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><a href="/wiki/Prescott_College" title="Prescott College">Prescott College</a> has offered a PhD degree in Sustainability Education using a cohort-based limited-residency model since 2005. Doctoral students complete most of the work offsite in three stages. Foundational coursework in pedagogy, sustainability science, and social science research methods are completed via virtual classroom space.  Elective courses are designed by the students and overseen by degreed mentors found by the students. The research work and writing are traditional, with a doctoral committee made up of a chair and two other members, plus an external reader. Each cohort of doctoral students also meets onsite for a week twice per year. The program admits between ten and twenty students per year approximately. Doctoral student work, including dissertation proposals, is highlighted at a Sustainability Symposium held on campus every April.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup>
</p>
