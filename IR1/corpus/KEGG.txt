<p><b>KEGG</b> (<b>Kyoto Encyclopedia of Genes and Genomes</b>) is a collection of databases dealing with <a href="/wiki/Genome" title="Genome">genomes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Biological_pathway" title="Biological pathway">biological pathways</a>, <a href="/wiki/Disease" title="Disease">diseases</a>, <a href="/wiki/Drug" title="Drug">drugs</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Chemical_substance" title="Chemical substance">chemical substances</a>. KEGG is utilized for <a href="/wiki/Bioinformatics" title="Bioinformatics">bioinformatics</a> research and education, including data analysis in <a href="/wiki/Genomics" title="Genomics">genomics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Metagenomics" title="Metagenomics">metagenomics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Metabolomics" title="Metabolomics">metabolomics</a> and other <a href="/wiki/Omics" title="Omics">omics</a> studies, modeling and simulation in <a href="/wiki/Systems_biology" title="Systems biology">systems biology</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Translational_research" title="Translational research">translational research</a> in <a href="/wiki/Drug_development" title="Drug development">drug development</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Introduction">Introduction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=KEGG&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Introduction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The KEGG database project was initiated in 1995 by <a href="/wiki/Minoru_Kanehisa" title="Minoru Kanehisa">Minoru Kanehisa</a>, Professor at the Institute for Chemical Research, <a href="/wiki/Kyoto_University" title="Kyoto University">Kyoto University</a>, under the then ongoing Japanese <a href="/wiki/Human_Genome_Project" title="Human Genome Project">Human Genome Program</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid10592173_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid10592173-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid9287494_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid9287494-2">[2]</a></sup> Foreseeing the need for a computerized resource that can be used for biological interpretation of <a href="/wiki/Genome_project" title="Genome project">genome sequence data</a>, he started developing the KEGG PATHWAY database. It is a collection of manually drawn KEGG pathway maps representing experimental knowledge on <a href="/wiki/Metabolism" title="Metabolism">metabolism</a> and various other functions of the <a href="/wiki/Cell_(biology)" title="Cell (biology)">cell</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Organism" title="Organism">organism</a>. Each pathway map contains a network of molecular interactions and reactions and is designed to link <a href="/wiki/Gene" title="Gene">genes</a> in the genome to <a href="/wiki/Gene_product" title="Gene product">gene products</a> (mostly <a href="/wiki/Protein" title="Protein">proteins</a>) in the pathway. This has enabled the analysis called KEGG pathway mapping, whereby the gene content in the genome is compared with the KEGG PATHWAY database to examine which pathways and associated functions are likely to be encoded in the genome.
</p><p>According to the developers, KEGG is a "computer representation" of the <a href="/wiki/Biological_system" title="Biological system">biological system</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid16381885_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid16381885-3">[3]</a></sup> It integrates building blocks and wiring diagrams of the system — more specifically, genetic building blocks of genes and proteins, chemical building blocks of <a href="/wiki/Small_molecule" title="Small molecule">small molecules</a> and reactions, and wiring diagrams of molecular interaction and reaction networks. This concept is realized in the following databases of KEGG, which are categorized into systems, genomic, chemical, and health information.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid24214961_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid24214961-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>Systems information
<ul><li>PATHWAY — <a href="/wiki/Biological_pathway" title="Biological pathway">pathway</a> maps for cellular and organismal functions</li>
<li>MODULE — modules or functional units of genes</li>
<li>BRITE — hierarchical classifications of biological entities</li></ul></li>
<li>Genomic information
<ul><li>GENOME — complete <a href="/wiki/Genome" title="Genome">genomes</a></li>
<li>GENES — <a href="/wiki/Gene" title="Gene">genes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Protein" title="Protein">proteins</a> in the complete genomes</li>
<li>ORTHOLOGY — <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ortholog" title="Ortholog">ortholog</a> groups of genes in the complete genomes</li></ul></li>
<li>Chemical information
<ul><li>COMPOUND, GLYCAN — <a href="/wiki/Chemical_compound" title="Chemical compound">chemical compounds</a> and <a href="/wiki/Glycan" title="Glycan">glycans</a></li>
<li>REACTION, RPAIR, RCLASS — <a href="/wiki/Chemical_reaction" title="Chemical reaction">chemical reactions</a></li>
<li>ENZYME — <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Enzyme_nomenclature" title="Enzyme nomenclature">enzyme nomenclature</a></li></ul></li>
<li>Health information
<ul><li>DISEASE — human <a href="/wiki/Disease" title="Disease">diseases</a></li>
<li>DRUG — <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Approved_drugs" title="Approved drugs">approved drugs</a></li>
<li>ENVIRON — <a href="/wiki/Crude_drug" title="Crude drug">crude drugs</a> and health-related substances</li></ul></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Databases">Databases</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=KEGG&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Databases">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Systems_information">Systems information</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=KEGG&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Systems information">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The KEGG PATHWAY database, the wiring diagram database, is the core of the KEGG resource. It is a collection of pathway maps integrating many entities including genes, proteins, RNAs, chemical compounds, glycans, and chemical reactions, as well as disease genes and drug targets, which are stored as individual entries in the other databases of KEGG. The pathway maps are classified into the following sections:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Metabolism" title="Metabolism">Metabolism</a></li>
<li>Genetic information processing (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Transcription_(genetics)" title="Transcription (genetics)">transcription</a>, <a href="/wiki/Translation_(biology)" title="Translation (biology)">translation</a>, <a href="/wiki/DNA_replication" title="DNA replication">replication</a> and <a href="/wiki/DNA_repair" title="DNA repair">repair</a>, etc.)</li>
<li>Environmental information processing (<a href="/wiki/Membrane_transport" title="Membrane transport">membrane transport</a>, <a href="/wiki/Signal_transduction" title="Signal transduction">signal transduction</a>, etc.)</li>
<li>Cellular processes (<a href="/wiki/Cell_growth" title="Cell growth">cell growth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cell_death" title="Cell death">cell death</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cell_membrane" title="Cell membrane">cell membrane</a> functions, etc.)</li>
<li>Organismal systems (<a href="/wiki/Immune_system" title="Immune system">immune system</a>, <a href="/wiki/Endocrine_system" title="Endocrine system">endocrine system</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nervous_system" title="Nervous system">nervous system</a>, etc.)</li>
<li>Human <a href="/wiki/Disease" title="Disease">diseases</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Drug_development" title="Drug development">Drug development</a></li></ul>
<p>The metabolism section contains aesthetically drawn global maps showing an overall picture of metabolism, in addition to regular metabolic pathway maps. The low-resolution global maps can be used, for example, to compare metabolic capacities of different organisms in genomics studies and different environmental samples in metagenomics studies. In contrast, KEGG modules in the KEGG MODULE database are higher-resolution, localized wiring diagrams, representing tighter functional units within a pathway map, such as subpathways conserved among specific organism groups and molecular complexes. KEGG modules are defined as characteristic gene sets that can be linked to specific metabolic capacities and other <a href="/wiki/Phenotype" title="Phenotype">phenotypic</a> features, so that they can be used for automatic interpretation of genome and metagenome data.
</p><p>Another database that supplements KEGG PATHWAY is the KEGG BRITE database. It is an <a href="/wiki/Ontology_(information_science)" title="Ontology (information science)">ontology</a> database containing hierarchical classifications of various entities including genes, proteins, organisms, diseases, drugs, and chemical compounds. While KEGG PATHWAY is limited to molecular interactions and reactions of these entities, KEGG BRITE incorporates many different types of relationships.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Genomic_information">Genomic information</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=KEGG&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Genomic information">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Several months after the KEGG project was initiated in 1995, the first report of the completely sequenced <a href="/wiki/Bacteria" title="Bacteria">bacterial</a> genome was published.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid7542800_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid7542800-5">[5]</a></sup> Since then all published complete genomes are accumulated in KEGG for both <a href="/wiki/Eukaryote" title="Eukaryote">eukaryotes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Prokaryote" title="Prokaryote">prokaryotes</a>. The KEGG GENES database contains gene/protein-level information and the KEGG GENOME database contains organism-level information for these genomes. The KEGG GENES database consists of gene sets for the complete genomes, and genes in each set are given <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Genome_annotation" title="Genome annotation">annotations</a> in the form of establishing correspondences to the wiring diagrams of KEGG pathway maps, KEGG modules, and BRITE hierarchies.
</p><p>These correspondences are made using the concept of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ortholog" title="Ortholog">orthologs</a>. The KEGG pathway maps are drawn based on experimental evidence in specific organisms but they are designed to be applicable to other organisms as well, because different organisms, such as human and mouse, often share identical pathways consisting of functionally identical genes, called orthologous genes or orthologs. All the genes in the KEGG GENES database are being grouped into such orthologs in the KEGG ORTHOLOGY (KO) database. Because the nodes (gene products) of KEGG pathway maps, as well as KEGG modules and BRITE hierarchies, are given KO identifiers, the correspondences are established once genes in the genome are annotated with KO identifiers by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Genome_annotation" title="Genome annotation">genome annotation</a> procedure in KEGG.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid24214961_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-pmid24214961-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Chemical_information">Chemical information</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=KEGG&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Chemical information">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The KEGG metabolic pathway maps are drawn to represent the dual aspects of the metabolic network: the genomic network of how genome-encoded <a href="/wiki/Enzyme" title="Enzyme">enzymes</a> are connected to catalyze consecutive reactions and the chemical network of how chemical structures of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Enzyme_substrate_(biology)" title="Enzyme substrate (biology)">substrates</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Product_(biology)" title="Product (biology)">products</a> are transformed by these reactions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid23816707_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid23816707-6">[6]</a></sup> A set of enzyme genes in the genome will identify enzyme relation networks when superimposed on the KEGG pathway maps, which in turn characterize chemical structure transformation networks allowing interpretation of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Biosynthetic" title="Biosynthetic">biosynthetic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Biodegradation" title="Biodegradation">biodegradation</a> potentials of the organism. Alternatively, a set of <a href="/wiki/Metabolite" title="Metabolite">metabolites</a> identified in the metabolome will lead to the understanding of enzymatic pathways and enzyme genes involved.
</p><p>The databases in the chemical information category, which are collectively called KEGG LIGAND, are organized by capturing knowledge of the chemical network. In the beginning of the KEGG project, KEGG LIGAND consisted of three databases: KEGG COMPOUND for chemical compounds, KEGG REACTION for chemical reactions, and KEGG ENZYME for reactions in the enzyme nomenclature.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid9847234_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid9847234-7">[7]</a></sup> Currently, there are additional databases: KEGG GLYCAN for glycans<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid16014746_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid16014746-8">[8]</a></sup> and two auxiliary reaction databases called RPAIR (reactant pair alignments) and RCLASS (reaction class).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid23384306_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid23384306-9">[9]</a></sup> KEGG COMPOUND has also been expanded to contain various compounds such as <a href="/wiki/Xenobiotic" title="Xenobiotic">xenobiotics</a>, in addition to metabolites.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Health_information">Health information</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=KEGG&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Health information">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In KEGG, diseases are viewed as perturbed states of the biological system caused by perturbants of genetic factors and environmental factors, and drugs are viewed as different types of perturbants.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid19880382_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid19880382-10">[10]</a></sup> The KEGG PATHWAY database includes not only the normal states but also the perturbed states of the biological systems. However, disease pathway maps cannot be drawn for most diseases because molecular mechanisms are not well understood. An alternative approach is taken in the KEGG DISEASE database, which simply catalogs known genetic factors and environmental factors of diseases. These catalogs may eventually lead to more complete wiring diagrams of diseases.
</p><p>The KEGG DRUG database contains <a href="/wiki/Active_ingredient" title="Active ingredient">active ingredients</a> of <a href="/wiki/Approved_drug" title="Approved drug">approved drugs</a> in Japan, the USA, and Europe. They are distinguished by chemical structures and/or chemical components and associated with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drug_target" title="Drug target">target</a> molecules, <a href="/wiki/Drug_metabolism" title="Drug metabolism">metabolizing enzymes</a>, and other molecular interaction network information in the KEGG pathway maps and the BRITE hierarchies. This enables an integrated analysis of drug interactions with genomic information. <a href="/wiki/Crude_drug" title="Crude drug">Crude drugs</a> and other health-related substances, which are outside the category of approved drugs, are stored in the KEGG ENVIRON database. The databases in the health information category are collectively called KEGG MEDICUS, which also includes <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Package_insert" title="Package insert">package inserts</a> of all marketed drugs in Japan.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Subscription_model">Subscription model</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=KEGG&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Subscription model">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In July 2011 KEGG introduced a subscription model for FTP download due to a significant cutback of government funding. KEGG continues to be freely available through its website, but the subscription model has raised discussions about sustainability of bioinformatics databases.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid22144685_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid22144685-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NatureNews_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-NatureNews-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p>
