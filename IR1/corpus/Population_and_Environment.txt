<p><i><b>Population and Environment</b></i> is a <a href="/wiki/Peer_review" title="Peer review">peer-reviewed</a> <a href="/wiki/Academic_journal" title="Academic journal">academic journal</a> covering research on the bi-directional links between population, natural resources, and the natural environment. The <a href="/wiki/Editor-in-chief" title="Editor-in-chief">editor-in-chief</a> is Dr Elizabeth Fussell,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> associate professor of population studies and environment and society at <a href="/wiki/Brown_University" title="Brown University">Brown University</a>. Former editors-in-chief of the journal include prominent <a href="/wiki/Racialism" title="Racialism">racialists</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Virginia_Abernethy" title="Virginia Abernethy">Virginia Abernethy</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kevin_B._MacDonald" title="Kevin B. MacDonald">Kevin B. MacDonald</a>; racialists appear to have since lost control of the journal since MacDonald's term as editor ended in 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
