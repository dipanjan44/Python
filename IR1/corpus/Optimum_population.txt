<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Optimum population</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Optimum population</b> refers to the size of a <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> that produces the best results according to chosen end targets.  One text from 1926 presented a single end target as being "...the largest per capita income of consumers' goods possible under the given conditions".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Journal_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Journal-1">[1]</a></sup>
Other potential end targets in favour of lower levels of population are cited, including: long term <a href="/wiki/Sustainability" title="Sustainability">sustainability</a>, efficient operation of <a href="/wiki/Democracy" title="Democracy">democracy</a>, the preservation of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Personal_freedom" title="Personal freedom">personal freedom</a> and the preservation of <a href="/wiki/Biodiversity" title="Biodiversity">biodiversity</a> while potential end targets in favour of higher levels of population are cited, including the abilities to preserve and foster <a href="/wiki/Cultural_diversity" title="Cultural diversity">cultural diversity</a>, to stimulate intellectual, artistic, and technological creativity and to facilitate social infrastructure.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Optimum_population&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r856303468">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>Excessive growth may reduce output per worker, repress levels of living for the masses and engender strife.</p><div class="templatequotecite">— <cite><a href="/wiki/Confucius" title="Confucius">Confucius</a> 551 – 479 BCE<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></cite></div>
</blockquote>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r856303468" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>The concept of an optimum, or ideal, size of population concerns both theory and policy.  Theoretically, there is for any given state of the arts and any given supply of available natural resources, together with a given supply of capital instruments and a given social organization, a certain size of population which can operate these resources to the best advantage and produce the largest per capita income of consumers' goods possible under the given conditions.</p><div class="templatequotecite">— <cite><i><a href="/wiki/Journal_of_Political_Economy" title="Journal of Political Economy">Journal of Political Economy</a></i> Vol. 37, No. 1, Feb., 1929, page 87.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Journal_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Journal-1">[1]</a></sup></cite></div>
</blockquote>
<p>Regarding the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Human_population" title="Human population">human population</a>, end-targets for an optimum population include ecological <a href="/wiki/Sustainability" title="Sustainability">sustainability</a>, <a href="/wiki/Output_(economics)" title="Output (economics)">economic output</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Intrinsic_value_(ethics)" title="Intrinsic value (ethics)">philosophical or ethical ends-in-themselves</a>.
</p><p>Some commentators disagree with the concept of "optimum population", believing that the human population will always, in the long-term, be able to adapt to the requirements of a larger population.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nyt19980212_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-nyt19980212-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>Any conception of an optimum population level must lie between a <a href="/wiki/Minimum_viable_population" title="Minimum viable population">minimum viable population</a> of the human species and the maximum level of population that can be sustained by the <a href="/wiki/Carrying_capacity" title="Carrying capacity">carrying capacity</a> of the planet <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Estimations">Estimations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Optimum_population&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Estimations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Various end-targets are often balanced together in estimating the optimum human population, and different emphasis on different end-targets cause variability among estimates.
</p><p>The optimal <a href="/wiki/World_population" title="World population">world population</a> has been estimated by a team co-authored by <a href="/wiki/Paul_R._Ehrlich" title="Paul R. Ehrlich">Paul R. Ehrlich</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ehrlichetal1994_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ehrlichetal1994-6">[6]</a></sup> End-targets in this estimation included:
</p>
<ul><li>Decent wealth and resources to everyone</li>
<li>Basic <a href="/wiki/Human_rights" title="Human rights">human rights</a> to everyone</li>
<li>Preservation of <a href="/wiki/Cultural_diversity" title="Cultural diversity">cultural diversity</a></li>
<li>Allowance of intellectual, artistic, and technological creativity</li>
<li>Preservation of <a href="/wiki/Biodiversity" title="Biodiversity">biodiversity</a></li></ul>
<p>Based on this, the estimation of optimum population was to be roughly around 1.5 billion to 2 billion people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ehrlichetal1994_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Ehrlichetal1994-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
