<p><b>Tellurium dioxide</b> (TeO<sub>2</sub>) is a solid <a href="/wiki/Oxide" title="Oxide">oxide</a> of <a href="/wiki/Tellurium" title="Tellurium">tellurium</a>. It is encountered in two different forms, the yellow orthorhombic mineral <a href="/wiki/Tellurite" title="Tellurite">tellurite</a>, β-TeO<sub>2</sub>, and the synthetic, colourless tetragonal (paratellurite), α-TeO<sub>2</sub>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Greenwood_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Greenwood-2">[2]</a></sup>  Most of the information regarding reaction chemistry has been obtained in studies involving paratellurite,  α-TeO<sub>2</sub>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-McWhinnie_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-McWhinnie-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparation">Preparation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tellurium_dioxide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Preparation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Paratellurite, α-TeO<sub>2</sub>, is produced by reacting tellurium with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dioxygen" title="Dioxygen">O<sub>2</sub></a>:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Greenwood_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Greenwood-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<dl><dd>Te + O<sub>2</sub> → TeO<sub>2</sub></dd></dl>
<p>An alternative preparation is to dehydrate tellurous acid, H<sub>2</sub>TeO<sub>3</sub>, or to thermally decompose <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Basic_tellurium_nitrate&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Basic tellurium nitrate (page does not exist)">basic tellurium nitrate</a>,Te<sub>2</sub>O<sub>4</sub>.HNO<sub>3</sub> above 400 °C.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Greenwood_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Greenwood-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Physical_properties">Physical properties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tellurium_dioxide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Physical properties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The speed of sound in Tellurium dioxide is 4250 m/s.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs a reliable source; TeO2 is anisotropic, and has varying speeds of sound in different directions. (November 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Chemical_properties">Chemical properties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tellurium_dioxide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Chemical properties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>TeO<sub>2</sub> is barely <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soluble" title="Soluble">soluble</a> in <a href="/wiki/Water" title="Water">water</a> and soluble in <a href="/wiki/Acid" title="Acid">strong acids</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alkali_metal_hydroxide" title="Alkali metal hydroxide">alkali metal hydroxides</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> It is an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amphoteric" title="Amphoteric">amphoteric</a> substance and therefore can act both as an acid or as a base depending on the solution it is in.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> It reacts with acids to make tellurium salts and bases to make <a href="/wiki/Tellurite_(ion)" title="Tellurite (ion)">tellurites</a>. It can be oxidized to <a href="/wiki/Telluric_acid" title="Telluric acid">telluric acid</a> or <a href="/wiki/Tellurate" title="Tellurate">tellurates</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Structure">Structure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tellurium_dioxide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Structure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Paratellurite, α-TeO<sub>2</sub>, converts at high pressure into the β-, tellurite form.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wells_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Wells-6">[6]</a></sup> Both the α-, (paratellurite) and β- (tellurite forms) contain four coordinate Te with the oxygen atoms at four of the corners of a trigonal bipyramid. In paratellurite all vertices are shared to give a <a href="/wiki/Titanium_dioxide" title="Titanium dioxide">rutile</a>-like structure, where the O-Te-O bond angle are 140°. α-TeO<sub>2</sub> In tellurite pairs of trigonal pyramidal, TeO<sub>4</sub> units, sharing an edge, share vertices to then form a layer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wells_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Wells-6">[6]</a></sup> The shortest Te-Te distance in tellurite is 317 pm, compared to 374 pm in paratellurite.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wells_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-Wells-6">[6]</a></sup> Similar Te<sub>2</sub>O<sub>6</sub> units are found in the mineral <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Denningite&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Denningite (page does not exist)">denningite</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wells_6-3"><a href="#cite_note-Wells-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p><span class="chemf nowrap">TeO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> melts at 732.6 °C, forming a red liquid.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wiberg_holleman_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-wiberg_holleman-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Uses">Uses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tellurium_dioxide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Uses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It is used as an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Acousto-optic" title="Acousto-optic">acousto-optic</a> material.
</p><p>Tellurium dioxide is also a conditional glass former, which means it will form a glass with small molar% additions of a second compound such as an oxide or halide. TeO<sub>2</sub> glasses have high <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Refractive_indices" title="Refractive indices">refractive indices</a> and transmit into the mid-<a href="/wiki/Infrared" title="Infrared">infrared</a> part of the <a href="/wiki/Electromagnetic_spectrum" title="Electromagnetic spectrum">electromagnetic spectrum</a>, therefore they are of technological interest for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Optical_waveguide" title="Optical waveguide">optical waveguides</a>. Tellurite glasses have also been shown to exhibit <a href="/wiki/Raman_amplification" title="Raman amplification">Raman gain</a> up to 30 times that of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Silica" title="Silica">silica</a>, useful in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Optical_fibre" title="Optical fibre">optical fibre</a> amplification.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Safety">Safety</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tellurium_dioxide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Safety">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>TeO<sub>2</sub> is a possible <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Teratogen" title="Teratogen">teratogen</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p><p>Exposure to tellurium compounds produces a <a href="/wiki/Garlic" title="Garlic">garlic</a>-like odour on the breath, caused by the formation of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ethyl_telluride&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ethyl telluride (page does not exist)">ethyl telluride</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p>
