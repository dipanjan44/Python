<p>The <b>Population Research Institute</b> (<b>PRI</b>) is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Non-profit_organization" title="Non-profit organization">non-profit organization</a> based in <a href="/wiki/Front_Royal,_Virginia" title="Front Royal, Virginia">Front Royal, Virginia</a>, USA.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> PRI describes itself as "a non-profit research group whose goals are to expose the myth of <a href="/wiki/Human_overpopulation" title="Human overpopulation">overpopulation</a>" as well as "human rights abuses committed in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Human_population_control" title="Human population control">population control</a> programs, and to make the case that people are the world's greatest resource."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> It operates programs against the advancement of contraception, sterilization, and abortion. PRI is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/501(c)(3)" title="501(c)(3)">501(c)(3)</a> organization and claims contacts to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pro-life" title="Pro-life">pro-life</a> groups in over 30 countries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Results are being released in an own online periodical, <i>PRI Review</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Population_Research_Institute&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Population Research Institute was founded in 1989 by Paul Marx (1920–2010), a family sociologist, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Catholic_priest" title="Catholic priest">Catholic priest</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Benedictine_monk" title="Benedictine monk">Benedictine monk</a> who had established <a href="/wiki/Human_Life_International" title="Human Life International">Human Life International</a> as well.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> PRI became an independent institute in 1996.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The same year, the think tank got headed by <a href="/wiki/Steven_W._Mosher" title="Steven W. Mosher">Steven W. Mosher</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> an anthropologist and author who studied and discovered excesses in <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> during the beginning of the <a href="/wiki/One-child_policy" title="One-child policy">one-child policy</a> in the late 1970s and the early 1980s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> PRI opposes such practices and asserts a causal link between the one-child policy on the one hand and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/China%27s_imbalanced_sex-ratio" title="China's imbalanced sex-ratio">China's imbalanced sex-ratio</a> as well as its aging population on the other hand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Family_Care_Center">Family Care Center</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Population_Research_Institute&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Family Care Center">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As of 2015 PRI operates a facility called Family Care Center, a family welfare and <a href="/wiki/Crisis_pregnancy_center" title="Crisis pregnancy center">crisis pregnancy center</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Saint_Lucia" title="Saint Lucia">Saint Lucia</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> the home country of its spiritual director, Father Linus Clovis.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Defunding_population_control_programs">Defunding population control programs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Population_Research_Institute&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Defunding population control programs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Times" title="Los Angeles Times">Los Angeles Times</a>, PRI successfully lobbied the <a href="/wiki/George_W._Bush" title="George W. Bush">George W. Bush</a> administration to withhold <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US$" title="US$">US$</a>34 to $40 million per year for seven years from the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Population_Fund" title="United Nations Population Fund">United Nations Population Fund</a> (UNFPA), the largest international donor to family planning programs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> This turn had been demanded by a 2002 appeal of about 140 groups the think tank had led.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> The PRI says that its mission includes to "[e]xpose the relentless promotion of abortion, abortifacient contraception, and chemical and surgical sterilization in misleadingly labeled '<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Population_stabilization" title="Population stabilization">population stabilization</a>,' 'family planning,' and '<a href="/wiki/Reproductive_health" title="Reproductive health">reproductive health</a>' programs".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> One of the institute's drives calls to end <a href="/wiki/Compulsory_sterilization" title="Compulsory sterilization">compulsory sterilizations</a> in <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> another one bases a renewed demand to defund budgets on its own investigations suggesting a lack of distance between UNFPA activities and coercive Chinese population control.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> PRI monitored UNFPA's involvement in controversial sterilization campaigns during the <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peruvian</a> <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Fujimori" title="Alberto Fujimori">Fujimori</a> era,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> and it also investigated forceful actions against <a href="/wiki/Degar" title="Degar">Degar</a> women in <a href="/wiki/Vietnam" title="Vietnam">Vietnam</a> aimed at preventing reproduction.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fundraising">Fundraising</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Population_Research_Institute&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Fundraising">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>PRI obtains the vast majority of its funding from charitable contributions, gifts, and grants, with a total revenue of 1.31 million dollars in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Financial_year" title="Financial year">financial year</a> 2016. Of this, 73.4% was spent on program expenses, 3.9% on administration, and 22.6% on fundraising.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-charity-navigator_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-charity-navigator-23">[23]</a></sup>
</p><p>The institute has received funding from <a href="/wiki/Bradley_Foundation" title="Bradley Foundation">The Lynde and Harry Bradley Foundation, Inc.</a> for conferences on human rights in China.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Criticism">Criticism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Population_Research_Institute&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Criticism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>PRI's stance on <a href="/wiki/Human_overpopulation" title="Human overpopulation">overpopulation</a> and the arguments for "Overpopulation is a Myth" have been described as deceptive.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup>
</p><p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Ten_Million_Club" title="The Ten Million Club">The Ten Million Club</a> Foundation <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> further adds to criticism,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> accusing PRI of having a religious hidden agenda (against family planning and contraception) that motivates its (allegedly) deceiving claims, stating that those are not backed up by any original research («[PRI hasn't] as yet published a single peer review paper in any scientific journal»).
</p><p>Charity Navigator classifies charities with respect to "Accountability &amp; Transparency" and "Financial Performance". It awarded one (out of four) stars to PRI with respect to "Accountability &amp; Transparency" and one (out of four) stars with respect to the "Financial".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup>
</p><p>A rather benevolent review issued by CatholicCulture.org misses clarification from PRI in cases where the institute's website provides a research link to secular and non-Catholic contents.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup>
</p>
