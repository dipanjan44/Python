<p><b>Chemical Entities of Biological Interest</b>, also known as <b>ChEBI</b>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid19854951_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-pmid19854951-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid17932057_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid17932057-2">[2]</a></sup> is a database and <a href="/wiki/Ontology_(information_science)" title="Ontology (information science)">ontology</a> of <a href="/wiki/Molecular_entity" title="Molecular entity">molecular entities</a> focused on 'small' chemical compounds, that is part of the <a href="/wiki/Open_Biomedical_Ontologies" title="Open Biomedical Ontologies">Open Biomedical Ontologies</a> effort. The term "molecular entity" refers to any "constitutionally or isotopically distinct <a href="/wiki/Atom" title="Atom">atom</a>, <a href="/wiki/Molecule" title="Molecule">molecule</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ion" title="Ion">ion</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ion_pair" title="Ion pair">ion pair</a>, <a href="/wiki/Radical_(chemistry)" title="Radical (chemistry)">radical</a>, <a href="/wiki/Radical_ion" title="Radical ion">radical ion</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Complex_(chemistry)" title="Complex (chemistry)">complex</a>, <a href="/wiki/Conformational_isomerism" title="Conformational isomerism">conformer</a>, etc., identifiable as a separately distinguishable entity".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The molecular entities in question are either products of nature or synthetic products which have potential bioactivity. Molecules directly encoded by the genome, such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nucleic_acids" title="Nucleic acids">nucleic acids</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Proteins" title="Proteins">proteins</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Peptides" title="Peptides">peptides</a> derived from proteins by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Proteolytic" title="Proteolytic">proteolytic</a> cleavage, are not as a rule included in ChEBI.
</p><p>ChEBI uses <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IUPAC_nomenclature" title="IUPAC nomenclature">nomenclature</a>, symbolism and terminology endorsed by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IUPAC" title="IUPAC">International Union of Pure and Applied Chemistry</a> (IUPAC) and Nomenclature Committee of the <a href="/wiki/International_Union_of_Biochemistry_and_Molecular_Biology" title="International Union of Biochemistry and Molecular Biology">International Union of Biochemistry and Molecular Biology</a> (NC-IUBMB).
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Scope_and_access">Scope and access</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ChEBI&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Scope and access">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>All data in the database is non-proprietary or is derived from a non-proprietary source. It is thus freely accessible and available to anyone. In addition, each data item is fully traceable and explicitly referenced to the original source.
</p><p>The ChEBI data is available through a public web interface, Web Service and downloads.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Similarity_measures">Similarity measures</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ChEBI&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Similarity measures">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Semantic similarity measures exploring the hierarchy of ChEBI classes<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and its disjointness axioms<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> were shown to be a feasible and effective way to compare chemical compounds and improve classification systems. 
</p>
