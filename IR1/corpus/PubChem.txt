<p><b>PubChem</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Database" title="Database">database</a> of <a href="/wiki/Chemistry" title="Chemistry">chemical</a> <a href="/wiki/Molecule" title="Molecule">molecules</a> and their activities against biological assays. The system is maintained by the <a href="/wiki/National_Center_for_Biotechnology_Information" title="National Center for Biotechnology Information">National Center for Biotechnology Information</a> (NCBI), a component of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Library_of_Medicine" title="National Library of Medicine">National Library of Medicine</a>, which is part of the United States <a href="/wiki/National_Institutes_of_Health" title="National Institutes of Health">National Institutes of Health</a> (NIH). PubChem can be accessed for free through a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Web_user_interface" title="Web user interface">web user interface</a>. Millions of compound structures and descriptive datasets can be freely downloaded via <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FTP" title="FTP">FTP</a>. PubChem contains substance descriptions and small molecules with fewer than 1000 atoms and 1000 bonds. More than 80 database vendors contribute to the growing PubChem database.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Databases">Databases</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=PubChem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Databases">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>PubChem consists of three dynamically growing primary databases. As of 1 November 2017:
</p>
<ul><li>Compounds, 93.9 million entries <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> (up from 54 million entries in Sept 2014), contains pure and characterized chemical compounds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li>Substances, 236 million entries<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> (up from 163 million entries in Sept 2014<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>), contains also mixtures, <a href="/wiki/Extract" title="Extract">extracts</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Complex_(chemistry)" title="Complex (chemistry)">complexes</a> and uncharacterized substances.</li>
<li>BioAssay, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bioactivity" title="Bioactivity">bioactivity</a> results from 1.25 million<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> (up from 6000 in Sept 2014<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>) <a href="/wiki/High-throughput_screening" title="High-throughput screening">high-throughput screening</a> programs with several million values.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Searching">Searching</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=PubChem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Searching">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Searching the databases is possible for a broad range of properties including chemical structure, name fragments, <a href="/wiki/Chemical_formula" title="Chemical formula">chemical formula</a>, <a href="/wiki/Molecular_mass" title="Molecular mass">molecular weight</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/XLogP" title="XLogP">XLogP</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hydrogen_bond" title="Hydrogen bond">hydrogen bond</a> donor and acceptor count.
</p><p>PubChem contains its own online <a href="/wiki/Molecule_editor" title="Molecule editor">molecule editor</a> with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Simplified_molecular_input_line_entry_specification" title="Simplified molecular input line entry specification">SMILES</a>/SMARTS and <a href="/wiki/International_Chemical_Identifier" title="International Chemical Identifier">InChI</a> support that allows the import and export of all common <a href="/wiki/Chemical_file_format" title="Chemical file format">chemical file formats</a> to search for structures and fragments.
</p><p>Each hit provides information about synonyms, chemical properties, chemical structure including SMILES and InChI strings, bioactivity, and links to structurally related compounds and other NCBI databases like <a href="/wiki/PubMed" title="PubMed">PubMed</a>.
</p><p>In the text search form the database fields can be searched by adding the field name in square brackets to the search term. A numeric range is represented by two numbers separated by a colon. The search terms and field names are case-insensitive. Parentheses and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Logical_operator" title="Logical operator">logical operators</a> AND, OR, and NOT can be used. AND is assumed if no operator is used.
</p><p>Example (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lipinski%27s_Rule_of_Five" title="Lipinski's Rule of Five">Lipinski's Rule of Five</a>):
</p>
<pre>0:500[mw] 0:5[hbdc] 0:10[hbac] -5:5[logp]
</pre>

<h2><span id="ACS.27s_concerns"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="ACS's_concerns">ACS's concerns</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=PubChem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: ACS's concerns">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/American_Chemical_Society" title="American Chemical Society">American Chemical Society</a> has raised concerns about the publicly supported PubChem database, since it appears to directly compete with their existing <a href="/wiki/Chemical_Abstracts_Service" title="Chemical Abstracts Service">Chemical Abstracts Service</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> They have a strong interest in the issue since the Chemical Abstracts Service generates a large percentage of the society's revenue. To advocate their position against the PubChem database, ACS has actively lobbied the US Congress.
</p><p>Soon after PubChem's creation, the <a href="/wiki/American_Chemical_Society" title="American Chemical Society">American Chemical Society</a> lobbied <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Congress_of_the_United_States" title="Congress of the United States">U.S. Congress</a> to restrict the operation of PubChem, which they asserted competes with their <a href="/wiki/Chemical_Abstracts_Service" title="Chemical Abstracts Service">Chemical Abstracts Service</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p>
