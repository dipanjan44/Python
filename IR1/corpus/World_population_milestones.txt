<p><b>World population milestones</b> were unnoticed until the 20th  century, since there were no reliable data on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Global_population" title="Global population">global population</a> dynamics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>It is estimated that the population of the world reached one billion for the first time in 1804. It would be another 123 years before it reached two billion in 1927, but it took only 33 years to rise by another billion people, reaching three billion in 1960. Thereafter, the global population reached four billion in 1974, five billion in 1987, six billion in 1999 and, by some estimates, seven billion in October 2011 with other estimates being in March 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UN7bn_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-UN7bn-3">[3]</a></sup> It is projected to reach eight billion by 2024–2030. According to current projections, the world's population is likely to reach around nine billion by 2035–2050, with alternative scenarios ranging from a low of 7.4 billion to a high of more than 10.6 billion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WorldPopProspects_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-WorldPopProspects-4">[4]</a></sup> Projected figures vary depending on underlying statistical assumptions and which variables are manipulated in projection calculations, especially the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fertility_rate" title="Fertility rate">fertility variable</a>. Long-range predictions to 2150 range from a population decline to 3.2 billion in the
'low scenario', to 'high scenarios' of 24.8 billion. One scenario predicted a massive increase to 256 billion by 2150, assuming fertility remains at 1995 levels.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LongRangeProjections2003KeyFindings_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-LongRangeProjections2003KeyFindings-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Global_billionth_milestones">Global billionth milestones</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_population_milestones&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Global billionth milestones">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There is no estimation for the exact day or month the world's population surpassed each of the one and two billion marks. The days of three and four billion were not officially noted, but the International Database of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau" title="United States Census Bureau">United States Census Bureau</a> places them in July 1959 and April 1974.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Five_billion">Five billion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_population_milestones&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Five billion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><b>The Day of Five Billion</b>, 11 July 1987, was designated by the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Population_Fund" title="United Nations Population Fund">United Nations Population Fund</a> as the approximate day on which <a href="/wiki/World_population" title="World population">world population</a> reached five billion.  <b>Matej Gašpar</b> from <a href="/wiki/Zagreb" title="Zagreb">Zagreb</a>, Croatia (then <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SR_Croatia" title="SR Croatia">SR Croatia</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SFR_Yugoslavia" title="SFR Yugoslavia">SFR Yugoslavia</a>), was chosen as the symbolic 5-billionth person concurrently alive on <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth</a>.  The honor went to Zagreb because the <a href="/wiki/1987_Summer_Universiade" title="1987 Summer Universiade">1987 Summer Universiade</a> was taking place in the city at the time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Six_billion">Six billion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_population_milestones&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Six billion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Population_Fund" title="United Nations Population Fund">United Nations Population Fund</a> designated 12 October 1999 as the approximate day on which the <a href="/wiki/World_population" title="World population">world population</a> reached six billion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-post_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-post-8">[8]</a></sup>  It was officially designated <b>The Day of Six Billion</b>.  Demographers do not universally accept this date as being exact.  In fact there has been subsequent research which places the day of six billion nearer to 18 June or 19 June 1999.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>  The International Programs division of the United States Census Bureau estimated that the world population reached six billion on 21 April 1999.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>  United Nations Population Fund spokesman Omar Gharzeddine disputed the date of the Day of Six Billion by stating, "The U.N. marked the '6 billionth' [person] in 1999, and then a couple of years later the Population Division itself reassessed its calculations and said, actually, no, it was in 1998."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-msnbc_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-msnbc-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p><p>On the Day of Six Billion, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UN_Secretary-General" title="UN Secretary-General">UN Secretary-General</a> <a href="/wiki/Kofi_Annan" title="Kofi Annan">Kofi Annan</a> was in <a href="/wiki/Sarajevo" title="Sarajevo">Sarajevo</a>, Bosnia and Herzegovina to monitor the <a href="/wiki/Dayton_Agreement" title="Dayton Agreement">Dayton Agreement</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-unsgt2204_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-unsgt2204-11">[11]</a></sup> At midnight he went to Koševo Hospital, where <b>Adnan Mević</b>, born at 12.01 am, was named the symbolic 6 billionth concurrently alive person on <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-post_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-post-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-unsgt2204_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-unsgt2204-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc472704_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc472704-12">[12]</a></sup>  He is the first son of Fatima Mević and Jasminko Mević and weighed 3.5 kg.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc472704_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-bbc472704-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Seven_billion">Seven billion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_population_milestones&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Seven billion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Day_of_Seven_Billion" title="Day of Seven Billion">Day of Seven Billion</a></div>
<p>The "<a href="/wiki/Day_of_Seven_Billion" title="Day of Seven Billion">Day of Seven Billion</a>" was targeted by the United States Census Bureau to be in March 2012,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USCBcite_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-USCBcite-13">[13]</a></sup> while the Population Division of the United Nations suggested 31 October 2011,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> and the latter date was officially designated by the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Population_Fund" title="United Nations Population Fund">United Nations Population Fund</a> (UNFPA) as the approximate day on which the <a href="/wiki/World_population" title="World population">world's population</a> reached seven billion people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-un_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-un-15">[15]</a></sup> United Nations Secretary General <a href="/wiki/Ban_Ki-moon" title="Ban Ki-moon">Ban Ki-moon</a> spoke at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_Nations_Headquarters" title="United Nations Headquarters">United Nations building</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> on this milestone in the size of world population, and promoted the website 7 Billion Actions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-unfpa_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-unfpa-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-actions_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-actions-17">[17]</a></sup> Ban Ki-moon did not choose a symbolic seven billionth baby, but several groups proposed candidates: Nargis Kumar of <a href="/wiki/Uttar_Pradesh" title="Uttar Pradesh">Uttar Pradesh</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Danica May Camacho of <a href="/wiki/Manila" title="Manila">Manila</a>, <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guardian-un_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-guardian-un-19">[19]</a></sup> and Wattalage Muthumai of <a href="/wiki/Colombo" title="Colombo">Colombo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">Sri Lanka</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="National_and_regional_population">National and regional population</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_population_milestones&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: National and regional population">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>National or subnational governments have sometimes made similar designations based on the date estimated by a demographic agency. Some national milestones relate to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Citizen" title="Citizen">citizens</a> rather than <a href="/wiki/Residency_(domicile)" title="Residency (domicile)">residents</a>. Commentators in countries with high <a href="/wiki/Immigration" title="Immigration">immigration</a> have pointed out that a population milestone may be reached by an immigrant rather than <a href="/wiki/Rate_of_natural_increase" title="Rate of natural increase">natural increase</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup>
</p>
