<p><b>Sustainability <a href="/wiki/Organization" title="Organization">organizations</a></b> are (1) organized groups of people that aim to advance sustainability and/or (2) those actions of organizing something sustainably. Unlike many business organizations, sustainability organizations are not limited to implementing <a href="/wiki/Sustainability" title="Sustainability">sustainability</a> strategies which provide them with economic and cultural benefits attained through <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Environmental_responsibility" title="Environmental responsibility">environmental responsibility</a>. For sustainability organizations, sustainability can also be an end in itself without further justifications.
</p><p>Recently,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (August 2015)">when?</span></a></i>]</sup> the natural environment has become a key strategic issue in both the <a href="/wiki/Business" title="Business">business</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academic_community" title="Academic community">academic communities</a>.  Through "implementing sustainability strategies, firms can integrate long-run profitability with their efforts to protect the <a href="/wiki/Ecosystem" title="Ecosystem">ecosystem</a>, providing them with opportunities to achieve the traditional competitive advantages of &amp; cost leadership and market differentiation via environmental responsibility".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
Sustainability strategies have been persistently employed in a number<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="This term requires quantification. (August 2015)">quantify</span></a></i>]</sup> of organizations.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Definition">Definition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainability_organizations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Definition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A sustainable system generally can be defined in environmental terminology as "a living system which operates in a way that it does not use up resources more quickly than they can be naturally replenished;  a sustainable economic system operates in a way so that expenditures are either equal or less than the income."  Sustainable social systems maintain that all members are allowed to contribute, thereby synthesizing the final product. 
<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Corporate_sustainability">Corporate sustainability</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainability_organizations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Corporate sustainability">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Corporate_Sustainability" title="Corporate Sustainability">Corporate Sustainability</a> refers to "a company’s activities, voluntary by definition, demonstrating the inclusion of social and environmental concerns in business operations and in interactions with stakeholders".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
Each individual organization should choose its own particular goals and approaches as they pertain to corporate sustainability, matching the organization’s aims and intentions and aligning with the organization’s strategy, as an appropriate response to the conditions in which it functions.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Agriculture">Agriculture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainability_organizations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Agriculture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sustainable agriculture incorporates design and management procedures that coincide with natural processes in order to conserve all resources and minimize waste production and damage to natural systems, while preserving or improving farm profitability.  Sustainable agriculture systems are "designed to take the greatest advantage of current soil nutrient and water cycles, energy flows, beneficial soil organisms, and natural pest controls.  These systems also seek to produce food that is nutritious yet uncontaminated with products such as synthetically compound fertilizers, pesticides, growth regulators, and livestock feed additives.  Sustainable agriculture systems often depend on crop rotations, crop residues, animal manures, legumes, green manures, off-farm organic wastes, appropriate mechanical cultivation or minimal tillage to optimize soil biological and natural pest control activity in order to maintain soil fertility and crop productivity."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-auto-4">[4]</a></sup>
Also, resistant species varieties, and biological and cultural controls are implemented to control pests, weeds and diseases.
<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-auto-4">[4]</a></sup>
Sustainable agriculture organizations aim to educate and encourage farmers to implement sustainable agriculture practices into their productions.  Many sponsor research and education outreach projects.
</p><p><b>Agricultural sustainability organizations:</b>
</p>
<ul><li><b>Alternative Farming Systems Information Center (AFSIC)</b> info and links to other internet sites and documents on sustainable agriculture</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/EnviroLink_Network" title="EnviroLink Network">EnviroLink Network</a></b>  sustainable business network news and information</li>
<li><b>National Sustainable Agriculture Information Service</b> offers in-depth publications on production practices, alternative crop and livestock enterprises, innovative marketing, organic certification, and highlights of local, regional, USDA and other federal sustainable agriculture activities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Sightline_Institute&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Sightline Institute (page does not exist)">Sightline Institute</a></b> formerly Northwest Environment Watch, a non-profit organization that aims to foster a sustainable economy and way of life in the Pacific Northwest, a bioregion defined by the watersheds of rivers flowing through North America's temperate rainforest zone</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Sustainable_Agriculture_Research_and_Education" title="Sustainable Agriculture Research and Education">Sustainable Agriculture Research and Education</a></b> communication and outreach arm of the Sustainable Agriculture Research and Education Program.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/The_Land_Institute" title="The Land Institute">The Land Institute</a></b> developing diverse perennial grain cropping systems to solve problems such as soil erosion, energy dependency, and water contamination.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Sustainable Northwest </b> a Portland, Oregon-based organization forging a sustainable economy in the Pacific Northwest<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>The Food Alliance</b> support for sustainable agriculture<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Building">Building</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainability_organizations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Building">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>"By using more efficient building methods and materials, it is estimated that we could reduce the energy, <a href="/wiki/Resource_consumption" title="Resource consumption">resource consumption</a> and / or waste production by 50-60% without decreasing value, aesthetics or function."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Taking the Earth's finite resources into consideration, along with acknowledging that manufactured products, including all building materials, affect the Earth’s resources, it is becoming very important to make sensible decisions regarding the use of these limited resources to preserve our natural environment along with human civilization.  Increased use of resource efficient construction materials and methods will help to induce more sustainable practices throughout the building and construction industry. 
<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>

<li><b>thePOOSH.org</b> an Estonian-based NGO with an international scope that connects volunteers with sustainable build projects all around the world to exchange labor, skills and knowledge<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Business">Business</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainability_organizations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Business">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sustainable business organizations participate in environmentally friendly or green practices in order to make certain that all processes, products, and manufacturing activities sufficiently address current environmental concerns while still retaining a profit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto1_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-auto1-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto2_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-auto2-17">[17]</a></sup> Concurrently, it is a business that “meets the needs of the present world without compromising the ability of the future generations to meet their own needs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto1_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-auto1-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto2_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-auto2-17">[17]</a></sup>
It is the method of evaluating how to design products that will correspond to current environmental conditions and how well a company’s products perform with renewable resources.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup>
There are many organizations and networks currently interacting with businesses in order to integrate sustainability into their central goals and contribute to the environmentally and socially responsible business movement.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup>
</p><p><b>Sustainable business organizations:</b>
</p>
<ul><li><b>Business and Industry Resource Venture</b>, provides free information, assistance and referrals to help Seattle businesses improve their environmental performance<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Business for Social Responsibility</b>  a business membership organization<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Center for a Sustainable Economy</b> non-partisan research and policy organization that promotes innovative tax and other market-based approaches to achieving a sustainable economy<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>CERES</b> the largest coalition of environmental, investor, and advocacy groups working together for sustainable prosperity. These groups form a community of forward-looking companies that have committed to continuous environmental improvement by endorsing the CERES principles, a ten-point code of environmental conduct.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Cool Companies</b>  A guide for businesses seeking to cut energy costs and reduce pollution.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Global Environmental Management Initiative</b> the Global Environmental Management Initiative standard reporting for environmental, economic and social activity<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>GreenBiz</b> A resource center on business, the environment, and the bottom line<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a class="external text" href="http://www.sustainabilityprofessionals.org/" rel="nofollow">International Society of Sustainability Professionals</a></b> the professional association for people working in the field of sustainability. Focus is on developing both the profession of sustainability as well as the competencies and expertise of those working in the field.</li>
<li><b>Lifestyles of Health and Sustainability (LOHAS)</b> a market segment focused on health and fitness, the environment, personal development, sustainable living, and social justice.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Sacramento Area Sustainable Business Program</b> Regional partnership that encourages businesses to adopt environmentally friendly and sustainable practices. Certifies and promotes businesses that take voluntary actions to conserve energy and water, reduce waste and pollution, recycle and purchase recycled products, improve air quality, and implement green building measures.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>FL Sustainability</b> is an online news and media outlet focused on people, place, and profit, the triple bottom line of sustainability. Based out of Florida, the organization organized a major summit that brought leaders from various innovation sectors together to discuss regional and statewide initiatives to improve quality of life for today and tomorrow.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Communities">Communities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainability_organizations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Communities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sustainable community organizations often encourage and cultivate collaborative community projects and education programs that improve connections between businesses, institutions and the public with their communities, the natural environment, and each other.  For example, the Sustainable Community Initiatives organization: 
</p>
<ul><li>Acts to help the business community, local government and the general public develop an awareness of the value of sustainable community development<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sustainablecommunityinitiatives.org_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-sustainablecommunityinitiatives.org-30">[30]</a></sup></li>
<li>Endeavors to combine knowledge (through public education) and action (in the form of community programs) to promote prosperous and healthy communities<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sustainablecommunityinitiatives.org_30-1"><a href="#cite_note-sustainablecommunityinitiatives.org-30">[30]</a></sup></li>
<li>Explores opportunities to develop educational, entrepreneurial and environmentally sound community-based projects<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sustainablecommunityinitiatives.org_30-2"><a href="#cite_note-sustainablecommunityinitiatives.org-30">[30]</a></sup></li>
<li>Develops materials on the efficient use of urban environmental resources and sustainable activities for distribution to local businesses and to the general public<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sustainablecommunityinitiatives.org_30-3"><a href="#cite_note-sustainablecommunityinitiatives.org-30">[30]</a></sup></li>
<li>Conducts sustainability seminars and workshops for a variety of audiences, e.g. financial institutions, housing development organizations, continuing education programs and community organizations<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sustainablecommunityinitiatives.org_30-4"><a href="#cite_note-sustainablecommunityinitiatives.org-30">[30]</a></sup></li>
<li>Participates in efforts to promote more sustainable tax policies and land use codes<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sustainablecommunityinitiatives.org_30-5"><a href="#cite_note-sustainablecommunityinitiatives.org-30">[30]</a></sup></li>
<li>Integrates into all of SCI's undertakings, the fundamental elements of community sustainability: community partnership, community enterprise, community conservation and community design <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sustainablecommunityinitiatives.org_30-6"><a href="#cite_note-sustainablecommunityinitiatives.org-30">[30]</a></sup></li></ul>
<p><b>Sustainable community organizations:</b>
</p>
<ul><li><b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Biomimicry_Institute" title="The Biomimicry Institute">The Biomimicry Institute</a></b>  a not-for-profit organization that promotes the study and imitation of nature’s remarkably efficient designs, bringing together scientists, engineers, architects and innovators who can use those models to create sustainable technologies.</li>
<li><b>Fostering Sustainable Behavior</b>  Doug McKenzie Moore on community-based social marketing<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Global_Ecovillage_Network" title="Global Ecovillage Network">Global Ecovillage Network</a> (GEN)</b>  a growing network of sustainable communities and initiatives that bridge different cultures, countries, and continents. GEN serves as umbrella organization for ecovillages, transition town initiatives, intentional communities, and ecologically minded individuals worldwide.</li>
<li><b>Partnership for Sustainable Communities (PSC)</b>  The Partnership for Sustainable Communities (PSC) is a national, nonprofit 501(c)(3) organization based in San Rafael, California dedicated to helping make American cities and towns more efficient.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Sacramento Area Sustainable Business Program</b> Regional partnership that encourages businesses to adopt environmentally friendly and sustainable practices. Certifies and promotes businesses that take voluntary actions to conserve energy and water, reduce waste and pollution, recycle and purchase recycled products, improve air quality, and implement green building measures.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></li>

<li><b>Sustainable Connections</b>  working with business and community leaders working to transform and model an economy built on sustainable practices<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Global_Ecovillage_Network" title="Global Ecovillage Network">Global Ecovillage Network</a></b> 1500+ pages on ecovillages and sustainability</li>
<li><b>Sustainable Architecture Buildings and Culture</b>  ideas for sustainable communities<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainability_organizations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Despite the fact that some places may be similar in economic terms, the characteristics of a sustainable business model vary from community to community.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup>  Many organizations aim to assist communities in developing economic opportunities for all citizens.  Some programs exist that "assist individuals in obtaining employment, that create jobs and that help individuals become economically self-sufficient." <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup>
</p><p><b>Economic sustainability organizations:</b>
</p>
<ul><li><b>Center for the Advancement of the Steady State Economy (CASSE)</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup>  is an organization dedicated to advocating a sustainable economy with stabilized population and consumption; such an economy is called a "steady state economy."</li></ul>
<h3><span id="Environment.2Fenergy"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Environment/energy">Environment/energy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainability_organizations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Environment/energy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Environmental sustainability is the "long-term maintenance of ecosystem components and functions for future generations."  In addition, environmental sustainability is the process of making sure current processes of interaction with the environment are pursued with the idea of keeping the environment as unspoiled as naturally possible.  It maintains that the Earth’s resources must not be depleted faster than they can naturally be replenished.  Depletion of our finite resources inevitably tries the ability of human civilizations to persist.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup>
</p><p><b>Environmental sustainability organizations:</b>
</p>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Bioneers" title="Bioneers">Bioneers</a></b> working to preserve biological and cultural diversity</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Center_for_a_New_American_Dream" title="Center for a New American Dream">Center for a New American Dream</a></b> New American Dream helps Americans consume responsibly to protect the environment, enhance quality of life, and promote social justice.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Friends_of_the_Earth" title="Friends of the Earth">Friends of the Earth</a> (FOE)</b> is an international environmental organization dedicated to preserving the health and diversity of the planet for future generations</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/International_Institute_for_Environment_and_Development" title="International Institute for Environment and Development">International Institute for Environment and Development</a> (IIED)</b> promotes sustainable patterns of world development through collaborative research, policy studies, networking and knowledge dissemination</li>
<li><b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/International_Union_for_the_Conservation_of_Nature" title="International Union for the Conservation of Nature">International Union for the Conservation of Nature</a> (IUCN)</b> seeks to conserve the integrity and diversity of nature and to ensure that any use of natural resources is equitable and ecologically sustainable</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Northwest_Earth_Institute" title="Northwest Earth Institute">Northwest Earth Institute</a></b> a pioneer in taking earth-centered education programs to people where they spend their time-in their neighborhoods, workplaces, homes, schools, and centers of faith</li>
<li><b>Renewable Energy Policy Project (REPP)</b> supports the advancement of renewable energy technology through policy research <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Rocky_Mountain_Institute" title="Rocky Mountain Institute">Rocky Mountain Institute</a></b> We work extensively with the private sector, as well as with civil society and government, to create abundance by design and to apply the framework of natural capitalism.</li>
<li><b><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Sightline_Institute&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Sightline Institute (page does not exist)">Sightline Institute</a></b> Sightline Institute is an independent, nonprofit research and communications center — a think tank — founded by Alan Durning in 1993.</li>
<li><b>Zero Waste Alliance</b>  The Zero Waste Alliance is a national leader providing assistance to industry sectors and organizations for development and implementation of standards, tools and practices that lead to a more sustainable future through the reduction and elimination of waste and toxics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup></li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Law_and_policy">Law and policy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainability_organizations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Law and policy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>"Regulations, incentives and policies affecting sustainable agriculture are rooted in environmental legislation and laws on the international, Federal, state and local level."
<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup>
</p><p><b>Law and policy sustainability organizations:</b>
</p>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Earth_Policy_Institute" title="Earth Policy Institute">Earth Policy Institute</a></b> dedicated to building an environmentally sustainable economy; raising awareness to support public response to population growth, rising CO2, loss of species, and other trends that are affecting the Earth</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Global_Exchange" title="Global Exchange">Global Exchange</a></b>  nonprofit organization that envisions a people-centered globalization that values the rights of workers and the health of the planet; that prioritizes international collaboration as central to ensuring peace; and that aims to create a local, green economy designed to embrace the diversity of our communities.</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="News">News</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainability_organizations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: News">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><b>News sustainability organizations:</b>
</p>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Worldwatch_Institute" title="Worldwatch Institute">Worldwatch Institute</a></b> dedicated to fostering the evolution of an environmentally sustainable society—one in which human needs are met in ways that do not threaten the health of the natural environment or the prospects of future generations</li>
<li><b>Triple Pundit </b> is an online publication dedicated to promoting the concepts of sustainability to a business audience. The name is a reference to the "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Triple_Bottom_Line" title="Triple Bottom Line">Triple Bottom Line</a>" which seeks to balance economy, society, and the environment - a key tenet to sustainable thinking.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Non-profit">Non-profit</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainability_organizations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Non-profit">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Nonprofit organizations "play a part in society through their social value creation." <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto3_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-auto3-45">[45]</a></sup> Many nonprofit organizations have adopted an organizational sustainability focus in both strategic and operational levels of management.  Present nonprofit organizations have been required to adopt strategies aimed at establishing viable, sustainable organizations so that they can maintain to pursue their social mission.   
<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto3_45-1"><a href="#cite_note-auto3-45">[45]</a></sup>
</p><p><b>Non-profit sustainability organizations:</b>
</p>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Action_Without_Borders" title="Action Without Borders">Action Without Borders</a></b> a nonprofit organization founded in 1995 with offices in the United States and Argentina. It runs Idealist.org, an interactive site where people and organizations can exchange resources and ideas, locate opportunities and supporters, and take steps toward building a world where all people can lead free and dignified lives.</li>
<li><b>Cielito Lindo Ranch</b> a non-profit organization operating off-the-grid in southwest New Mexico focusing on research into lost-cost easy to implement sustainable technologies that anyone can use to simplify and improve their life.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Corporate_Watch" title="Corporate Watch">Corporate Watch</a></b> A non-profit organization committed to “holding corporations accountable.” Functions as a resource on corporate globalization research, news stories, and ways to take action. Also, organizes campaigns against political campaign financing, <a href="/wiki/Greenwashing" title="Greenwashing">greenwashing</a>, and war-profiteering.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Environmental_Working_Group" title="Environmental Working Group">Environmental Working Group</a> (EWG)</b> a not-for-profit environmental research organization that uses the power of information to improve public health and protect the environment by reducing pollution in air, water and food. Based in Washington, D.C., and with an office in Oakland, California, EWG conducts groundbreaking, computer-assisted research on a variety of environmental issues.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Green_Cross_International" title="Green Cross International">Green Cross International</a></b> non-profit NGO founded by Mikhail Gorbachev with a mission to help create a sustainable future by cultivating harmonious relationships between humans and the environment</li>
<li><b>Shajar Dost Foundation</b> (Pakistan) Shajar Dost is working to increase forest cover in Pakistan. Pakistan has the highest deforestation rate in Asia. Shajar Dost is planting suplings in Murree at Mussyari on three hundred acres.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Social_Venture_Network" title="Social Venture Network">Social Venture Network</a> (SVN)</b> promotes new models and leadership for socially and environmentally sustainable business in the 21st century.” As a nonprofit network, they do this by championing member initiatives, information services, and community forums.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Fair_Trade_USA" title="Fair Trade USA">Fair Trade USA</a></b>  nonprofit organization, is one of twenty members of Fairtrade Labelling Organizations International (FLO), and the only third-party certifier of Fair Trade products in the United States.</li></ul>

Many educational organizations offer their own global education programs in the area of environmental sustainability, or resources for the development of sustainability programs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Networking">Networking</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainability_organizations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Networking">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Many organizations provide an outlet for expanding and cultivating social networks in order to attain the most beneficial responses for various goals among sustainability stakeholders.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup>   In other words, "many sustainability networking organizations are a catalyst in bringing about systemic change by giving decision makers a shared, science-based understanding of sustainability and a unifying framework for developing a sustainable society."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup>
</p><p><b>Sustainability networking organizations:</b>
</p>

<li><b><a href="/wiki/Sierra_Club" title="Sierra Club">Sierra Club</a></b> is one of the US' oldest, largest environmental organizations for the protection, restoration, and responsible use of earth’s resources; site has extensive resources for information, networking, and action.</li></ul>
