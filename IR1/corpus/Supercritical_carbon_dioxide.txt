<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Supercritical carbon dioxide</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Carbon_dioxide_pressure-temperature_phase_diagram.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="700" data-file-width="742" height="208" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/13/Carbon_dioxide_pressure-temperature_phase_diagram.svg/220px-Carbon_dioxide_pressure-temperature_phase_diagram.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/13/Carbon_dioxide_pressure-temperature_phase_diagram.svg/330px-Carbon_dioxide_pressure-temperature_phase_diagram.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/13/Carbon_dioxide_pressure-temperature_phase_diagram.svg/440px-Carbon_dioxide_pressure-temperature_phase_diagram.svg.png 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Carbon_dioxide_pressure-temperature_phase_diagram.svg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Carbon dioxide pressure-temperature phase diagram</div></div></div>
<p><b>Supercritical carbon dioxide</b> (<b>s<span class="chemf nowrap">CO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span></b>) is a fluid state of <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide" title="Carbon dioxide">carbon dioxide</a> where it is held at or above its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Critical_temperature" title="Critical temperature">critical temperature</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Critical_pressure" title="Critical pressure">critical pressure</a>.
</p><p>Carbon dioxide usually behaves as a <a href="/wiki/Gas" title="Gas">gas</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Air" title="Air">air</a> at <a href="/wiki/Standard_conditions_for_temperature_and_pressure" title="Standard conditions for temperature and pressure">standard temperature and pressure</a> (STP), or as a <a href="/wiki/Solid" title="Solid">solid</a> called <a href="/wiki/Dry_ice" title="Dry ice">dry ice</a> when frozen. If the <a href="/wiki/Temperature" title="Temperature">temperature</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pressure" title="Pressure">pressure</a> are both increased from STP to be at or above the <a href="/wiki/Critical_point_(thermodynamics)" title="Critical point (thermodynamics)">critical point</a> for carbon dioxide, it can adopt properties midway between a gas and a <a href="/wiki/Liquid" title="Liquid">liquid</a>. More specifically, it behaves as a <a href="/wiki/Supercritical_fluid" title="Supercritical fluid">supercritical fluid</a> above its critical temperature (304.25 K, 31.10 °C, 87.98 °F) and critical pressure (72.9 atm, 7.39 MPa, 1,071 psi), expanding to fill its container like a gas but with a <a href="/wiki/Density" title="Density">density</a> like that of a liquid.
</p><p>Supercritical <span class="chemf nowrap">CO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> is becoming an important commercial and industrial <a href="/wiki/Solvent" title="Solvent">solvent</a> due to its role in chemical <a href="/wiki/Extraction_(chemistry)" title="Extraction (chemistry)">extraction</a> in addition to its low toxicity and environmental impact. The relatively low temperature of the process and the stability of <span class="chemf nowrap">CO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> also allows most compounds to be extracted with little damage or <a href="/wiki/Denaturation_(biochemistry)" title="Denaturation (biochemistry)">denaturing</a>. In addition, the solubility of many extracted compounds in <span class="chemf nowrap">CO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> varies with pressure,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> permitting selective extractions.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Applications">Applications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Supercritical_carbon_dioxide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Applications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solvent">Solvent</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Supercritical_carbon_dioxide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Solvent">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Supercritical_fluid_extraction" title="Supercritical fluid extraction">Supercritical fluid extraction</a></div>
<p>Carbon dioxide is gaining popularity among <a href="/wiki/Coffee" title="Coffee">coffee</a> manufacturers looking to move away from classic decaffeinating <a href="/wiki/Solvent" title="Solvent">solvents</a>, because of real or perceived dangers related to their use in food preparation. s<span class="chemf nowrap">CO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> is forced through the green coffee beans which are then sprayed with water at high pressure to remove the caffeine. The caffeine can then be isolated for resale (e.g. to the pharmaceutical or beverage manufacturers) by passing the water through <a href="/wiki/Activated_carbon" title="Activated carbon">activated charcoal filters</a> or by <a href="/wiki/Distillation" title="Distillation">distillation</a>, <a href="/wiki/Crystallization" title="Crystallization">crystallization</a> or <a href="/wiki/Reverse_osmosis" title="Reverse osmosis">reverse osmosis</a>. Supercritical carbon dioxide is used to remove <a href="/wiki/Organochloride" title="Organochloride">organochloride</a> pesticides and metals from agricultural crops without adulterating the desired constituents from the plant matter in the herbal supplement industry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Supercritical carbon dioxide can be used as a more environmentally friendly solvent for <a href="/wiki/Dry_cleaning" title="Dry cleaning">dry cleaning</a> over traditional solvents such as hydrocarbons, including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Perchloroethylene" title="Perchloroethylene">perchloroethylene</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>Supercritical carbon dioxide is used as the extraction solvent for creation of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Essential_oils" title="Essential oils">essential oils</a> and other <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Herbal_distillates" title="Herbal distillates">herbal distillates</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Its main advantages over solvents such as <a href="/wiki/Hexane" title="Hexane">hexane</a> and <a href="/wiki/Acetone" title="Acetone">acetone</a> in this process are that it is non-toxic and non-flammable. Furthermore, separation of the reaction components from the starting material is much simpler than with traditional <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Organic_solvents" title="Organic solvents">organic solvents</a>. The <span class="chemf nowrap">CO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> can evaporate into the air or be recycled by condensation into a cold recovery vessel. Its advantage over <a href="/wiki/Steam_distillation" title="Steam distillation">steam distillation</a> is that it operates at a lower temperature, which can separate the plant <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Waxes" title="Waxes">waxes</a> from the oils.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/Laboratory" title="Laboratory">laboratories</a>, s<span class="chemf nowrap">CO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> is used as an extraction solvent, for example for determining total recoverable hydrocarbons from soils, sediments, fly-ash and other media,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> and determination of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Polycyclic_aromatic_hydrocarbons" title="Polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons">polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons</a> in soil and solid wastes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Supercritical fluid extraction has been used in determining <a href="/wiki/Hydrocarbon" title="Hydrocarbon">hydrocarbon</a> components in water.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p><p>Processes that use s<span class="chemf nowrap">CO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> to produce micro and <a href="/wiki/Nano-" title="Nano-">nano</a> scale particles, often for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pharmaceutical" title="Pharmaceutical">pharmaceutical</a> uses, are under development. The gas <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Antisolvent" title="Antisolvent">antisolvent</a> process, rapid expansion of supercritical solutions and supercritical antisolvent <a href="/wiki/Precipitation_(chemistry)" title="Precipitation (chemistry)">precipitation</a> (as well as several related methods) process a variety of substances into particles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p><p>Due to its ability to selectively dissolve organic compounds and assist the functioning of enzymes, s<span class="chemf nowrap">CO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> has been suggested as a potential solvent to support biological activity on <a href="/wiki/Venus" title="Venus">Venus</a>- or <a href="/wiki/Super-Earth" title="Super-Earth">super-Earth</a>-type planets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Manufactured_products">Manufactured products</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Supercritical_carbon_dioxide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Manufactured products">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Environmentally beneficial, low-cost substitutes for rigid <a href="/wiki/Thermoplastic" title="Thermoplastic">thermoplastic</a> and fired <a href="/wiki/Ceramic" title="Ceramic">ceramic</a> are made using s<span class="chemf nowrap">CO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chemical_reagent" title="Chemical reagent">chemical reagent</a>. The s<span class="chemf nowrap">CO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> in these processes is reacted with the alkaline components of fully hardened <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hydraulic_cement" title="Hydraulic cement">hydraulic cement</a> or <a href="/wiki/Gypsum" title="Gypsum">gypsum</a> <a href="/wiki/Plaster" title="Plaster">plaster</a> to form various carbonates.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The primary byproduct is water.
</p><p>Supercritical carbon dioxide is used in the foaming of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Polymers" title="Polymers">polymers</a>. Supercritical carbon dioxide can saturate the polymer with solvent. Upon depressurization and heating the carbon dioxide rapidly expands, causing voids within the polymer matrix, i.e., creating a foam. Research is also ongoing at many universities in the production of microcellular foams using s<span class="chemf nowrap">CO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span>.
</p><p>An <a href="/wiki/Electrosynthesis" title="Electrosynthesis">electrochemical</a> <a href="/wiki/Carboxylation" title="Carboxylation">carboxylation</a> of a para-<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Isobutyl" title="Isobutyl">isobutyl</a><a href="/wiki/Benzyl_chloride" title="Benzyl chloride">benzyl chloride</a> to <a href="/wiki/Ibuprofen" title="Ibuprofen">ibuprofen</a> is promoted under s<span class="chemf nowrap">CO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SCY_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-SCY-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Working_fluid">Working fluid</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Supercritical_carbon_dioxide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Working fluid">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Supercritical <span class="chemf nowrap">CO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> is chemically stable, reliable, low-cost, non-toxic, non-flammable and readily available, making it a desirable candidate working fluid.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Power_generation">Power generation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Supercritical_carbon_dioxide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Power generation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The unique properties of s<span class="chemf nowrap">CO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> present advantages for closed-loop power generation and can be applied to various power generation applications. Power generation systems that use traditional air <a href="/wiki/Brayton_cycle" title="Brayton cycle">Brayton</a> and steam <a href="/wiki/Rankine_cycle" title="Rankine cycle">Rankine cycles</a> can be upgraded to s<span class="chemf nowrap">CO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> to increase efficiency and power output.
</p><p>The relatively new <a href="/wiki/Allam_power_cycle" title="Allam power cycle">Allam power cycle</a> uses sCO2 as the working fluid in combination with fuel and pure oxygen. The CO2 produced by combustion mixes with the sCO2 working fluid and a corresponding amount of pure CO2 must be removed from the process (for industrial use or sequestration). This process reduces atmospheric emissions to zero. 
</p><p>It presents interesting properties that promise substantial improvements in system efficiency. Due to its high fluid density, s<span class="chemf nowrap">CO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> enables extremely compact and highly efficient turbomachinery. It can use simpler, single casing body designs while <a href="/wiki/Steam_turbine" title="Steam turbine">steam turbines</a> require multiple turbine stages and associated casings, as well as additional inlet and outlet piping. The high density allows for highly compact, microchannel-based heat exchanger technology.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2016, General Electric announced an s<span class="chemf nowrap">CO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span>-based turbine that operated at 50% efficiency. In it the <span class="chemf nowrap">CO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> is heated to 700 °C. It requires less compression and allows heat transfer. It reaches full power in 2 minutes, whereas steam turbines need at least 30 minutes. The prototype generated 10 MW and is approximately 10% the size of a comparable steam turbine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup>
</p><p>Further, due to its superior thermal stability and non-flammability, direct heat exchange from high temperature sources is possible, permitting higher working fluid temperatures and therefore higher cycle efficiency. And unlike two-phase flow, s<span class="chemf nowrap">CO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span>’s single-phase nature eliminates the necessity of a heat input for phase change that is required for the water to steam conversion, thereby also eliminating associated <a href="/wiki/Fatigue_(material)" title="Fatigue (material)">thermal fatigue</a> and corrosion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup>
</p><p>Despite the promise of substantially higher efficiency and lower capital costs, the use of s<span class="chemf nowrap">CO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> presents material selection and design issues. Materials in power generation components must display resistance to damage caused by <a href="/wiki/Thermal_resistance" title="Thermal resistance">high-temperature</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anti-corrosion" title="Anti-corrosion">oxidation</a> and <a href="/wiki/Creep_(deformation)#Preventing_creep" title="Creep (deformation)">creep</a>. Candidate materials that meet these property and performance goals include incumbent alloys in power generation, such as nickel-based superalloys for turbomachinery components and <a href="/wiki/Austenitic_stainless_steel" title="Austenitic stainless steel">austenitic stainless steels</a> for piping. Components within s<span class="chemf nowrap">CO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> Brayton loops suffer from corrosion and erosion, specifically erosion in turbomachinery and recuperative heat exchanger components and intergranular corrosion and pitting in the piping.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup>
</p><p>Testing has been conducted on candidate Ni-based alloys, austenitic steels, ferritic steels and ceramics for corrosion resistance in s<span class="chemf nowrap">CO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> cycles. The interest in these materials derive from their formation of protective surface oxide layers in the presence of carbon dioxide, however in most cases further evaluation of the reaction mechanics and corrosion/erosion kinetics and mechanisms is required, as none of the materials meet the necessary goals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Other">Other</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Supercritical_carbon_dioxide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Other">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Work is underway to develop a s<span class="chemf nowrap">CO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> <a href="/wiki/Closed-cycle_gas_turbine" title="Closed-cycle gas turbine">closed-cycle gas turbine</a> to operate at temperatures near 550 °C. This would have implications for bulk thermal and nuclear generation of electricity, because the supercritical properties of carbon dioxide at above 500 °C and 20 MPa enable thermal efficiencies approaching 45 percent. This could increase the electrical power produced per unit of fuel required by 40 percent or more. Given the volume of carbon fuels used in producing electricity, the environmental impact of cycle efficiency increases would be significant.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup>
</p><p>Supercritical <span class="chemf nowrap">CO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> is an emerging natural refrigerant, used in new, low carbon solutions for domestic <a href="/wiki/Heat_pump" title="Heat pump">heat pumps</a>. Supercritical <span class="chemf nowrap">CO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> heat pumps are commercially marketed in Asia. <a href="/wiki/EcoCute" title="EcoCute">EcoCute</a> systems from Japan, developed by Mayekawa, develop high temperature domestic water with small inputs of electric power by moving heat into the system from the surroundings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup>
</p><p>Supercritical <span class="chemf nowrap">CO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> has been used since the 1980s to enhance recovery in mature oil fields.
</p><p>"<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Clean_coal" title="Clean coal">Clean coal</a>" technologies are emerging that could combine such enhanced recovery methods with <a href="/wiki/Carbon_sequestration" title="Carbon sequestration">carbon sequestration</a>. Using <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gasifier" title="Gasifier">gasifiers</a> instead of conventional furnaces, coal and water is reduced to hydrogen gas, carbon dioxide and ash. This hydrogen gas can be used to produce electrical power In <a href="/wiki/Combined_cycle" title="Combined cycle">combined cycle</a> gas turbines, <span class="chemf nowrap">CO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> is captured, compressed to the supercritical state and injected into geological storage, possibly into existing oil fields to improve yields. The unique properties of s<span class="chemf nowrap">CO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> ensure that it remains out of the atmosphere.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup>
</p><p>Supercritical <span class="chemf nowrap">CO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> could be used as a working fluid in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Enhanced_Geothermal_Systems" title="Enhanced Geothermal Systems">enhanced geothermal systems</a>. Possible advantages compared to water include higher energy yield resulting from its lower viscosity, better chemical interaction, <span class="chemf nowrap">CO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> storage through fluid loss and higher temperature limit. As of 2011, the concept had not been tested in the field.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Aerogel_production">Aerogel production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Supercritical_carbon_dioxide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Aerogel production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Supercritical carbon dioxide is used in the production of silica, carbon and metal based <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aerogels" title="Aerogels">aerogels</a>. For example, silicon dioxide gel is formed and then exposed to s<span class="chemf nowrap">CO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span>. When the <span class="chemf nowrap">CO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> goes supercritical, all surface tension is removed, allowing the liquid to leave the aerogel and produce nanometer sized pores.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sterilization_of_biomedical_materials">Sterilization of biomedical materials</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Supercritical_carbon_dioxide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Sterilization of biomedical materials">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Supercritical <span class="chemf nowrap">CO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> is an alternative for terminal sterilization of biological materials and medical devices with combination of the additive <a href="/wiki/Peracetic_acid" title="Peracetic acid">peracetic acid</a> (PAA). Supercritical <span class="chemf nowrap">CO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> does not sterilize the media, because it does not kill the spores of microorganisms. Moreover, this process is gentle, as the morphology, ultrastructure and protein profiles of inactivated microbes are preserved.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cleaning">Cleaning</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Supercritical_carbon_dioxide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Cleaning">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Supercritical <span class="chemf nowrap">CO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> is used in certain industrial <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide_cleaning" title="Carbon dioxide cleaning">cleaning processes</a>.
</p>
