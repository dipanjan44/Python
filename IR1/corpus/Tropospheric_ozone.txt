<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Tropospheric ozone</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:TOR.Seasonal.Global.CLIM.jpeg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="540" data-file-width="700" height="170" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/2/2f/TOR.Seasonal.Global.CLIM.jpeg/220px-TOR.Seasonal.Global.CLIM.jpeg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/2/2f/TOR.Seasonal.Global.CLIM.jpeg/330px-TOR.Seasonal.Global.CLIM.jpeg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/2/2f/TOR.Seasonal.Global.CLIM.jpeg/440px-TOR.Seasonal.Global.CLIM.jpeg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:TOR.Seasonal.Global.CLIM.jpeg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Seasonal average vertical columns of tropospheric ozone in <a href="/wiki/Dobson_unit" title="Dobson unit">Dobson units</a> over the period 1979 to 2000. In June to August photochemical ozone production causes very high concentrations over the East Coast of the USA and China.</div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Ozone" title="Ozone">Ozone</a> (O<sub>3</sub>) is a constituent of the <a href="/wiki/Troposphere" title="Troposphere">troposphere</a> (it is also an important constituent of some regions of the <a href="/wiki/Stratosphere" title="Stratosphere">stratosphere</a> commonly known as the <a href="/wiki/Ozone_layer" title="Ozone layer">ozone layer</a>). The <a href="/wiki/Troposphere" title="Troposphere">troposphere</a> extends from the <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth</a>'s surface to between 12 and 20 kilometers above <a href="/wiki/Sea_level" title="Sea level">sea level</a> and consists of many layers. Ozone is more concentrated above the mixing layer, or ground layer. Ground-level ozone, though less concentrated than ozone aloft, is more of a problem because of its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropospheric_Ozone#Health_effects" title="Tropospheric Ozone">health effects</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>Photochemical and chemical reactions involving it drive many of the chemical processes that occur in the atmosphere by day and by night. At abnormally high concentrations brought about by human activities (largely incomplete combustion of fossil fuels, such as gasoline, diesel, etc.), it is a <a href="/wiki/Pollutant" title="Pollutant">pollutant</a>, and a constituent of <a href="/wiki/Smog" title="Smog">smog</a>. Many highly energetic reactions produce it, ranging from combustion to photocopying. Often laser printers will have a smell of ozone, which in high concentrations is toxic. Ozone is a powerful <a href="/wiki/Oxidizing_agent" title="Oxidizing agent">oxidizing agent</a> readily reacting with other chemical compounds to make many possibly <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Toxic" title="Toxic">toxic</a> oxides.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Tropospheric ozone is a <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gas" title="Greenhouse gas">greenhouse gas</a> and initiates the chemical removal of <a href="/wiki/Methane" title="Methane">methane</a> and other <a href="/wiki/Hydrocarbon" title="Hydrocarbon">hydrocarbons</a> from the atmosphere. Thus, its concentration affects how long these compounds remain in the air.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Measurement">Measurement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropospheric_ozone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Measurement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Satellite" title="Satellite">Satellites</a> are able to measure tropospheric ozone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Measurements specifically of ground-level ozone require <a href="/wiki/In_situ#Earth_and_atmospheric_sciences" title="In situ"><i>in situ</i></a> monitoring technology.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Formation">Formation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropospheric_ozone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Formation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The majority of tropospheric ozone formation occurs when <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nitrogen_oxides" title="Nitrogen oxides">nitrogen oxides</a> (NOx), <a href="/wiki/Carbon_monoxide" title="Carbon monoxide">carbon monoxide</a> (CO) and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Volatile_organic_compounds" title="Volatile organic compounds">volatile organic compounds</a> (VOCs), react in the atmosphere in the presence of sunlight. NOx, CO, and VOCs are called ozone precursors. Motor vehicle exhaust, industrial emissions, and chemical solvents are the major anthropogenic sources of these chemicals. Although these precursors often originate in urban areas, winds can carry NOx hundreds of kilometers, causing ozone formation to occur in less populated regions as well. Methane, a VOC whose atmospheric concentration has increased tremendously during the last century, contributes to ozone formation but on a global scale rather than in local or regional photochemical smog episodes. In situations where this exclusion of methane from the VOC group of substances is not obvious, the term Non-Methane VOC (NMVOC) is often used.
</p><p>The chemical reactions involved in tropospheric ozone formation are a series of complex cycles in which carbon monoxide and VOCs are oxidised to water vapour and carbon dioxide. The reactions involved in this process are illustrated here with CO but similar reactions occur for VOC as well. The oxidation begins with the reaction of CO with the <a href="/wiki/Hydroxyl_radical" title="Hydroxyl radical">hydroxyl radical</a> (<sup>•</sup>OH).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The radical intermediate formed by this reacts rapidly with oxygen to give a <a href="/wiki/Hydroperoxyl" title="Hydroperoxyl">peroxy radical</a> <span class="chemf nowrap">HO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span><sup>•</sup>
</p>
<dl><dd><dl><dd><sup>•</sup>OH + CO → <a href="/wiki/Hydrocarboxyl" title="Hydrocarboxyl"><sup>•</sup>HOCO</a></dd>
<dd><sup>•</sup>HOCO + <span class="chemf nowrap">O<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> → <span class="chemf nowrap">HO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span><sup>•</sup> + <span class="chemf nowrap">CO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span></dd></dl></dd></dl>
<p>Peroxy radicals then go on to react with NO to give <span class="chemf nowrap">NO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> which is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Photolysis" title="Photolysis">photolysed</a> to give atomic oxygen and through reaction with oxygen a molecule of ozone:
</p>
<dl><dd><dl><dd><span class="chemf nowrap">HO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span><sup>•</sup> + NO → <sup>•</sup>OH + <span class="chemf nowrap">NO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span></dd>
<dd><span class="chemf nowrap">NO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> + hν → NO + O(<sup>3</sup>P)</dd>
<dd>O(<sup>3</sup>P) + <span class="chemf nowrap">O<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> → <span class="chemf nowrap">O<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">3</sub></span></span></dd></dl></dd></dl>
<p>The balance of this sequence of chemical reactions is:
</p>
<dl><dd><dl><dd>CO + 2<span class="chemf nowrap">O<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> + hν → <span class="chemf nowrap">CO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> + <span class="chemf nowrap">O<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">3</sub></span></span></dd></dl></dd></dl>
<p>The amount of ozone produced through these reactions can be calculated using the <a href="/wiki/Leighton_relationship" title="Leighton relationship">Leighton relationship</a>.
</p><p>This cycle involving <span class="chemf nowrap">HO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">x</sub></span></span> and <span class="chemf nowrap">NO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">x</sub></span></span> is terminated by the reaction of OH with <span class="chemf nowrap">NO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> to form <a href="/wiki/Nitric_acid" title="Nitric acid">nitric acid</a> or by the reaction of peroxy radicals with each other to form <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Peroxides" title="Peroxides">peroxides</a>. The chemistry involving VOCs is much more complex but the same reaction of peroxy radicals oxidizing NO to <span class="chemf nowrap">NO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> is the critical step leading to ozone formation.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Health_effects">Health effects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropospheric_ozone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Health effects">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Ozone#Health_effects" title="Ozone">Ozone § Health_effects</a></div>
<p>Health effects depend on ozone precursors, which is a group of pollutants, primarily generated during the combustion of fossil fuels. Reaction with daylight ultraviolet (UV) rays and these precursors create ground-level ozone pollution (Tropospheric Ozone).
Ozone is known to have the following health effects at concentrations common in urban air:
</p>
<ul><li>Irritation of the <a href="/wiki/Respiratory_system" title="Respiratory system">respiratory system</a>, causing coughing, throat irritation, and/or an uncomfortable sensation in the chest.</li>
<li>Reduced <a href="/wiki/Lung" title="Lung">lung</a> function, making it more difficult to breathe deeply and vigorously. Breathing may become more rapid and more shallow than normal, and a person's ability to engage in vigorous activities may be limited.</li>
<li>Aggravation of <a href="/wiki/Asthma" title="Asthma">asthma</a>. When ozone levels are high, more people with asthma have attacks that require a doctor's attention or use of medication. One reason this happens is that ozone makes people more sensitive to <a href="/wiki/Allergen" title="Allergen">allergens</a>, which in turn trigger asthma attacks.</li>
<li>Increased susceptibility to <a href="/wiki/Upper_respiratory_tract_infection" title="Upper respiratory tract infection">respiratory infections</a>.</li>
<li>Inflammation and damage to the lining of the lungs. Within a few days, the damaged cells are shed and replaced much like the skin peels after a sunburn. Animal studies suggest that if this type of inflammation happens repeatedly over a long time period (months, years, a lifetime), lung tissue may become permanently scarred, resulting in permanent loss of lung function and a lower quality of life.</li></ul>
<p>A statistical study of 95 large urban communities in the United States found significant association between ozone levels and premature death. The study estimated that a one-third reduction in urban ozone concentrations would save roughly 4000 lives per year (Bell et al., 2004). Tropospheric Ozone causes approximately 22,000 premature deaths per year in 25 countries in the European Union. (WHO, 2008)
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Problem_areas">Problem areas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropospheric_ozone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Problem areas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<table class="plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div style="width:52px"><img alt="Accuracy dispute" data-file-height="620" data-file-width="620" height="40" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Ambox_contradict.svg/40px-Ambox_contradict.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Ambox_contradict.svg/60px-Ambox_contradict.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Ambox_contradict.svg/80px-Ambox_contradict.svg.png 2x" title="Accuracy dispute" width="40"/></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This article <b>appears to contradict the  article <a href="/wiki/Ozone" title="Ozone">Ozone</a></b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> Please see discussion on the linked <a href="/wiki/Talk:Tropospheric_ozone" title="Talk:Tropospheric ozone">talk page</a>.</span> <small><i>(July 2007)</i></small><small class="hide-when-compact"><i> (<a href="/wiki/Help:Maintenance_template_removal" title="Help:Maintenance template removal">Learn how and when to remove this template message</a>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:US-ozone-non-attainment-2007-06.png"><img alt="US-ozone-non-attainment-2007-06.png" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="514" data-file-width="713" height="216" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5f/US-ozone-non-attainment-2007-06.png/300px-US-ozone-non-attainment-2007-06.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5f/US-ozone-non-attainment-2007-06.png/450px-US-ozone-non-attainment-2007-06.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5f/US-ozone-non-attainment-2007-06.png/600px-US-ozone-non-attainment-2007-06.png 2x" width="300"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:US-ozone-non-attainment-2007-06.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div></div></div></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Environmental_Protection_Agency" title="United States Environmental Protection Agency">United States Environmental Protection Agency</a> has developed an Air Quality index to help explain air pollution levels to the general public. 8-hour average ozone <a href="/wiki/Mole_fraction" title="Mole fraction">mole fractions</a> of 76 to 95 nmol/mol are described as "Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups", 96 nmol/mol to 115 nmol/mol as "unhealthy" and 116 nmol/mol to 404 nmol/mol as "very unhealthy" <a class="external autonumber" href="http://www.airnow.gov/index.cfm?action=health2.smog1#4" rel="nofollow">[1]</a>.  The EPA has designated over 300 counties of the United States, clustered around the most heavily populated areas (especially in California and the Northeast), as failing to comply with the <a href="/wiki/National_Ambient_Air_Quality_Standards" title="National Ambient Air Quality Standards">National Ambient Air Quality Standards</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate_change">Climate change</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropospheric_ozone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Climate change">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Melting of <a href="/wiki/Arctic_sea_ice_decline" title="Arctic sea ice decline">sea ice</a> releases molecular <a href="/wiki/Chlorine" title="Chlorine">chlorine</a>, which reacts with UV radiation to produce chlorine radicals. Because chlorine radicals are highly reactive, they can expedite the degradation of <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_methane" title="Atmospheric methane">methane</a> and tropospheric ozone and the oxidation of <a href="/wiki/Mercury_(element)" title="Mercury (element)">mercury</a> to more toxic forms.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Ozone production rises during heat waves, because plants absorb less ozone. It is estimated that curtailed ozone absorption by plants is responsible for the loss of 460 lives in the UK in the hot summer of 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> A similar investigation to assess the joint effects of ozone and heat during the European heat waves in 2003, concluded that these appear to be additive.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>
