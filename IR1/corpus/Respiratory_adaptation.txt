<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Respiratory adaptation</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>

<p><b>Respiratory adaptation</b> is the specific changes that the <a href="/wiki/Respiratory_system" title="Respiratory system">respiratory system</a> undergoes in response to the demands of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Physical_exercise" title="Physical exercise">physical exertion</a>. Intense physical exertion, such as that involved in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fitness_training" title="Fitness training">fitness training</a>, places elevated demands on the respiratory system. Over time, this results in respiratory changes as the system adapts to these requirements.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-:1-1">[1]</a></sup> These changes ultimately result in an increased exchange of <a href="/wiki/Oxygen" title="Oxygen">oxygen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide" title="Carbon dioxide">carbon dioxide</a>, which is accompanied by an increase in <a href="/wiki/Metabolism" title="Metabolism">metabolism</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Respiratory adaptation is a physiological determinant of peak endurance performance, and in elite athletes, the pulmonary system is often a limiting factor to exercise under certain conditions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Neural_control">Neural control</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Respiratory_adaptation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Neural control">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Respiratory adaptation begins almost immediately after the initiation of the physical <a href="/wiki/Stress_(biology)" title="Stress (biology)">stress</a> associated with exercise. This triggers signals from the <a href="/wiki/Motor_cortex" title="Motor cortex">motor cortex</a> that stimulate the respiratory center of the <a href="/wiki/Brainstem" title="Brainstem">brain stem</a>, in conjunction with feedback from the <a href="/wiki/Proprioception" title="Proprioception">proprioreceptors</a> in the muscles and joints of the active limbs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Breathing_rate">Breathing rate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Respiratory_adaptation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Breathing rate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>With higher intensity training, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Breathing_rate" title="Breathing rate">breathing rate</a> is increased in order to allow more air to move in and out of the lungs, which enhances gas exchange. <a href="/wiki/Endurance_training" title="Endurance training">Endurance training</a> typically results in an increase in the respiration rate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lung_capacity">Lung capacity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Respiratory_adaptation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Lung capacity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>With adaptation, <a href="/wiki/Tidal_volume" title="Tidal volume">lung capacity</a> is increased over time, allowing a greater quantity of air to move in and out. Endurance training typically results in an increase in <a href="/wiki/Tidal_volume" title="Tidal volume">tidal volume</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Respiratory_muscles">Respiratory muscles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Respiratory_adaptation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Respiratory muscles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Muscles involved in respiration, including the <a href="/wiki/Thoracic_diaphragm" title="Thoracic diaphragm">diaphragm</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Intercostal_muscles" title="Intercostal muscles">intercostal muscles</a>, increase in strength and endurance. This results in an improved ability to breathe in more air, for longer amounts of time with less fatigue. <a href="/wiki/Aerobic_exercise" title="Aerobic exercise">Aerobic training</a> typically improves the endurance of respiratory muscles, whereas <a href="/wiki/Anaerobic_exercise" title="Anaerobic exercise">anaerobic training</a> tends to increase the size and strength of respiratory muscles. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-:1-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lung_capillaries">Lung capillaries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Respiratory_adaptation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Lung capillaries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Exercise increases the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vascularization" title="Vascularization">vascularization</a> of the lungs. This allows the more blood flow in and out of the lungs. This enhances the uptake of oxygen, since there is greater surface area for blood to bind with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Haemoglobin" title="Haemoglobin">haemoglobin</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-:1-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Alveoli">Alveoli</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Respiratory_adaptation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Alveoli">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Respiratory adaptation results an increase in the number of <a href="/wiki/Pulmonary_alveolus" title="Pulmonary alveolus">alveoli</a>, which enables more gas exchange to occur. This is coupled with an increase in alveolar oxygen tension.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
